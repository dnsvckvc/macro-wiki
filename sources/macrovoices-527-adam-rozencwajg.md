## Summary
Transcript of MacroVoices Episode 527 (recorded 8 April 2026, aired 9 April). [[AdamRozencwajg]] (co-founder of Goehring & Rozencwajg, natural resource fund managers since 1991) interviewed by Eric Townsend. The wiki's **fourth author** and **first physical-commodity specialist**. Rozencwajg provides *independent confirmation* of several load-bearing wiki claims from a fundamentals/geology perspective: (1) the physical dislocation is the biggest in modern oil market history but the price hasn't fully reflected it because the forward curve hasn't moved (spot ~$114, Dec26 only $75); (2) the **IEA surplus was wrong** — 2-3mmbpd surplus should have produced ~720mmbbl of inventory build in 2025, but inventories barely budged; (3) OPEC surged 2-2.5mmbpd last year and it was absorbed — the market was **balanced, not in surplus**; (4) the forward curve's next shoe to drop is when people realize inventories aren't rebuilding after the crisis; (5) **fertilizer/food is a classic asymmetry** — grain demand off the charts (protein consumption in developing markets = 7x grain requirement), yields razor-edge dependent on fertilizer; (6) uranium supply deficit is already here (Japanese stockpiles gone), long-term price ~$150/lb needed; (7) gold has speculative risk (GLD inflows were 4Q25/1Q26 momentum money) but the "insolvency trade" for gold comes after the current "debasement trade."

## Key Claims

### Oil — the biggest physical dislocation in history
- ~10-15mmbpd of impacted oil (conservative low 10mm, high 15mm, split ~12mm). "Every physical market expert agrees."
- US is shielded by 13mmbpd shale production + NGLs; Asia and Europe are feeling it most.
- **The price hasn't fully adjusted because the forward curve hasn't moved** — spot ~$114, but Dec26 WTI only ~$75. Stocks up 30-50% vs. spot's 2x move. The reason: the market thinks this resolves and prices return to ~$50-70.
- **The next shoe to drop:** after the proximal crisis, analysts will start tracking inventory rebuilds and realize they aren't happening → the back end of the curve reprices → oil equities finally respond.

### The IEA surplus was wrong — independent confirmation
- IEA/consensus said 2-3mmbpd surplus for 2025. At 2mmbpd, that should have produced ~720mmbbl of inventory build. **"Instead, we saw storage barely budge."**
- OPEC surged production by **2-2.5mmbpd** in 2025 (a gesture to help Trump post-tariffs — *"the day Trump announced the tariffs was the day OPEC said we're going to flood the world with oil"*). It was absorbed by strong demand.
- "The market was balanced. That's why inventories didn't grow."
- This independently confirms [[PauloMacro]]'s thesis in [[oil-has-turned-a-very-big-corner]] about IEA "missing barrels" and speculative positioning pricing in a surplus that didn't exist.

### SPR and inventories
- The 400mmbbl OECD SPR release will take reserves to "dangerously low strategic levels" (US already halved). Countries will want to rebuild → additional future demand not in the curve.
- Total global inventories will be 300-400mmbbl lower than January after weeks of 10mmbpd+ losses.
- Even if you believed the IEA's surplus, you could rebuild in ~100 days. But the surplus wasn't real, so rebuilding will be far harder.

### Fertilizer and food — classic asymmetry
- "Quite a bit of fertilizer passes through the Strait of Hormuz" and is being disrupted.
- Grain demand has been "off the charts" for 15 years due to rising incomes in developing markets → protein consumption → 7x the grain per calorie (sun → grain → animal → food chain).
- Yields have been record year after year (better fertilizer, better seeds, **longer growing season from warming**) — "razor's edge" between demand and yields.
- **"If anything happens to impact the yields, this market will be very tight in a hurry."** Fertilizer disruption could be the catalyst.
- This is "a classic asymmetry" and "convexity" — confirms [[PauloMacro]]'s [[Wheat]] thesis from [[make-1973-great-again]].

### Uranium
- Supply deficit is **already here** — Japanese post-Fukushima stockpiles are now gone. Market trading "heads up supply and demand."
- Long-term incentive price: **~$150/lb U3O8** needed to bring NextGen/Denison and the next tier of deposits into production.
- No demand destruction possible — fuel is minuscule in nuclear economics; regulated utilities will pass through any cost.
- NRC reforms under Trump: major permits (TerraPower), responsibility moved to Army Corps of Engineers. SMRs are a 2030+ story.

### Gold
- Central bank buying is "good buying" — price-agnostic, sticky, not hot money.
- Speculative GLD accumulation was 4Q25/1Q26 momentum money → risk of reversal if trend breaks.
- **Oil will outperform gold for "a couple of years"** (precedent: 1970s, early 2000s — energy leads in the middle part of commodity bull markets).
- Gold isn't done — "could probably double from here." But the *next* catalyst is the **"insolvency trade"** (government fiscal distress), not the current "debasement trade." That's 1-3 years out.

### Renewables
- "Not a very efficient form of energy conversion." Countries that pushed hard for renewables (Germany, England) are feeling the crisis most acutely. Countries that doubled down on oil and gas (US) are in a much better position.
- Potential return to coal in the Global South (India), driven by LNG disruption from Russia/Ukraine and now Iran.
