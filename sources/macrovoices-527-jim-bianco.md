## Summary
Second segment of MacroVoices Episode 527 (recorded 8 April, aired 9 April 2026). [[JimBianco]] (Bianco Research founder, macro/bond specialist) interviewed by Eric Townsend on the "ceasefire" status, military dynamics, and the Fed's dilemma. The wiki's **fifth author**. Bianco's key contribution: the **military-technical explanation for why the [[StraitOfHormuz]] cannot be forced open** — the US military excels at offense but lacks a defensive shield against decentralized drone/missile operations under Iran's Mosaic Doctrine. Even with a deal, pre-Feb-28 market levels are gone forever (permanent risk premiums). Without a deal, "crude oil goes up $3 a day" (quoting Javier Blas). The Fed is "all over the place" — two-sided between cut (war slows growth) and hike (war raises inflation). Bianco's framework: nominal GDP (real + inflation) → if inflation exceeds the real-growth drag, rates trend higher. He's in a "3-ish percent inflation world" — 5% fair-value rates, 7% mortgages as the new normal.

## Key Claims

### The ceasefire doesn't exist in practice
- Day 1 after the announcement: US and Iran "not even close to understanding" what the deal means. Farsi and English term sheets differ on uranium enrichment.
- Drones and missiles from Iran are at their **highest level of the month** on day 1 of the "ceasefire." No ships moving. Israelis still attacking Lebanon.
- **What matters for markets = the Strait, not the war.** "If we had Hormuz open and were still dealing with what we've been dealing with in Iran, the markets would look past it."

### Military: why the Strait can't be forced open
- The US is "very good at offense" (attacking, blowing things up) but **lacks a defensive shield** against decentralized, small-scale drone/missile operations.
- **Iran's Mosaic Doctrine:** if the IRGC command structure is knocked out, individual sector commanders have **standing orders to keep firing**. ("The courier pigeons haven't gotten to the sector commanders.") Hundreds/thousands of drones and launchers spread across mountainous terrain; can be fired from the back of a pickup truck; impossible to offensively eliminate them all.
- **39 days of bombing haven't stopped the attacks.** "I don't see a scenario where aggressively, offensively attacking is going to be enough to get them to back off."
- **Ukraine's drone revolution as template:** Ukraine killing 30–35K Russians/month in 2026. 90% of casualties from drones. *"First war since the 19th century where the primary method of killing is not a bullet or an artillery shell."* Operation Hedgehog (NATO exercise): 10 Ukrainian drone operators wiped out 2,000 NATO soldiers in simulation. General Petraeus: "redefining the way war is fought."
- **Ukraine has signed 10-year security agreements with Gulf states** to bring interceptor drone technology as a defensive shield for the Strait — but scaling to 600 miles will take a long time.

### Even a deal doesn't fix it
- Pre-February-28 market levels are **permanently gone**. Higher risk premiums in interest rates, equity vol, and crude prices from now on.
- *"These issues, now that they've been uncorked, can be uncorked again and flare up again at any point."*

### Without a deal: "$3 a day"
- Quoting Javier Blas (Bloomberg): oil price rises on average **$3/day** while ships are stuck. Back to pre-ceasefire prices and keep climbing.

### The Fed's dilemma — nominal GDP framework
- Fed minutes show a "two-sided argument": some members want to cut (war slows real growth), others want to hike (war increases inflation).
- *"Gone are the days where we look at the chairman's words. Chairman is now one of 12 voters."*
- Bianco's framework: **nominal GDP = real + inflation.** If inflation rise > real-growth drag → nominal up → rates higher. He's in this camp for now, but a prolonged war could flip it.
- [[KevinWarsh]] hearings scheduled for Monday 13 April. "We haven't heard from him in several months."

### Inflation outlook: "3-ish percent world"
- Five years without hitting the Fed's 2% target.
- March CPI consensus: **0.9% m/m** (one of the highest ever), driven by $1+ gasoline price rise → headline year-over-year above 3%.
- Even if gasoline deflates, reversal takes months.
- Fair-value rates closer to 5%. 7% mortgages as the new normal.
- *"We are in a period of deglobalization… whether free commerce on the open seas is going to be free commerce, or whether everybody's going to have to pay a toll."*
