## Summary
[[MichaelHowell]]'s 6 April 2026 note arguing that the Iran conflict is less damaging to the real economy than feared, but **potentially highly destructive to [[GlobalLiquidityCycle|Global Liquidity]]** — a deterioration that began *before* the conflict and will persist long after. Howell's AI-based world GDP nowcast shows only a 0.5–0.75pp hit so far (vs. ~1pp from the 2025 tariff tantrum, ~3pp from COVID). But the combined shock from oil (+$30–40/bbl), MOVE (+20 pts), and DXY (+5%) delivers a **~20% hit to Global Liquidity** if sustained. Bond markets are already signaling via falling term premia and a flattening yield curve that the demand for safety is rising. Howell's "strongest market conviction for 2026" — yield curves flatten by mid-year — is on track. His asset-allocation cycle says we are in the *Speculation* phase, with *Turbulence* (definitively Risk Off) next in sequence. *"Markets move in cycles, not themes. Forget the latest investment fad and seriously consider where we are in the cycle."*

## Key Claims

### Iran's real-economy impact — less than feared
- World GDP has slipped only **0.5–0.75pp** (daily AI-based nowcast using commodity prices, credit spreads, trade-sensitive FX).
- Smaller than the ~1pp hit from the 2025 tariff tantrum and far smaller than the ~3pp from COVID.
- Risk appetite has fallen but remains elevated; stock markets "bruised but still standing."

### Iran's liquidity impact — far worse
- The liquidity deterioration **began before the conflict** (the [[GlobalLiquidityCycle]] peaked Sep/Oct 2025) and "will persist long afterwards."
- The combined shock: oil +$30–40/bbl, MOVE +20 index points, DXY +5% = **~20% hit to Global Liquidity** if sustained.
- Latest weekly data: the biggest hit to combined (aggregate + market) liquidity in almost three years.
- Projected model: "The Iran conflict did not trigger this deterioration, but it will plainly make it worse." The projection "aint pretty."

### Bond markets are the truth
- **Falling term premia** across most major markets (except Japan/China) confirm rising demand for safety — investors want long-duration govvies.
- "Forget the nonsense spouted by the media that bonds are the big casualties: flattening yield curves tell us that something else is going on."
- Bond investors expect policy rates to jump ~50bp on average, but the long end is being pulled *down* by collapsing term premia. This is the signature of a **bear flattening** transitioning to a **bull flattening** = Turbulence.
- The Global Liquidity → term premia relationship has a "long track record" and currently confirms the deterioration.

### Yield curve phase taxonomy
Howell maps four phases of the yield curve to the liquidity cycle:
1. **Expansion:** bull steepening (long rates fall, short rates follow) — Risk On, early cycle.
2. **Speculation:** bear flattening (short rates rise faster than long) — current phase. Positive economic surprises still possible.
3. **Turbulence:** bull flattening (long rates fall faster than short) — definitively Risk Off. Next in sequence.
4. **Retreat:** bear steepening (long rates rise faster than short) — late recession.

### Asset allocation
- In *Speculation*: still some equity upside but increasingly fragile.
- In *Turbulence* (approaching): shift to government bonds, gold, defensives. Equities and credit underperform.
- Early warning signal: SOFR rate above the 2Y note yield = "tight" liquidity conditions.

## Contradictions
- **Interesting nuance on bonds (not a contradiction):** Paulo in [[make-1973-great-again]] argues long-end bonds are "an accelerant not a stabilizer" because deficit/GDP blows out. Howell here argues the long end will be *bid* because falling term premia = demand for safety, and the yield curve will *flatten*. Both can be right at different horizons: Howell's term-premia compression is the near-term flight-to-quality dynamic; Paulo's fiscal-acceleration is the longer-term structural risk once the recession is priced in and deficits explode. Muir's positioning (2Y/5Y overweight, long-end as a "contrarian punt") may thread the needle.
- **Emphasis nuance:** Howell says "the liquidity cycle is the culprit, not Iran." Paulo says the Iran Nash equilibrium is the irreducible cause. Both arrive at "Risk Off" — the difference is analytical framing, not conclusion. Howell would say the cycle was already turning and Iran accelerated it; Paulo would say Iran is the physical-world reality that the cycle can't overcome. The wiki's data supports both: the cycle peaked before the war (Q3 2025 per Howell, October NQ100 ATH per Paulo), and the war compounds it.
