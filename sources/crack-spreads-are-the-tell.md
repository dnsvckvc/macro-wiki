## Summary
[[PauloMacro]] (aka Cloudbear) argues that refinery [[CrackSpreads]] are flashing the same warning they flashed in April 2020 — but in reverse. The 2020 setup was a >10mmbpd demand shock that forced [[WTI]] negative; today's setup is a >10mmbpd+ crude *supply* shock on the back of Mid-East conflict. Cracks peaked near $60/bbl on 20 March 2026 and have been falling for two weeks — mirroring the April 2020 inflection where cracks rose just before crude imploded. By [[CharlieMunger]]'s "invert, always invert" logic, the equivalent move this cycle is not a crude crash but a crude *moon*: falling cracks signal demand destruction at the pump feeding back up the chain, forcing any available upstream product (any grade, any owner, including SPR) to market. He also warns that long [[RefineryStocks]] today is the same seductive "outsized returns" logic that got investors (himself included) stuck long tankers in the April 2020 supercontango.

## Key Claims
- Refinery [[CrackSpreads]] are a leading "second derivative" indicator for crude oil: they inflected higher in early April 2020, days before WTI went negative on 20 April 2020.
- The current episode is structurally the April 2020 move **inverted**: same >10mmbpd dislocation, but supply shock instead of demand shock.
- Cracks peaked near $60/bbl on 20 March 2026 and have been falling for ~two weeks, putting the market in the mirror-image window of early April 2020.
- Falling cracks in this regime signal demand destruction at the tip of the spear (the pump) feeding back up the chain to the wellhead — forcing all available product to market rather than being shut in.
- Implication: crude does not crash, it *moons*. And this dislocation is **bigger and more persistent than Covid**.
- Long [[RefineryStocks]] today rhymes with long tankers in the April 2020 [[Supercontango]] — a tempting trade whose payoff is about to reverse.
- The market is broadly missing the signal because we are in a "new era of speculation" where cross-asset chains like this are ignored.
