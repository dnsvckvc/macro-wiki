## Summary
[[PauloMacro]]'s 6 March 2026 morning note — one day after his foundational [[tom-hanks-has-covid]] essay. Oil is still "absurdly underpriced" for what is happening: a Brent 2m–3m backwardation regression suggests Brent should be $130 not $90 (red dot on the regression chart). Physical-market signals (Dated Brent over front-month at Russia-invasion levels, Dubai premiums to Brent at $10, 1yr backward yield at 20%) are screaming while the flat price lags because of (1) Trump jawboning/SPR/Bessent PPT rumors, (2) producer hedging (from the Tom Hanks appendix), and (3) the "Nothing Ever Happens" delusion. Qatar's energy minister Saad Al-Kaabi warns it would take "weeks to months" to normalize even if the war ended today and says the disruption could "bring down the economies of the world." NFP came in as a big miss; the Fed is "totally lost on what to do about AI and oil." Bond market tried to rally on the weak jobs data and got "smoked" — a news-failure pattern. Regime: **USD Up, Bonds Down, Equities Down.**

## Key Claims
- Oil is absurdly underpriced: Brent backwardation regression → $130 fair value vs. $90 actual. Jet fuel at $180/bbl equivalent.
- Qatar energy minister Saad Al-Kaabi: "even if the war ended today, it would take Qatar weeks to months to get delivery schedules back to normal" and this could "bring down the economies of the world."
- LNG carriers cannot risk a containment-tank breach in a 30km-wide strait with a navigable channel of only a few km. US escorts are "unfeasible in providing absolute security."
- NFP was a "nasty big miss" — confirming labor-market weakness pre-war and supporting the [[CreditCycle]] thesis.
- The bond market tried to rally on weak jobs data and then got smoked — a **news failure** (tried to do the expected thing and reversed). This is the same pattern Paulo later catalogs as the signature of approaching crash risk.
- **USD Up, Bonds Down, Equities Down** — the trifecta signaling the market cannot find a safe haven.
- *"You can't put the genie back in the bottle here. Every day this goes on it's certainly worse, but it's already enough to have very long lasting ripples."*
