## Summary
[[PauloMacro]]'s 10 March 2026 "Loose Thoughts" essay — five interconnected arguments explaining why the Iran war is structurally different from every prior "TACO" (Trump Always Chickens Out) episode. Key themes: (1) The latest TACO only produced +83bps (vs. the original Liberation Day TACO's +10% on a -20% decline) — the half-life is collapsing because **commodities are not a cartoon** and Trump has wandered into a market he cannot control; (2) There is **no Nash equilibrium for a deal** — *this ends when Iran says it ends*; (3) Clarifies the [[TomHanksHasCovidMoment]] distinction between energy (already happened: China diesel/gasoline export ban) and equities (hasn't happened yet but is about to — most likely via **hyperscalers using the oil crisis as cover to cut capex**, with Oracle reporting 10 March as the potential trigger); (4) Quantifies the Hormuz closure as **20% of global supply** (20mmbpd out of 100mmbpd) — 2–3x worse than any prior oil crisis; (5) Argues Trump's jawboning to keep oil below $100 (SPR releases, fuel tax holidays, Bessent trading desk threats) is *prolonging* the crisis by delaying demand destruction, which means the floor in crude goes higher and farther out in time.

## Key Claims

### #1 — Commodities are not a cartoon
- Trump has lived in a financial cartoon his whole life (junk credit, real estate, equities) where narratives and flow dominate. But **commodity price discovery converges into the real world.**
- Equities and credit can ignore reality until a bankruptcy or force majeure. Commodities cannot. The "forcing function in commodities is the price-insensitive buyer who needs the physical when it is no longer available."
- Trump has made the crude futures market "untradable" in the words of one commodity friend — $40/bbl daily range, likely blowing up every producer's hedge book.
- "Trump is Making Macro Great Again."

### #2 — No [[NashEquilibrium]] for a deal
- *"This ends when Iran says it ends — not when Trump decides to declare victory, packs up, and leaves a bunch of Gulf states wondering what their security blanket was good for."*
- "It's Iran's neighborhood… we are just visiting."
- The consensus around Trump's control of the market is at an all-time high → prime setup for the consensus to be wrong.

### #3 — Distinct Tom Hanks moments for energy vs. equities
- Clarifies from [[tom-hanks-has-covid]]: the China diesel/gasoline export ban was the Tom Hanks moment for *crude*, not equities. "The Nasdaq will never care about a Chinese products export ban. They really should, but they won't… until they get their own Tom Hanks Moment."
- Paulo's candidate for the equities Tom Hanks moment: **hyperscalers using the oil crisis as cover to cut AI capex.**
  - The "Block, Shumer, & Citrini Effect": companies are already using AI as cover to cut headcount they should have cut years ago. Same logic extends: what if hyperscalers start using the oil crisis to delay data center builds without admitting the capex bubble is popping?
  - *"Those power hungry data centers need to be… delayed… for just a little while… you know, because Trump bombed Iran — totally unrelated to your giant capital misallocation."*
  - Oracle — "the posterchild for a credit contagion downgrade from IG to HY" — reports Tuesday 10 March after the close.

### #4 — Scale of the Hormuz closure
- **20mmbpd / 100mmbpd = 20% of global supply** — dwarfs every prior oil crisis:
  - Iranian Revolution 1978: 5.5M/60M = 9.2%
  - Yom Kippur 1973: 4.5M/56M = 8%
  - Iraq-Kuwait 1990: 4.3M/61M = 7%
  - Iran-Iraq 1980: 4M/60M = 6.7%
  - Russia-Ukraine 2022: 2M/95M = 2.1%

### #5 — Trump's jawboning is *prolonging* the crisis
- By trying to suppress oil below $100 (SPR releases, fuel tax holiday trial balloons, threats of a Bessent trading desk selling futures), Trump delays demand destruction.
- Demand destruction is the only mechanism that resolves a supply shock. Delay it → floor in crude goes higher and farther out in time → "consumers will boil like the frog in the pot."
- The back of the oil curve is already too low because of [[ProducerHedging]] (from the [[tom-hanks-has-covid]] appendix), and the crisis will percolate and extend.
- "The oil futures curve is not predicting prices in the future — it never does… The truth is in the *change* in the curve — the second derivative."
- What Trump has accomplished: $5/gallon crisis becomes a $4/gallon crisis politically because he made $3 gasoline the benchmark. He is "shooting bullets prematurely."
- Rest of world (Taiwan, Korea, Japan, India, Pakistan, Europe) hurt more initially → explains the Reverse Broadening pattern (RoW hit, USD bid) and why the rotation from [[MAG7]] to cyclicals is whiplashing.
