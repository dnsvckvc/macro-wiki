## Key Claims

### Transit reality as of 16 April
- **7 ships today, high of 16 on April 12** vs. pre-crisis 120-140 daily (70 each direction).
- Headline "20 ships through" is misleading — many are tiny vessels (50,000 bbl) rather than VLCCs (2mmbbl).
- **Zero non-Iranian VLCCs transited since Saturday's 3-VLCC one-off** — the only movement that would actually matter volumetrically.
- Iran is **actively choosing who gets through** — politically filtering transit, not just "the Strait is open or closed."

### The US blockade of Iran — the policy pivot
- For the first 5-6 weeks, the US *allowed* Iranian oil flow. Even **temporarily rolled back sanctions on Iranian floating storage** to let every barrel into the market. Same philosophy as the 2022 Russia **price cap** approach: violator's oil still flows because the world needs every barrel.
- **Trump admin has now shifted to "maximum economic pressure":** the sanctions waiver is not being renewed; US Navy blockade imposing new restrictions.
- If it holds: Iran's 2mmbpd effectively comes off the market → shut-in Gulf production goes from **13mmbpd to 15mmbpd.**
- Strategic rationale: until now Iran had captured windfall export profits during the war. The blockade at least shares the pain. But Rory: "this accelerates pressure on Tehran *and* brings more oil off the market at the worst possible time. Battle of patience."
- Moving-target blockade: formally "any ships planning to service Iranian ports," but today (16 April) two empty VLCCs traversed into Iran.

### Iran's new "deal" is back to square one
- Iran's proposal: ships may exit on Oman side of the Strait — **if** the US accepts original terms (uranium enrichment, sanctions relief, etc.).
- Red flags in the proposal:
  - Doesn't specify all ships or just non-US/Israel-aligned ships.
  - No offer to help identify or remove mines.
  - Headline is "exit" only — may just be a humanitarian acquiescence to get the **20,000 seafarers trapped in the Gulf** home, not a bidirectional transit deal.
- Market read of "deal on the horizon" is an over-interpretation.

### The Bab el-Mandeb / Houthi threat
- Iran has threatened to use Houthi proxies to close the **Strait of Bab el-Mandeb** (Red Sea) if no deal.
- Rory: the Red Sea now handles **~4.5-5 million barrels/day** of Saudi East-West-pipeline rerouted flow — plus historical trade via Suez.
- Houthis have proven capacity (prior closures).
- Cape of Good Hope detour = **+2 weeks delivery time.** "We simply do not have the time. This fundamentally remains a crisis of lost time."
- Not getting press because it's been a lingering threat — but it is especially consequential now given the East-West pipeline rerouting.

### The air pocket — region-by-region timing
The delivery-time lag means the "air pocket" of lost Middle East outflow hits different regions at different weeks:
- **East Africa:** week 3 (mid-March)
- **East Asia:** week 4 (late March)
- **Europe:** week 5 (early April)
- **North America:** week 6 (mid-April — *now*)
- "Each market has tightened up almost immediately once that air pocket hit."

Concrete example: US diesel tanker heading to Europe broke course and turned back toward **East Africa** when the air pocket landed there (East Africa is a major importer of Gulf distillates). That's how the shock transmits via bidding.

### US Gulf Coast as global swing supplier
- **China has blocked exports** of refined products (historically a major swing supplier in crises).
- US Gulf Coast is now the only functional global swing supplier.
- Tankers are inbound to the US *because other regions will pay more than Americans are willing to pay*. This is how the shock transmits.
- Trump sees inbound tankers as "North American energy security" — true, but misleading about the price mechanism.
- **Mid-continent US** (Oklahoma, Texas, etc.) is "arguably the most energy-secure region on the planet" — domestic refining + captive Canadian crude via pipelines + limited trade exposure. Coastal US will feel the shock.

### Unprecedented physical/futures spread
- **Brent futures** (June delivery): ~$100/bbl on Monday.
- **Dated Brent** (physical, 10-30 days ahead): **~$132/bbl.**
- **Spread: ~$32.** "Never exploded to that level before. Even in COVID, maximum was a couple of dollars."
- Add physical delivered premia of **~$20/bbl** on top → **~$150/bbl physical.**
- **HSBC CEO publicly quoted:** highest delivered barrel seen in the crisis so far = **$286/bbl for a cargo delivered to Sri Lanka.**
- "If Hormuz remains closed for another month without material upgrade across the curve, I would be truly, truly shocked." The arb will eventually force benchmarks up.

### If Bab el-Mandeb also closes
- **Pattern:** panic spike → fade (market sees "resolution" narrative) → gradual organic rise as the delayed barrels don't arrive.
- Ends up worse than just Hormuz alone; "even faster pace" of price rise in the medium term.
