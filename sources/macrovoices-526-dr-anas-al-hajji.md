## Summary
Second segment of MacroVoices Episode 526 (recorded 2 April 2026, immediately after Trump's Wednesday-night address). [[DrAnasAlHajji]] (Energy Outlook Advisors founder, Middle East energy expert) interviewed by Eric Townsend. The wiki's **seventh author** and first dedicated geopolitical-energy-intelligence specialist. Al-Hajji provides the most operationally detailed analysis of the conflict in the wiki, including: (1) the provocative question of *who really closed the Strait* (US via insurance companies vs. Iran — and why the insurance thesis is more internally consistent with Trump's own claims); (2) the IRGC takeover of the Iranian government and the independent-action Mosaic dynamic after leadership was killed; (3) after all mitigation measures (pipelines, SPR, sanctions relief), the world is still **short 10-12 million bpd** with a residual shortage of ~8mmbpd after demand destruction; (4) demand destruction kicks in at **Brent ~$160** per his model; (5) global recession (probably stagflation) by May if the war drags on; (6) the real story is **price differentials, not price levels** (Asia paying $170+ while US at ~$90); (7) post-crisis, every country will link energy to national security, making offshore wind/nuclear/storage a security play not an economic one — *"Trump unwittingly gave the climate change people the biggest gift of their lives."*

## Key Claims

### Who really closed the Strait?
- Two competing explanations: (a) Iran closed it or (b) the US closed it via insurance companies.
- Al-Hajji argues (b) is more internally consistent: Trump himself said Iran's navy is gone, no radars, total devastation. 28 countries' navies are present. How can a devastated Iran control the Strait against 28 navies? *"To believe Iran closed the Strait is a bigger conspiracy theory."*
- The insurance mechanism: even with navy escorts, **ships won't move without insurance.** If insurance is non-existent or extremely expensive, no shipping company will transit regardless of military escorts. *"It is an insurance story any way you look at it."*
- The National Security Strategy (November 2025 — four months before the war) said "the Strait of Hormuz remained open." Al-Hajji: *"It never been closed. This statement makes sense only if someone was planning to close it."* The same document explicitly ties Hormuz to Israel's security — unusual.
- **China knew in advance:** stopped importing US LNG and oil, built massive inventories, was "ahead on Venezuela." Al-Hajji wrote in June/July that China "got the message" — from stories in major media outlets with identical talking points — that the Strait would be closed, and that the US would close it.

### The IRGC takeover
- After the killing of leadership including Ayatollah Khamenei, the Revolutionary Guards took over the government completely. The official government (president, foreign minister, parliament) has "no actual power on the ground."
- For a period after the leadership killings, IRGC sector commanders operated independently — "every group, you are on your own." This created scattered attacks on targets that make no strategic sense (a waste refinery in the UAE, oil depots in Salalah Oman, a stationary Kuwaiti tanker 200 miles from Hormuz).
- *"There are too many questions that have no answers."*

### No red lines — historic
- No side has red lines. The desalination threat is the "nuclear option" — Iran will target desalination facilities of Israel and Gulf states if their civilian power infrastructure is hit. Trump signaled intention to target all of Iran's civilian power plants "probably simultaneously." This is exactly the trigger for Iran's threatened retaliation.
- Iran has listed the UAE's 4 Baraka nuclear reactors as targets. Israel/US have already attacked Iran's Bushar nuclear plant (though not the reactor itself).
- Panic among Gulf populations is itself destabilizing — "millions of people taking the highways."

### The shortage math
- After all mitigation (Saudi/UAE/Iraqi/Iranian pipelines + SPR releases): **still short 10-12 million bpd**.
- Demand destruction/decline eats 2-4 million → residual shortage of **~8 million bpd**.
- His model: **demand destruction at Brent ~$160.** Major global recession (probably stagflation) by May.
- *"This is the largest global crisis we have in our lifetime."* Not comparable to 1973, 1979, 1990, 2003, or 2022.
- Trump has "exhausted all his options" — SPR used, Russian/Iranian exemptions already in the market, Jones Act workaround already priced in. Only manipulation statements left, and "crying wolf" will stop working.

### Price differentials, not price levels
- The real story is the **differential** between medium-sour crude in Asia ($170+) and WTI in the US ($90).
- SPR releases impact differentials, not the overall level — the crude goes to US refiners, not Asia.
- The US now has the **lowest energy prices among all competitors** — exactly what the National Security Strategy called for. *"Whether this is a conspiracy theory or not, forget about it. This is reality right now."*
- SPR crude takes 4-6 weeks to reach Asia. "Will the war end by that time?"

### Post-crisis: energy = national security
- Every country will link energy sources to national security — economics and finance "are out of it."
- China was first: massive solar/wind/EV investment was about national security, not climate.
- Europe "got the message" when Trump weaponized LNG (Greenland threat) → now prioritizing offshore wind, nuclear, battery storage as security assets.
- *"Trump unwittingly gave the climate change people the biggest gift of their lives."*

## Contradictions
- **The insurance-closure thesis introduces a genuinely new perspective** that no other wiki author presents. Paulo, Bianco, and the others assume Iran controls the Strait via its Mosaic Doctrine and drone/missile capabilities. Al-Hajji argues it may be the *US* (via insurance companies) that is keeping the Strait closed, because this serves the US national security strategy of having the lowest energy prices among competitors. Both views share the same practical conclusion (the Strait stays closed, oil stays constrained, prices stay high), but the *agency* differs. This is the wiki's first genuine alternative explanation for the *mechanism* of closure, though not for the *outcome*.
- **Not a contradiction with the wiki thesis** — it's actually a *deeper* explanation that subsumes the surface narrative. Whether Iran keeps firing drones because of Nash, or whether the US keeps insurance prohibitive because of strategy, the result is identical: ships don't move, the physical crisis persists, and the financial cascade follows.
