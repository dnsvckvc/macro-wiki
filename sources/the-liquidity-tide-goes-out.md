## Summary
A summary of [[MichaelHowell]]'s 29 March 2026 X Spaces appearance hosted by [[GeorgeNoble]] (ex-Fidelity Overseas, colleague of Peter Lynch). Howell — the "Liquidity King" and founder of CrossBorder Capital / GLIndexes — reiterates his core thesis: the **~65-month (5.4-year) [[GlobalLiquidityCycle]] has peaked** in Q4 2025 / early 2026 and momentum is now fading. Absolute global liquidity remains high (~$188T) but *growth* is slowing. This is the wiki's **first source from a third author** (neither Paulo Macro nor Kevin Muir), providing the macro-level liquidity framework that sits above the US-plumbing-level mechanics Paulo tracks.

## Key Claims

### The cycle has peaked
- The global liquidity cycle likely peaked **Q4 2025 or early 2026**. Momentum is inflecting lower.
- This aligns with Howell's historical ~65-month sine-wave pattern for advanced economies, where liquidity growth slows after a multi-year upswing from late 2022.
- Short-term boosts (PBOC injections, Fed/Treasury effects) provide support, but rising **bond volatility** and a **stronger USD** are checking momentum.

### Level vs. momentum
- Absolute liquidity remains high (~$188T) — the level supports the economy. But what drives risk-asset rallies is the *rate of change*, and that is now negative.
- This distinction is critical: you can have a growing real economy *and* declining risk-asset prices simultaneously if liquidity growth is slowing but the level remains sufficient for real activity.

### Shift to the real economy
- In advanced economies, liquidity is becoming **negatively correlated with business activity**. As the real economy accelerates (GDP growth, defense spending, capex), money flows toward production and real-world needs rather than financial assets.
- This is the macro-level expression of [[PauloMacro]]'s "Can't Print Molecules" thesis: the physical economy is absorbing the money that used to inflate financial assets.

### Central bank divergence
- The Fed has ended QT and may ease further.
- But the **ECB, BoE, and BoJ are expected to continue balance-sheet shrinkage**, draining liquidity globally.
- China's easing provides some offset, but overall conditions are less abundant than in prior years.
- Fiscal policy (government spending/deficits) is becoming a more important liquidity source than monetary policy.

### Debt refinancing pressures
- Large global debt rollovers (**trillions due 2026–2028**) absorb liquidity as governments and corporations refinance at higher costs or in volatile conditions.
- This is consistent with Paulo's [[BasisTrade]] / [[SwapSpreads]] observations about dealer balance-sheet stress.

### Commodity and deflation risks
- Fertilizer/potash shortages driving potential food inflation (consistent with Paulo's [[Wheat]] thesis).
- Demand destruction in emerging markets may lead to global deflation risk.
- Oil/gold ratio flagged.
- Ethanol policy (E15 fuel) impacting sugar supply.

### Implications
- **Risk assets face headwinds** — a liquidity downswing historically leads to lower multiples, corrections, and deleveraging, even if the real economy grows.
- **Defensive rotation** toward staples, pharma, longer-duration bonds.
- **Bitcoin/crypto** highly sensitive — leveraged crypto hit hard in liquidity stress. Recent sell-offs during tight episodes.
- Potential for counter-trend moves but not broad rallies.
