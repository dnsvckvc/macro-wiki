## Key Claims

### The empirical setup

- **JGB 10Y** quietly broke out to a new closing high last night.
- **USD/JPY** has been range-bound 158–160 for nearly two months, with implied vol at near-4-year lows.
- **USD/JPY ↔ US 10Y** historically co-moved tightly (higher US yields = stronger dollar / weaker yen). The divergence began around **Liberation Day (~9 April 2026)** — visible in the chart Paulo annotates with arrows.

### The gold-vs-real-yields template

The structural analogy:
- **2007 → Feb 2022:** gold and US 5Y TIPS yield (real yields) traded in tight inverse lockstep.
- **24 Feb 2022 onward:** the West froze Russia's reserves; gold *diverged sharply higher* despite real yields ripping up to multi-year highs.
- **2022–2025:** the regression broke entirely — Fed tightening should have crushed gold to ~$1,250 (where gold bulls were waiting); gold *doubled+* instead because the [[LedgerToRealWorldThesis]] had taken over as structural driver.

This is the wiki's **canonical regression-break case study**, formalized by [[KevinMuir]] in [[geopolitics-doesnt-change-prices-in-the-long-run]] (12 March 2026). Muir at the time *pre-registered* the hypothesis for what an Iran-war equity regression break would look like (slow-motion, years not days).

### What a carry unwind would look like in this regime

The conventional risk-off pattern for yen carry:
1. Risk-off shock → equities sell off.
2. Yen rallies (carry unwind, repatriation flows).
3. USTs rally (flight to quality).
4. USD/JPY falls; UST yields fall together.

Paulo's "Upside Down" pattern:
1. Risk-off shock → equities sell off.
2. Yen rallies (carry unwind).
3. **USTs sell off** (no flight-to-quality bid, foreign holders stop buying or actively sell).
4. USD/JPY falls *and* UST yields rise — the regression break manifests.

> *"What happens if we get a carry unwind in USD/JPY, and US bonds… sell off?"*

This is consistent with the [[BasisTrade]] / Cayman Islands hot-money frame from [[LukeGromen]] / [[macrovoices-528-luke-gromen]] (37% of net UST issuance over the past 4 years to Cayman hedge funds running the basis trade). Hot money sells Treasuries when vol rises — making USTs *risk-on and risk-off simultaneously*.

### The "Upside Down" framing

Paulo invokes a Stranger Things reference: *"In the Upside Down of our markets today, I am openminded to any and all craziness. I mean, when gold trades like a risk asset…"* This is a complement to his prior framings ([[NorthStar]], [[TomHanksHasCovidMoment]], [[TurduckenOfMarketRisks]]) — the post-Liberation-Day regime where conventional cross-asset relationships are inverted, and prior-trustworthy correlations should be assumed broken until re-confirmed.

This is the **second independent application** of Muir's pre-registered transformative-event-regression-break hypothesis:
- **First application** (Muir, 12 March 2026): potential equity regression break from Iran war.
