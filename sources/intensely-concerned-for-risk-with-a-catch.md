## Summary
[[PauloMacro]]'s 7 February 2026 equity bear thesis — written **three weeks before the Iran/US/Israel war**. Paulo is "intensely concerned that a proper bear market has finally begun after a lengthy topping process." He documents: (1) SaaS/software names crashing and barely bouncing — the canary; (2) a **reflexive contagion boomerang** between software equities and private credit (25% of BDCs are tech-exposed; software is 15–20% of private credit; Apollo cut its PC software exposure almost by half in 2025); (3) hyperscaler bonds barely rallying despite equity bounces (Oracle CDS ticking higher); (4) retail as the dominant market driver (options volumes exceeding Jan-2021 GME/SPAC mania; Vanda showing retail determining marginal SPX prices) but with a 100% historical hit rate of eventually leaving it on paper; (5) Crowded L/S factor having its worst day since 27 Jan 2021 — the day the retail/SPAC/Cathie Wood mania peaked; (6) index all-time highs staggering (NQ100 Oct 29 → R2K Jan 22 → SPX Jan 28 → Dow Feb 6); (7) **"we have not seen The Fear"** — complacency with initial liquidation but no capitulation. "The Catch": [[BroadeningOut]] is a very late-cycle phenomenon that can produce one final large-cap jam higher before the ultimate top — shown in both 2000 and 1972.

## Key Claims

### Software is the canary
- IGV Software ETF and individual SaaS names (shown: multiple broken charts) are in a historic crash. Friday's rally saw software "barely bounce" despite record call buying in IGV.
- The overlap between SaaS and private credit is the load-bearing nexus: SaaS companies were ground zero for PE buyouts; ~25% of BDCs are tech-exposed; software is 15–20% of private credit. Software loans in CLOs are also getting killed.
- **Reflexive contagion:** public software cracks → private credit marks down → BDC investors redeem → forced selling of public credit → loop back to software. *"A contagion boomerang is in full effect."*
- Apollo cut its private credit funds' software exposure **almost by half** in 2025 (from ~20%).
- Hyperscaler bonds barely rallied on the Friday equity bounce. Oracle CDS continued ticking higher despite the stock bouncing.

### Retail dominance — and vulnerability
- Retail options volumes bigger than the GME/SPAC mania of Jan 2021. Activity increasingly 0DTE. Small trader net long in NQ100 futures exceeds 2020 levels.
- Vanda: retail buying and SPX are moving in "virtual lockstep" — retail is determining marginal prices for the entire US equity market.
- Retail piled in at all-time highs with "everything they have" per Scott Rubner / Citadel.
- The 100% historical pattern: "retail will win for a time… and then leave it all on paper."

### Rolling Blowouts → Rolling VAR Shock
- Connects the current tape to his October 2024 "Era of [[RollingBlowouts]]" framework (2018 template). The [[RollingVARShock]] was touched off by the precious metals collapse in late January 2026.
- The Quant Quake earlier that week saw extreme moves in Momentum, Growth, and Value pairs. The **Crowded Long/Short pair** survived the quake unscathed — then had **its worst day since 27 January 2021** on Friday.
- 27 January 2021 = the day the retail/SPAC/Cathie Wood trash mania peaked. Paulo: *"That was the March 2000 Moment — even if the S&P 500 didn't top until September 2000."*

### "We have not seen The Fear"
- JPM de-grossing at −3z (extreme) but hedge fund turnover only +1.4z — "not extremely so." Momentum positioning "remains near highs globally" even after the quake.
- Barclays aggregate equity positioning at the **94th percentile**, "leaving little room for error." US equity mutual fund cash at extreme lows.
- *"We've seen the complacency and euphoria with an initial minor liquidation, but we have not seen The Fear."*

### [[BroadeningOut]] — the late-cycle catch
- Despite his bearishness, Paulo identifies an analog where things "actually do move up": **Broadening Out is a very late-stage bull narrative** which appears throughout history before one final jam higher by large caps into the ultimate top.
- **2000 analog:** NQ100 underperformed Russell in Jan 2000, then had one last run into the March top — which caught Druckenmiller buying hours from the high.
- **1972 analog:** Nifty Fifty broadened out in 4Q72 (smaller names caught up), then there was a final rotation back into the Nifty just before the 1973–74 bear.
- In both cases, even though small caps outperformed large caps during the bear, *you still lost money in absolute terms* — wait for the bear to run its course, then buy.
- The current Mag7 vs Energy chart ([[Nullcharts]] flagged: MAG7 topped vs. energy in December 2025, at the same level as October 2020 when XLE bottomed and ran 250% over 2 years) rhymes with the 2000/1972 pattern.
- Adam Smith quote: *"We are all at a wonderful ball where the champagne sparkles in every glass… we know the Black Horsemen will come shattering through the great terrace doors… but no one wants to leave while there is still time."*
