## Summary
[[HFIResearch]]'s 26 March 2026 *What Caught The Wire* column — the wiki's first source where HFI speaks in its own voice rather than as a data citation inside a Paulo note. Published ~4 weeks into the Iran conflict, it updates HFI's 9 March scenario framework with the new 400mmbbl IEA global [[SPRRelease]], enumerates concrete production shut-ins across the GCC ([[Iraq]] −3.6, [[Kuwait]] −2.35, [[SaudiArabia]] −3.05, [[UAE]] −1.8, [[Bahrain]] −0.18 = **10.98mmbpd total**), and defines the **Oil Market Breaking Point** as mid-April: the date by which tanker-flow normalization is required before onshore inventory damage goes from manageable to demand-destruction-territory ($200+ crude). Closes with an explicitly game-theoretic read of Iran's bargaining position that independently echoes [[PauloMacro]]'s [[NorthStar]] and [[MarkoPapic]]'s "update the utility function of the West."

## Key Claims

### The shut-in math as of 26 March 2026
- Total GCC production shut in = **10.98mmbpd**:
  - [[Iraq]]: −3.60mmbpd
  - [[Kuwait]]: −2.35mmbpd
  - [[SaudiArabia]]: −3.05mmbpd
  - [[UAE]]: −1.80mmbpd
  - [[Bahrain]]: −0.18mmbpd
- Saudi East-to-West pipeline **maxed out at ~4mmbpd** via the Red Sea ([[SaudiArabia]] Yanbu terminal — bypasses Hormuz).
- UAE Abu Dhabi pipeline **(Habshan–Fujairah) maxed out at ~1.8mmbpd**.
- Tanker traffic through the [[StraitOfHormuz]] *"remains nonexistent."*
- *"Even if the war ends tomorrow, it will take a few months to increase production and resume normal traffic."*

### Scenarios (updated from 9 March, incorporating the 400mmbbl SPR)
| Scenario | War resolution | Net impact on global inventories | Brent year-end path |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ends this week (late March) | −50 mmbbl | Initial sell-off into low-$80s → mid-to-high $80s avg |
| 2 | Ends mid-April | −210 mmbbl | Initial sell-off into low-$90s → mid-to-high $90s avg |
| 3 | Ends end-April | −370 mmbbl | Initial sell-off into $110s → **$110–$120 average** |

### The Breaking Point (central claim)
> *"For the oil market, there will be a breaking point. For the time being, the market is taking the view that the conflict will resolve before the middle of April. This view is important in the context of analyzing where oil prices are headed."*
- Mid-April is when "evaporation of global oil-in-transit" translates from *floating* inventory to *onshore* inventory depletion.
- If not resolved by then: the [[IEA]] will need to coordinate **another ~400mmbbl SPR release**; otherwise oil moves to **demand-destruction levels ($200+)**.
- This pre-falsifiably defines the date the market learns whether the SPR bridge is enough. The 7 April Iran/US two-week ceasefire documented in [[making-sense-of-the-last-two-months]] landed *inside* the breaking-point window, consistent with HFI's claim that the market is "taking the view" that resolution arrives by mid-April.

### Why the SPR bought time rather than solved the problem
- HFI's earlier [[SPRRelease]] piece: *"From an oil trading standpoint, traders will be reluctant to push oil prices up until the cushion is gone… the simultaneous SPR release will help dampen a lot of the worries over immediate supply needs, but it will only be a short-term fix."*
- If a deal by March 15: net inventory balance = **+110mmbbl** (400mmbbl SPR − 290mmbbl lost) → Brent to mid-$70s.
- If no deal to end-March: net balance = **−50mmbbl**, and **the deficit worsens by ~80mmbbl per week.**

### Why Iran has no tactical incentive to deal now (independent Nash derivation)
> *"Iran is fully aware of the math behind the oil market. All it needs to do is wait for the breaking point to unfold in the oil market before extracting max concessions from the US. From a tactical standpoint, it has no advantage to reach a deal now. The Strait of Hormuz card has been played and it can't be replayed again in the future."*

> *"As a result, the logic leads me to believe that it's not up to the US to dictate terms here. It's up to the Iranians, and given this being the case, it will push for the breaking point in the oil market. It will test the US's resolve. All it needs to do is 'survive' for another 3 weeks before things start to break."*

This is independent derivation of [[PauloMacro]]'s [[NorthStar]] / [[NashEquilibrium]] framing and [[MarkoPapic]]'s "update the utility function of the West" — three analysts, three routes, one game-theoretic lock-in. HFI adds the specific *"3 weeks to survive"* quantification.

### The [[EnergyAspects]] upside-case
- Energy Aspects: **total supplies lost = ~930mmbbl**; cumulative production lost May–December = **~340mmbbl** (the "air pocket" that will land on forward balances).
- HFI: *"Their estimate is far more aggressive than ours. In our storage sensitivity analysis, we did not factor in how countries like Iraq and Kuwait will take 3-4 months to bring production back to normal. It appears that we may be far too conservative on this front."*
- Goldman: *"The longer the conflict lasts, the higher oil prices will last."*

### "This time was different" (3 March 2026 tweet, cited)
> *"I'm not a geopolitical expert but here's some context to look at when thinking about it from the oil market's standpoint. In April 2024, Israel attacked Iran. Iran retaliated after, but only towards Israel. Some US bases were attacked, but they communicated in advance."*
- Post-28-Feb retaliations done **with zero notice**; Iran targeting Gulf nations, not just Israel. *"The response is what initially prompted me to think that 'this time was different.'"*

### GCC-incumbency read (independent echo of [[oil-and-retail]]'s Nash-2 extension)
> *"For the Gulf Coast countries, if the current Iranian regime is not toppled, it will be held hostage to this situation time and time again in the future. This is unacceptable even if there's some framework for a 'toll fee' going forward."*

HFI arrives at the same conclusion as [[PauloMacro]]'s 21 April [[oil-and-retail]] — that the GCC faces a structural-dominance problem that cannot be resolved by deal alone — but via the Gulf's *security* calculus rather than the Saudi *budget* arithmetic (`4mmbpd × $150 > 7mmbpd × $80`).

## Key Quotes

> "So yes, SPR buys time, but it does not fix the issue at hand. Tanker flows need to return to normal."

The combination of jawboning from the Trump administration, sanction relief on Iran/Russian crude, and the SPR release have kept prices at bay. **None of it will work once we hit this breaking point.**"

## Contradictions
- **UAE shut-in figure:** HFI 26 March says −1.80mmbpd; [[PauloMacro]] 21 April ([[oil-and-retail]]) says ~1.6mmbpd. Minor (~0.2mmbpd) and likely a timing artifact of UAE bringing some Fujairah-routed barrels back, not an analytical disagreement. Both figures are within measurement error of Kpler flows.
- **Saudi shut-in figure:** HFI 3.05mmbpd (26 March) vs. Paulo's ~3mmbpd (21 April). Consistent.
- **Breaking-point timing:** HFI's mid-April forecast was *bridged but not breached* by the 7 April ceasefire ([[making-sense-of-the-last-two-months]]). HFI's scenario-2 Brent path (mid-$90s) aligned with the realized price trajectory; the breaking-point threshold was not tested because the two-week ceasefire delayed it. This is a forecasting win with caveat: HFI correctly identified mid-April as the barrier; the market's ceasefire bet made it in-time by days.
- **Demand-destruction price:** HFI $200+; Al-Hajji ~$160. Both consistent with "above $150 is the floor of the danger zone"; HFI's threshold is the further-up-the-scale framing.
