Paulo explains *why* the bounce happened (short-term [[LiquidityMechanics]] from [[ScottBessent]]'s TGA drawdown + Fed RMP + the largest weekly hedge-fund short-covering in a decade) while maintaining that his [[NorthStar]] — Iran's [[NashEquilibrium]] strategy — hasn't changed. ## Key Claims

### The North Star = Nash
- Paulo's *North Star* is to see the conflict through the lens of [[NashEquilibrium]]: **Iran's strategy does not change regardless of Trump's moves.** Whether Trump leaves, escalates, puts boots on the ground — Iran continues to constrain oil/gas/fertilizer through the Straits until the economic pain forces "Never Again."
- *"Iran does not need to win militarily. It needs to sustain disruption at a level that forces the global economy to adjust under constraint."* — [[RobertPape]], who independently arrived at the same conclusion via his 5-stage "Escalation Trap."
- USS Tripoli and 31st Marine Expeditionary Force arriving in the next few days with at least two other battalion groups en route → **boots on the ground highly likely**, confirming the path remains escalatory.
- The 40-day mourning period for the Ayatollah ends April 9th — the IRGC has been running the show in the meantime.

### The TACO Misconception
- The [[NachoTaco]] / TACO framework worked during Liberation Day and other events because they were **crises of Trump's own making** — entirely within his power to reverse.
- The Iran war is **not in Trump's control** and requires Iran's cooperation to resolve. Under Nash, Iran's cooperation is not forthcoming at any price Trump can offer.
- Investors don't change their minds easily on concepts that have worked so well → hence the market jams risk higher on "ceasefire" headlines despite the North Star.

### Why the bounce happened: [[LiquidityMechanics]]
- **TGA drawdown:** [[ScottBessent]] held back debt issuance in anticipation of April 15 tax receipts, spending down the Treasury General Account by ~$300bn from late last year (>$200bn just since end of March). This injected liquidity into the banking system.
- **Fed RMP:** the Fed's Reserve Management Purchase program (initiated December 2025 after QT pushed bank reserves too low and short-term rates breached the Fed Funds corridor) continues to inject.
- Combined effect: bank reserves up ~$250bn since late last year, $100bn+ in the past 2 weeks alone.
- **This is short-term:** on April 15, tax payments flow into the TGA, draining the liquidity tailwind. Only the relatively small RMP remains. Paulo: *"the traditional Sell in May & Go Away phenomenon."*

### Positioning
- **Largest weekly ETF short-covering in a decade** — US-listed ETF shorts decreased 11.5%, the most in GS's records (−3.2 z-score). Hedge funds net bought macro products at the fastest pace since May 2025.
- This means fast money has **dumped its hedges** into the rally — they're exposed again.
- CTA and vol-control funds have "tens of billions of stock to buy back" per GS, which creates mechanical upward pressure.
- Retail panicked in late March (sold into the hole, bought puts) and is now getting squeezed back.
- Long-only mutual fund money remains sticky at pre-Liberation-Day levels — the one cohort that held on.
- Overall equity positioning at **27th percentile since 2010** — negative but far from Liberation Day, 2022, 2020, or 2018 lows.
- Dealer gamma is **negative on the upside** (accelerates moves up) and **positive on the downside** (dampens moves down) — asymmetric setup that favors continuation of the ramp.
- Corporate buybacks roaring back — "surprising considering the plunging FCF among hyperscalers."
- Analyst earnings estimates **still being raised** despite the oil crisis → a 100%-hit-rate recession signal from oil deviation vs. trend.

### The endgame framing
- "The barrels have been counted, and we are past the point of no return for a major global energy crisis."
- The boats *coming out* don't matter because they're already in the oil balances. It's the boats *going back in* to drain onshore Gulf storage that dictate when oilfields restart. Shut-in production is lost forever.
- **"Rallies in bear trends make the market weaker."** — trader JJ / Alyosha.
- Possible final-ramp pattern: major tops (1972, 2000, 2021-22) feature a late-cycle Broadening Out into small caps, then one final move back into megacaps as the "cleanest dirty shirt." Could this be that move?
- Paulo waits "in suspended animation… with Liquidity & Positioning on one side propelling the financial plate-spinning cartoon, and Can't Print Molecules on the other side… wondering when the two collide and fuse in a Tom Hanks Moment."
