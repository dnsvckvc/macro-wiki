## Summary
MacroVoices Episode 528 (16 April 2026) — the **most recent MacroVoices in the wiki**. [[LukeGromen]] (Forest for the Trees founder) interviewed by Eric Townsend. The wiki's **twelfth author**. Gromen elevates Michael Every's "1956 Suez moment" framing from a tail risk to his **base case**. Key contributions: (1) **"One-factor market"** — is Hormuz open or closed?; (2) the supply-chain lag confirmed — the Feb 28 tanker arrives *next week*, actual disruption "hasn't even started yet"; (3) operational intelligence on US military setbacks (Iranian hits on CIA section of Riyadh embassy; Fifth Fleet at Bahrain evacuated; Russians helping target); (4) **true interest expense = 102% of US receipts** through March — the fiscal math is past the breaking point; (5) **Net International Investment Position** framework — sovereign holders will sell $70T gross / $27T net of dollar assets for food and energy; (6) **Treasuries and stocks are below food and energy on Maslow's hierarchy**; (7) Trump's Treasury yield bogey is **4.4% not 4.6%** (every break triggers intervention; Bessent did $15B single-day buyback, biggest ever); (8) **37% of net UST issuance over last 4 years = Cayman Islands hedge funds** (basis trade) — foreign demand is lower-quality than it looks; (9) **Bitcoin as IGV-software correlation trade** for the next risk-off leg.

## Key Claims

### "One-factor market": Is Hormuz open?
- Easiest macro event to trade in some time — simultaneously easiest (binary: Hormuz open/closed) and hardest (inflation vs. deflation sequencing).
- **Suez 1956 is now the base case.** When the war started, Suez was Gromen's worst-case scenario; seven weeks in it's been promoted to base case.
- **Hormuz still closed in a month is his base case.** **10-20% chance still closed on July 4.** Nobody is positioned for that.
- *"If it continues, it's going to bring us closer to a non-linear break in supply chains. Just feels like a Wile E. Coyote moment."*

### Supply-chain lag has not landed yet
- The last tanker through Hormuz on Feb 28 arrives at destination *next week*. Actual disruption of supply *hasn't even started*.
- Early signals already visible: **Toto** (Japanese toilet maker) halted new orders for lack of raw materials, stock down 7%; social unrest over fuel in Ireland; individual names getting crushed on supply chain problems.
- Fertilizer on the clock for **growing season** — "you're not going to be throwing fertilizer down in June or July." Haber-Bosch math: world population 7.5B with synthetic fertilizer; 3.9B without (Our World in Data, 2015). This is the existential scaling constraint.

### Military/operational intelligence
- Iranians hit **CIA section** of US embassy at Riyadh with precision — not the embassy generally, but specifically the CIA office.
- Much of CIA evacuated from the Middle East early.
- **Fifth Fleet at Bahrain** — 1,500 people evacuated so fast initially they weren't allowed to take pets.
- Russians helping Iran with targeting.
- Air defense wasn't as good as publicly reported.
- *"If they're getting through [embassy + fleet defenses], sailing a ship through the Strait is like shooting fish in a barrel."* — reinforces Johnston's "TACO expectation isn't the main reason ships aren't moving; physical risk is."

### The fiscal math is past the breaking point
- Through March (6 months of fiscal year): **US true interest expense = 102% of receipts** (net interest + entitlements + VA).
- Decision point: print money for interest/entitlements, or default. Zero chance of default.
- *"There is nothing that is more inflationary in the long run than a government who needs to print the money just to pay the interest on their existing debt and obligations."*
- This is the operational manifestation of Gromen's famous axiom: **"Government deficits don't matter until they do, and then they matter a whole lot."**

### Trump's Treasury yield bogey: 4.4% not 4.6%
- Every time 10Y yield breaks 4.4%, Trump/Bessent intervene (TACO statements, Bessent action).
- Three ugly UST auctions in a row three weeks ago (including an *ugly 2Y*, "you should never have a bad 2Y auction").
- Five days later: **Bessent did a $15B single-day Treasury buyback** — the biggest in history.
- Shaping operations: *"Treasuries, when you look at the correlations, have become risk-off at risk-off / risk-on assets."*

### Net International Investment Position — the piggy-bank unwind
- World owns **$70T gross / $27T net** in dollar assets (accumulated from years of trade surpluses).
- Japan is the canary — massive NIIP, trillions in dollar assets.
- **Treasury bonds and stocks sit below food and energy on Maslow's hierarchy.**
- In a global food/energy crunch, sovereigns will **sell dollar assets** (stocks AND bonds) to buy food and energy. "In the same way that you or I would sell everything we own to feed our children."
- **Japan's price action is emerging-market-style already:** higher JGB yields are *weakening* (not strengthening) the yen against the dollar — EM signal that a country can't afford higher rates and must print. *"Everybody in the emerging markets is doing the Leonardo DiCaprio meme — I know what that is."*

### Foreign demand for Treasuries is lower-quality than it looks
- Nominal foreign holdings are at all-time highs — true but misleading.
- **Foreign central banks haven't bought net since 2014.** Slightly *down* actually.
- **Cayman Islands (US hedge funds) = 37% of net UST issuance** over the last 4 years (per October 2025 Fed white paper). This is the **basis trade** — heavily levered, sells Treasuries when risk goes off.
- Patient creditors replaced by hot money. Lower-quality structural buyer base.
- Long-term UST bond futures **priced in gold** are down **90% since 2014** — a one-way trade with stunning Sharpe ratio.

### Market manipulation / "shaping operations"
- **$500M notional oil short on March 24** front-ran a Trump tweet.
- **$950M notional ceasefire trade** front-ran the peace announcement.
- *"These are not our fathers' markets. There's a huge political impetus in these markets."*
- Bessent shapes yields; someone shapes oil. Trump's "I'm thinking about export controls" is enough to send oil limit down.

### China's move = supply-chain squeeze
- China is already past the "this is existential for China too" realization.
- *"They just need to squeeze supply chains a little, get inflation going, and the bond market will do the rest."*
- Iran's stated plan per the former IRGC commander: keep Hormuz closed, collapse global economy, see if Trump wants to keep being a "warmonger."

### Inflation/deflation sequencing
- Short run: inflationary (commodity pass-through) → deflationary (oil spike = recession, demand destruction) → then inflationary again (fiscal cannot fund entitlements + interest without printing).
- Cash + bullion in the short run because the sequencing is "too hard" to trade nimbly (explicitly cites Alden's "too hard" camp).

### Trade preferences
- **Cash + bullion** (gold) in the short run.
- **Long term:** energy, uranium, domestic electrical infrastructure, industrials selling into that; grocery stores (high fixed cost leverage); gold; **Bitcoin later this year** (using IGV-software ETF correlation as a tell).
