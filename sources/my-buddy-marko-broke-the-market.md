## Summary
[[KevinMuir]]'s *"Watch Your Bids"* podcast interview with [[MarkoPapic]], recorded Friday night 6 March 2026 (published 7 March) — just **one week into the war**. Retroactively the wiki's **earliest interview source on Papic's framework** and the earliest voice (alongside [[PauloMacro]]'s [[tom-hanks-has-covid]] from 5 March) converging on what would later become [[PauloMacro]]'s [[NorthStar]] game-theoretic thesis.

Muir teases Papic with the accusation that he has "broken the market" — meaning Papic has been so successful teaching the [[FadeGeopoliticalAngst]] framework that investors now reflexively refuse to price geopolitical risk even when it's warranted. Papic responds with (1) the fullest articulation in the wiki of **[[GeopoliticalAlpha]]**, his "material constraints on policymakers" framework; (2) his **January 6, 2026 long-Brent call** — the first time since Oct 7, 2023 that he stopped fading the Middle East, because "the Iranian regime is now existentially threatened"; (3) an independently derived game-theory argument that **Iran will not eat Trump's TACO** because it must *"update the utility function of the West so they don't attempt this nonsense for the next 50 years"* — the same insight Paulo codifies as [[NorthStar]]; (4) a **duration call of "mere days"** for Iran's drone closure of [[StraitOfHormuz]].

**Scoreboard as of 20 April 2026.** Through seven weeks of war:
- **[[FadeGeopoliticalAngst]] vindicated on equities.** S&P 7126 > pre-war 6879; Nasdaq 13 up days; Muir's own bull-pillar #1 in [[outlining-the-bull-and-bear-arguments]] concedes *"the buy the geopolitical angst trade worked in spades… just a little late in kicking in."*
- **Long-oil / Brent entry:** vindicated.
- **Game theory on Iran's Nash lock-in:** vindicated (no TACO eaten).
- **"Mere days" Hormuz duration call:** falsified — closure is 50+ days, US pivoted to sanctions + blockade rather than regime-destruction.
- **"Take profits on half" of Brent at 6 March:** premature — crude went higher.

Net: Papic got the equity meta-call right (this is the load-bearing framework call) and missed on Hormuz tactical duration. Paulo got the Hormuz duration right and is still waiting on the equity leg. Both still agree on the underlying game theory. This is the wiki's clearest inter-author empirical divergence to date — and the scoreboard is a contest between two complementary-but-distinct framings, not between "right" and "wrong."

## Key Claims

### 1. "Marko broke the market" — Muir's thesis
- Papic has so successfully taught the [[FadeGeopoliticalAngst]] framework via *Geopolitical Alpha* that the market now trades with *zero* geopolitical risk priced in.
- Muir: *"I think that now you've kind of convinced so many people of that the market is not even reacting."*
- Papic's self-deprecating response: *"I'm the Norman Angell of our era."* Reference to [[NormanAngell]]'s 1909 book *The Great Illusion*, which argued the economic cost of war was so catastrophic that no rational actor would start one — spectacularly wrong by 1914.
- Client joke quoted by Papic: *"My boss told me that you would fade your own death."*

### 2. [[GeopoliticalAlpha]] — the framework in full
Papic articulates the two reasons geopolitical events rarely have lasting market impact:
1. **The market always overstates the risk.** "It always starts to trade what's possible instead of what's probable. No one's there to talk about material constraints through the potential apocalypse."
2. **Reflexive policy pivot.** Even if things get dire, the event itself gives policymakers an excuse to pivot, stimulate, cut rates, or say "maybe that wasn't a great idea."

**But the framework requires analysis:** *"What [retail] don't understand is that they still have to do the geopolitical analysis."* Papic's phrase: *"There is nothing automatic or supernatural about fading geopolitical risk. It's based on a framework that focuses on material constraints, but you still got to understand those constraints and do the work."*

**Coined term: "geo-macro."** Papic: *"If you're not taking geopolitics to be endogenous to your process, I don't think you're a macro investor."*

### 3. Papic's 2025 track record — fading Israel-Iran
- Since 7 October 2023 (Hamas attack), Papic had been fading the Middle East consistently.
- Told clients: *"Two countries can nuke each other and it won't affect the rest of the world at all."*
- Reasoning: the *fulcrum* of Middle East market impact is [[StraitOfHormuz]]. Israel-Iran missile exchanges don't matter unless Hormuz is threatened.
- This was right in 2024 and 2025. Multiple rocket/escalation episodes during that period faded cleanly.

### 4. **The 6 January 2026 long-Brent call** — what changed
Papic flipped to **long Brent on 6 January 2026** — the first time since 2023 he stopped fading Middle East geopolitics. Triggers:
1. **US used the "R-word": regime change.** No plausible deniability for Iran anymore — this is existential.
2. **Gulf Arab states now have plausible deniability** for wanting the Iranian regime gone, so Iran can no longer manage its escalation against only Israel.
3. **Internal Iranian regime discord in December 2025** undermined the regime's cohesion.
4. **Technological asymmetry:** Iran can't defend its skies but it can impact shipping with drones — Houthis in the Red Sea were the precedent with "even less technology."

Additional **market-positioning setup** Papic flags as reinforcing the trade:
- Oil positioning was extremely one-sided short going into January. Client consensus: "massive blood, oversupply, economic fundamentals don't support oil long."
- Papic's counter: *"Unless you expect a global recession, how's oil going to go to 30? Saudi Arabia will just cut production — there is kind of a floor. So then what's the harm in being long?"*
- This matches [[PauloMacro]]'s 28 January [[oil-has-turned-a-very-big-corner]] setup exactly — independent convergence on the same contrarian long-oil trade in the same month.

### 5. Independent derivation of [[NorthStar]] game theory
This is the load-bearing contribution — Papic arrives at Paulo's conclusion via a different route, *one week into the war*:

> *"If I was running Iran, I would not eat the taco. I would say I need to update the utility function of the West so they don't attempt this nonsense for the next 50 years. I need to remind them because apparently they have forgotten why Strait of Hormuz matters and why."*

This is structurally identical to Paulo's "Never Again" / Pearl-Harbor framing of Iran's [[NashEquilibrium]]. Both are game-theory reconstructions. Both argue Iran cannot relent because relenting defeats the purpose of the deterrent. Papic's articulation is actually more abstract and structural than Paulo's, using a first-principles utility-function language.

Papic also names Trump's [[NachoTaco]] model: *"He doesn't really chicken out. He gets a deal. And the deal, you know, the deal doesn't get him to the moon, but he gets to go to the top of his house… In every previous situation where President's delicious taco was eaten whole by his counterparts, in this particular case the Islamic Republic might say, you know what, take your taco and shove it exactly where you should shove it."*

### 6. **The duration call — "mere days"** (now falsified)
Papic's key *calibration error*, cleanly identified for the wiki:
- Expected Iran's drone-closure of Hormuz to last **"not one year or five years or ten years — mere days."**
- Reasoning: Iran is in a "Mexican standoff against the largest military force ever assembled. The United States military is not going to invade you. They'll turn you into an economy-sized parking lot outside of an airport… they'll take the gloves off and start degrading their ability to exist as a post-medieval state. They'll take them back to the Abbasid Caliphate for those five, six, seven days."
- Accordingly he told clients to **take profits on half** their Brent long from the January 6 entry, expecting a "double bottom" in S&P / "double top" in oil.

**What actually happened (per subsequent wiki sources):**
- Strait has been under Iranian control **50+ days** as of 20 April 2026, not days.
- US has *not* taken "the gloves off" with a degrading punitive campaign — maximum pressure and a Navy blockade came later, but well short of "back to the Abbasid Caliphate."
- Iran's Mosaic Doctrine (per [[JimBianco]] in [[macrovoices-527-jim-bianco]]) means individual sector commanders keep firing even if command is degraded — exactly the operational model Papic didn't price.
- Papic's *direction* was right (long Brent, Iran won't fold); his *duration* was off by at least an order of magnitude.

### 7. Supporting observations
- **Macro backdrop more pernicious than summer 2024:** *"A lot of cockroaches in the private space that nobody really knows where they're going to end up."* Echoes Paulo's credit-cycle-already-turning thesis from [[intensely-concerned-for-risk-with-a-catch]] (7 February 2026).
- **White House dialog prediction:** Papic walks through an imagined Oval Office conversation where Hegseth ("I just did 47 pushups") and Trump both argue the market is fine, and Bessent pushes back that the underlying factors (labor, oil, inflation) that matter for mid-terms are "getting red hot even if that front burner of the stock market is not flashing red." This prefigures the actual policy dialog Paulo and Muir later document.
- **The "architect vs contractor" metaphor for his own trading:** Papic positions himself as the "architect" designing the strategic call; traders like Muir are "contractors" who execute and refine it. Humbler stance than his self-deprecating Norman Angell line implies.
- **BCA Research history:** 77 years old; founded 1949 by Hamilton Bolton; stewarded by Tony Boeckh; Papic joined 2011 to start the geopolitical service; now run by Matt Kirk. BCA = Bank Credit Analyst.

## Scoreboard as of 20 April 2026

### Papic's core framework has been vindicated on equities
The headline empirical result, which Muir himself concedes in bull-pillar #1 of [[outlining-the-bull-and-bear-arguments]]:

> *"The buy the geopolitical angst trade worked in spades… it was just a little late in kicking in."*

The S&P closed pre-war at 6879 and finished 17 April at **7126 — above pre-war**. Nasdaq ran 13 consecutive up days (first since 15 July 2013). [[FadeGeopoliticalAngst]] / [[GeopoliticalAlpha]] predicted exactly this: the market first mis-prices the risk, then the event gives policymakers an excuse to pivot, then risk assets recover past the starting point. That is the April 2026 tape.

In other words, Papic's meta-call ("this war will not have lasting impact on asset prices") has — so far — been more right than [[PauloMacro]]'s [[NorthStar]] equity-crash prediction. The "Marko broke the market" framing Muir opens with is, on the equity side, an *admission* rather than a counter: the framework that made the market complacent also kept it pinned higher.

### The duration / punitive-campaign miss
Where Papic was clearly wrong in this podcast:
- **Hormuz duration:** Papic said "mere days" (5-6-7); actual closure is 50+ days and counting.
- **US response:** Papic expected a degrading punitive campaign "back to the Abbasid Caliphate"; actual response has been sanctions + Navy blockade (per [[macrovoices-528-rory-johnston]]) but far short of regime-destruction campaigning.
- **Operational mistake:** Papic didn't anticipate Iran's [[StraitOfHormuz]] closure being sustainable through the Mosaic Doctrine (standing orders to keep firing across distributed drone/missile sectors even if command is degraded) — the mechanism [[JimBianco]] later codifies in [[macrovoices-527-jim-bianco]].
- Accordingly Papic's 6 March recommendation to **take profits on half** of the Brent long was premature (Brent continued higher).

Paulo's [[NorthStar]] can still be right on a longer horizon (the "stage 4 is pending" framing he and [[MichaelHowell]] both maintain), but the shorter-horizon scoreboard is unambiguously Papic's.

Paulo later elevates the framing to [[NorthStar]] on 12 April. The game-theory is identical; the two authors only diverge on whether that game theory flows through to a crash in risk assets.
