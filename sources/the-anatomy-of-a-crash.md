## Summary
The foundational [[PauloMacro]] essay (14 March 2026) laying out why the April 2026 market is displaying the classic pre-crash pattern he calls [[AnatomyOfACrash]], and why the crash is "days/weeks, not months" away. He walks through the [[TurduckenOfMarketRisks]] initial conditions (1973 Nifty Fifty + 2000 tech mania + 2007 credit + 1987 portfolio insurance), argues positioning has barely de-grossed, flags the [[DispersionTrade]] and [[BasisTrade]] as the two unwinds that will release index vol, documents a [[RollingVARShock]] hitting different hedge fund strategies in sequence, and notes deeply negative [[SwapSpreads]] signaling dealer balance-sheet stress. The sentiment section tracks a "Progression of Anecdotes" — the common-knowledge break is still forming. Discloses buying VIX calls on 14 March for the first time since late April 2010.

## Key Claims
- **Turducken of Market Risks** (historical stack):
  1. 1973 Nifty Fifty concentration taken even further — 40% of S&P in 7 names (plus a fresh oil crisis echo).
  2. 2000 US Exceptionalism / Tech/Dotcom/Retail mania.
  3. 2007 opaque credit structures and circular shadow vendor financing for datacenters in the hundreds of billions, on top of a decade-long private-credit mismark/unmark bubble.
  4. 1987 portfolio insurance, re-expressed as 0DTE intraday leveraged instability.
- **Rolling Blowouts → Risk Off** framework: originally developed summer 2018 (bitcoin Jan → Volmageddon Feb → FAANG/Libor Mar → 10Y through 3% Apr → Turkey/Argentina/Brazil May → Italy/Spain BTPs Jun). "Rolling Blowouts have only ever ended in one way — Risk Off."
- **Positioning has barely cut:** hedge fund gross still 86th percentile of past year / 97th percentile 5-year; private wealth barely cut per BofA Flow Show; long-onlys at historically low cash; participants are in *rotation* mindset (hedge and stay invested) not *de-gross* (sell to cash).
- **[[DispersionTrade]] is in the crosshairs of the [[RollingVARShock]]:** rising correlation is eating the long-single-stock-vol leg; wider single-stock spreads (Paulo's AirBNB June-puts anecdote: quote gapped *above* his bid, implied vol jumped from 40 to 55+) confirm the book is buckling. Once dispersion degrosses, they must buy index vol → index vol explodes → S&P down 1.5–3%/day.
- **[[BasisTrade]] is quietly unwinding:** hedge fund net short in futures has declined >25% over recent months; [[SwapSpreads]] deeply negative (10Y / 30Y approaching Liberation Day levels) signals dealer balance-sheet stress and the trade needing much more negative spreads to justify capital use.
- **Credit vol is leading Treasury vol higher** for the first time in this cycle. In 2022 the problem was a rate issue (rate vol led). Today credit vol leads — this is *both* a credit and a rate issue, which Paulo views as systemically more dangerous.
- **Rising Treasury collateral volatility forces hedge fund degrossing:** 2Y yield exploded 10bps Thursday over Fed Funds; multimanagers with upside-down-pyramid books can't sustain high gross when collateral whips.
- **No buyer:** hedge fund strategies are degrossing; retail is "suddenly going quiet"; long-only/private wealth at record-low cash; sovereign wealth and Asian surplus economies (Japan, Korea, Taiwan) face local-currency Brent at Russia/Ukraine highs and may have to liquidate Western assets. Japan year-end is two weeks away.
- **The Fed is stuck:** [[JeromePowell]] is chair until May; [[NeelKashkari]] said the Fed is "of two minds" on an oil crisis; [[KevinWarsh]] (Trump's potential pick) not yet confirmed; multi-month window of institutional inertia dangerous for the "Fed has our back" Pavlovian reflex. *"How does the Fed print oil?"*
- **The [[AnatomyOfACrash]] 4-stage pattern:**
  1. Initial correction and a relief bounce.
  2. Retest of the low, often with a minor breach and an intraday "tail."
  3. A 1–2 day final bounce ("all clear, we can rally because everyone is bearish").
  4. Collapse.
  Examples: 1987 Black Monday, 2021 BTC $60k→$30k, 1929, 1997 Hang Seng.
- **Index divergence is the most notable of Paulo's career.** All-time high dates (showing the staggered leadership break):
  - Nasdaq 100 — 29 Oct 2025
  - Russell 2000 — 22 Jan 2026
  - S&P 500 — 28 Jan 2026
  - Dow Industrials — 10 Feb 2026
  - Dow Trannies — 11 Feb 2026
  - NYSE Comp — 12 Feb 2026
- October 2025 was the "real" high because **beta peaked then** — and so did [[MAG7]]. The Nasdaq 100 is most concentrated (~40% in MAG7 vs. S&P ~32%).
- **Sentiment "Progression of Anecdotes":** from a "Grumpy Old Men" chat being the only bearish corner → Paulo's skeptical friends still Pavlovian on the Fed post-[[TomHanksHasCovidMoment]] → a lone pod PM "at his wits' end" on the Pretty Complete Taco Tuesday → institutional inbounds suddenly asking "where do I hide?" → allocators confirming the rolling VAR shock hitting different pods in sequence. Bearishness is only just beginning.
- Crashes are not born of euphoria — they happen when investors start to grow *concerned* from hyperextended positioning and unstable conditions.
- Discloses buying a significant amount of VIX calls on Friday 14 March — first near-dated VIX calls he'd bought in **nearly sixteen years** (since late April 2010).
