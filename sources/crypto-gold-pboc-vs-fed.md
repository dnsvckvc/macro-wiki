## Summary
[[MichaelHowell]] (CrossBorder Capital) — **27 April 2026, the most recent piece in the wiki and Howell's seventh wiki appearance.** Synthesizes the late-stage *Speculation* phase via three new framework crystallizations: (1) the **three engines of Global Liquidity** — US Fed, [[PBOC]], and the [[MOVEIndex]] — of which two are stalling and the third "decent but vulnerable"; (2) the **twin paradoxes of liquidity** ([[TwinParadoxesOfLiquidity]]): debt needs liquidity (refinancing) ↔ liquidity needs debt (collateral); and money has competing uses → strong real economies and strong asset markets rarely coexist; (3) the **four-signal warning watchlist**: SOFR-FedFunds spread (must stay near zero) / [[MOVEIndex]] (stay around 80) / 10-2 yield curve (avoid bearish flattening) / Gold + Crypto (do not break lower). Howell **quantifies the late-2025-to-April-2026 liquidity swing** (US$606bn): −$400bn shortfall in late-Oct 2025 → +$200bn surplus in early April 2026 = $251bn from eSLR regulatory change (1 Jan 2026, lower required reserves) + $192bn [[ReserveManagementPurchases]] cash + $163bn other. Excess reserves are now back to par. He **independently confirms Paulo's RMP-front-loaded-into-April-15 hypothesis** ("the Fed has previously warned that the RMP would be 'front-loaded' ahead of April 15th 'Tax Day'"). The PBoC has **stalled its easing** (likely Iran-driven caution), which Howell argues is now a headwind for gold via his explicit PBoC-cumulative-injections-vs-bullion causal chart. He elevates the [[Crypto]] basket (60% BTC / 30% ETH / 10% SOL) to a formal Global Liquidity barometer with a 13-week lead and 0.57 correlation since 2024 → "watch out for a tougher crypto market." Cyclical-vs-defensive equity internals + AI-based daily World economic activity model both rebound — *"as if nothing had happened"* — supporting his liquidity-crowd-out thesis: the real economy is absorbing money that previously inflated financial assets. **Conclusion: 2026 at best range-bound; continue reducing risk in tactical portfolio; maintain long-term inflation hedges in core.**

## Key Claims

### The "late-Speculation" diagnosis is intact
- *"Liquidity conditions; internal market movements; World economic data, and commodity prices all point to a late-'Speculative' phase of the investment cycle."*
- "Eery stand-off between fading liquidity and an urge to test new highs."
- *"It is not that investors cannot make profits in this phase, rather that exposure needs to more targeted and, given higher volatility, the quality of returns deteriorates."*
- The yield-curve phase taxonomy from [[bond-markets-point-the-way]] is unchanged: Speculation → Turbulence is still next.

### The Three Engines of Global Liquidity
Howell crystallizes a tri-partite framework for tracking incremental Global Liquidity creation. The crystallization is new in this piece even if the constituent pieces appear earlier in his work:

| Engine | Status (April 2026) | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| **US Federal Reserve** (RMP + balance sheet) | Stalling — RMP "front-loaded" ahead of April 15 | Asset purchases, RMP, regulatory changes |
| **[[PBOC]]** | Disappointing — easing has stalled | Cumulative Chinese central-bank stimulus |
| **[[MOVEIndex]]** | "Decent but remains vulnerable" | Low bond vol → buybacks + supports basis trade |

*"Two of the three main engines of Global Liquidity creation are stalling (US Fed and PBoC), and while the third (MOVE Index) looks decent again, it remains vulnerable."*

### Twin paradoxes of liquidity ([[TwinParadoxesOfLiquidity]])
1. **Debt needs liquidity (for refinancing) and liquidity needs debt (as collateral).** Financial crises threaten when debt-refinancing needs exceed available liquidity.
2. **Money has competing uses — cannot be in two places at once.** Investors may cheer good economic data, but the greater the liquidity demands of real economies, the less is available for asset markets.

*"In short, strong economies and strong asset markets rarely go together."* This is the formal articulation of the [[GlobalLiquidityCycle]]'s "shift to real economy" claim.

### Quantitative reconstruction of the Oct-2025-to-April-2026 liquidity swing — **+$606bn**
Bank-reserves-vs-adequacy went from a **−$400bn shortfall** in late October 2025 to a **+$200bn surplus** in early April 2026. The $606bn breakdown:

| Component | Amount | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Reduction in *required* reserves | $251bn | **eSLR regulatory change**, phased from 1 Jan 2026 |
| [[ReserveManagementPurchases]] cash | $192bn | Fed RMP (asset purchases) |
| Various other sources | $163bn | (Treasury operations, RRP residuals) |

In the past two weeks excess reserves have been trimmed back to around par. Howell: *"interesting questions to pose are whether these were 'one-off' moves and what would markets have done without these Fed changes? The latter answer is that markets would have likely struggled."*

The Fed's SOMA account shows a sharp **V-shaped rise** that *"should dispel any doubts that the Fed has revived (albeit temporarily) its QE-program."*

### Independent confirmation of Paulo's RMP-front-loading hypothesis
Howell: *"the Fed has previously warned that the RMP would be 'front-loaded' ahead of April 15th 'Tax Day'."*

This **directly confirms** [[PauloMacro]]'s [[the-north-star-vs-liquidity]] (12 April) prediction — that the TGA-drawdown + RMP support was a finite, calendar-driven tailwind that would reverse after April 15 tax receipts. Three authors now triangulate: Paulo's mechanic, Howell's "phoney rally" frame ([[a-phoney-rally]]), and now Howell's explicit Fed-side confirmation.

### Treasury bond-volatility management
- *"In our collateral-based World, low bond volatility is crucial. It not only supports the on-going [[BasisTrade]], but it is a crucial pillar in Global Liquidity creation."*
- The Treasury manages bond volatility through **buybacks**.
- **Quantitative rule** (Howell): each **10pt rise in the [[MOVEIndex]] triggers a near-$30bn jump in buybacks.**
- MOVE peaked at **115 on March 26**; closed last Friday at **67**.
- *"We view anything below 80 as a stimulus to Global Liquidity."*

### PBoC stall is a gold headwind
- The PBoC has stalled its expected aggressive easing, likely due to Iran/oil uncertainty.
- Howell's chart shows a tight historical relationship between **cumulative PBoC liquidity injections** and the **bullion price** — *"a clear cause and effect from the PBoC to the bullion price."*
- Implication: the gold consolidation [[OleHansen]] flagged in [[macrovoices-529-ole-hansen]] (and [[KevinMuir]]'s pause in [[buying-gold-again]]) has a **specific Chinese-policy explanation**, not just speculative positioning.
- *"Whatever the reason, this will (and may be has) affect the gold market, and, if continued, will feed negatively back on to commodity markets. Chinese liquidity needs to be carefully monitored."*

### Crowd-out from real economy — quantified
- Despite the Iran shock, **MSCI cyclical names outperforming defensives** (chart vs. World industrial business confidence).
- Howell's proprietary **AI-based daily World economic activity model** — based on commodity prices, credit spreads, trade-sensitive currencies — has rebounded strongly. *"Almost as if nothing had happened!"*
- Two interpretations: (a) investors view geopolitical tensions as transitory; (b) **pre-Iran economic momentum was much stronger than perceived** — and is therefore harder to derail.
- This is *why* Global Liquidity has slowed: money is flowing from financial markets to feed the working-capital demands of industry → confirmed by stronger commodity prices, *"performing exactly as expected in the 'Speculation' phase of the liquidity cycle."*

### Crypto as Global Liquidity barometer
- Howell formalizes a **BES$ basket: 60% BTC / 30% ETH / 10% SOL.**
- 13-week lead from Global Liquidity to BES$.
- 6-week log change correlation **0.57 since 2024** (recent shorter-window weekly chart).
- *"Looking ahead, if this linkage holds, watch out for a tougher crypto market."*

### Market-liquidity (microstructure) is already deteriorating
Per Howell's microstructure index (bid/ask spreads, volatility, market depth): *"deterioration in market liquidity… can already be seen."* The same Speculation-phase signature that precedes Turbulence.

### The SOFR-2yr spread — the new monetary tightening dial
- When the **2-year note yield > SOFR**, bond investors expect Fed funds to rise.
- Excellent metric in the last cycle: fell rapidly from late-2021 through 2022 *before* rebounding sharply ahead of the **October 2022 market rally**.
- Currently **modestly negative** — *"sitting in a 'no man's land'."*

### The Four-Signal Warning Watchlist
Howell's explicit watchlist for "warnings of upcoming trouble":
1. **SOFR spreads** (Fed funds and 2-year) must remain near zero.
2. **[[MOVEIndex]]** needs to stay around 80 or below.
3. **US Treasury 10-2 year yield curve** — *avoid a bearish flattening*.
4. **Gold bullion and crypto prices** — *do not break lower*.

If all four remain healthy, *"it may be 'safe' to stay invested."* Otherwise, *"join us in reducing risk."*

### Portfolio stance
- **Tactical portfolio:** continuing to reduce risk.
- **Core portfolio:** maintain long-term holdings, focused on long-term monetary inflation hedges.
- **2026 base case:** at best a range-bound Wall Street.
