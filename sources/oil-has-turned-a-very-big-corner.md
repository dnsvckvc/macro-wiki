## Summary
[[PauloMacro]]'s 28 January 2026 foundational oil bull thesis — the wiki's **earliest chronological source**, written one month before the Iran/US/Israel war. Paulo is accumulating "a significant position in oil" for a multi-quarter/multi-year run. He methodically walks through: (1) bearish consensus at a level he "can't overstate" (GS $56, DB $55, JPM $42–58, John Kemp's 657-respondent survey showing "tightly clustered" $62 for 5 years); (2) speculative positioning at Lehman/Covid extremes (managed money WTI went net *short* in 4Q25); (3) physical market contradicting the bear case (backwardated curves, Dated Brent persistently positive); (4) supply fundamentals showing the IEA's 4mmbpd surplus forecast is wrong (US shale Permian plateau, DOE possibly overstating crude by mixing in NGLs per [[MikeRothman]]/Cornerstone, OPEC spare capacity 1–2mmbpd not 3–5mmbpd); (5) China building 250mmbbl of new storage (+700kbpd just to keep fill rates flat); (6) optimal trade expression via front-month Brent (BNO ETF) to capture roll yield from backwardation, plus Dec27 OTM WTI calls for multi-year convexity. Introduces **[[ShrubsRazor]]** ("the funniest, most absurd outcome is the most likely one") and applies it: Trump banning US crude exports would be the ultimate irony. References [[KevinMuir]] on Canadian oil companies and cites an RBC report warning about what happens if there's an Iranian war lasting "longer than a weekend" — prophetic given what followed.

## Key Claims

### 1. Bearish consensus at a historic extreme
- Goldman Sachs: Brent $56 avg for 2026.
- Deutsche Bank: Brent $55 avg for 2026, $65 in 2027.
- JP Morgan: surplus of 2.8mmbpd in 2026, Brent to $40s by 4Q26, avg $42 in 2027.
- John Kemp's 11th annual oil price survey (657 respondents): Brent expected to avg $62 in 2026; **three-quarters of respondents in the $55–65 range**; forecasts "tightly clustered" for the full 5-year horizon (2026–2030). Uncertainty has declined to pre-Ukraine-invasion levels.
- Goldman client survey: ~60% bearish — a level exceeded only in April 2020 (WTI negative).
- Jim Cramer bearish on the retail side.
- Energy ETF assets as % of all US equity ETFs are **below 2020 lows**. Energy is 3% of S&P 500 market cap.

### 2. Positioning at Lehman/Covid extremes
- Non-commercial net long as % of open interest at levels not seen since 2010.
- Managed money WTI+Brent combined net long fell to nearly $0 — a level seen only in April 2020 *because WTI went negative and flipped the sign*. In 4Q25, managed money actually went **net short** briefly — the only other time was late 2008 (Lehman).
- XLE saw the **worst annual outflow in history** in 2025.

### 3. Physical market contradicts the bear
- Curves have been **backwardated** in the front months the vast majority of the past few years — "not the profile of a market that is rewarding storage."
- Dated Brent (immediate physical delivery vs front-month) has been **persistently positive** since October 2024.
- IEA calls for a 4mmbpd surplus in 2026 (109mm supply vs 106mm demand) — but IEA has "consistently underestimated demand ('missing barrels') which are subsequently revised higher many years later."

### 4. Supply fundamentals — the bull case
- **US shale plateau/decline:** Tier 1 Permian acreage long past the midpoint of exhaustion. Permian production growth has "flattened out and is now starting to roll over." Shale breakevens running $56–61/bbl — how does shale even replace steep declines at $50–55 consensus prices?
- **DOE may be overstating US crude** by including some NGLs in crude production figures (per [[MikeRothman]] / Cornerstone Analytics). DOE has not responded to inquiries.
- **OPEC spare capacity far less than consensus:** Paulo estimates **1–2mmbpd** of real spare (500k–1mmbpd from Saudi at ~10.5–11mmbpd, ~350kbpd from UAE, ~150kbpd from Kazakhstan, ~250kbpd assorted others). Consensus assumes 3–5mmbpd. 17 of 22 OPEC+ countries achieved peak production *before* Covid. [[HFIResearch]] ran a detailed audit confirming this.
- **Non-US, non-OPEC growth** (Brazil, Guyana, Canada, Norway) is modest — a combined ~1mmbpd annual growth in 1H26 per the IEA's own numbers, then flattening.
- **China building 250mmbbl of new storage capacity in 2026** — needs +700kbpd imports just to keep fill rates flat. "I want to buy what China is buying and *needs* to buy."

### 5. Trade expression
- **Front-month Brent via BNO ETF** — captures the roll yield (positive carry) from persistent backwardation. Even during the 2022–2025 decline, BNO returned +50% while Brent flat price was −25%. "So what exactly are the bears celebrating?"
- **Dec 2027 WTI 50% OTM calls** for multi-year convexity (fixed-loss, potential multi-bagger as the market transitions from "Superglut" to "Drawing").
- Prefers BNO over USO (Brent over WTI) because if Trump bans US crude exports under [[ShrubsRazor]], East of Suez barrels benefit.
- Oil equities (XLE, XOP, OIH) have outperformed crude since Liberation Day; on P/NAV at the strip, the majors/largecaps are at or above NAV — "not extremely cheap." Paulo prefers commodity-price convexity over equity beta.
- References [[KevinMuir]] on **Canadian oil companies** as attractive single-stock exposure; also likes non-US/international producers over US names given Trump intervention risk.

### 6. [[ShrubsRazor]]
Introduced here: *"The funniest, most absurd outcome is also the most likely one."* Applied: Trump (the most pro-free-market president in history) reinstituting the US crude export ban (which was repealed by Obama) would be the ultimate irony — and therefore, per Shrub's Razor, the most likely outcome if oil rallies hard.
