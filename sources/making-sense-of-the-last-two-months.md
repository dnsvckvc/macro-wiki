## Summary
[[KevinMuir]]'s 19 April 2026 retrospective — **part 1 of 2** (part 2 will outline bull/bear arguments). Written the Sunday after the 13-day Nasdaq winning streak that took the S&P **above its pre-war level** (pre-war 6879 → 19 April 7126), Muir tries to "make sense" of the two months since the 28 February offensive by breaking the move into **four discrete stages** and admits he is "flabbergasted" by the ferocity of the second-stage rally. He was never bearish *because* of the war — he has separate recession concerns — but the violence of the moves and the "never happened before" stats worry him. The piece is valuable to the wiki because it fills in **precise mechanical detail** missing from the real-time notes: Dec 2026 WTI as the barometer of Dec SOFR, exact SOFR repricing (2.05% → 2.95%), 3-month swaption vol divergence (1-yr ≈ Liberation Day levels, 10-yr barely moved), the March 30 market low coinciding with the [[JPMCollarTrade]] roll, the April 7 US/Iran two-week ceasefire as the second-rally catalyst, and the historical precedents for the current rally pace (Nasdaq 13 up days = 15 July 2013; "rally 10% in 10 days into new ATH" = **21 March 2000**, per [[WarrenPies]]).

## The four-stage breakdown

### 1. The setup (late February, pre-war)
- S&P 500 had gone sideways for four months.
- Most everyone was bullish; BofA's Bull & Bear Indicator at rarely-seen optimism levels.
- "Folks were getting more bullish, yet the stock market was failing to rise" — a quiet [[BruceKovner]] warning signal that didn't get acted on.

### 2. First part of the decline (week of 28 Feb → ~mid-March)
- US/Israel attacked Iran weekend of **28 February** ("all wars in the Trump era are fought on weekends when GLOBEX is closed").
- S&P *barely down* at the index level — but the under-the-hood action was dramatic:
  - Sector divergence: industrials and materials "absolutely hammered"; energy bid.
  - **[[Gold]] rallied one day, then collapsed $1,400 over next two weeks.** Post-hoc explanation: "too many weak longs chasing the recent best-performing asset were forced to de-lever. Adding to this, some Gulf States and Turkey had to sell gold to fund the war."
  - **L/S community's largest losses since Liberation Day** — "dramatic reductions in both longs *and* shorts," [[Degrossing]] rather than net short.
- **The signal:** first-stage decline was "more about levered players getting themselves back closer to neutral. They sold down the stuff they were overweight and brought in their shorts." Not across-the-board beta selling, but gross-exposure reduction — the hallmark of a podshop-and-quant-fund-dominated environment.

### 3. Second part of the decline (second half of March → low 30 March)
- As the Strait of Hormuz closure stretched, the market repriced longer-dated crude.
- **Dec 2026 WTI: $60 → $70 in second week**, then much higher in the second half of March.
- **Dec 2026 WTI became the barometer for where Dec SOFR would end the year.** [[KevinMuir]]: *"The Dec 2026 WTI crude oil contract became the best barometer of where SOFR (or Fed Funds) would end the year."*
- **Dec SOFR rate repriced from 2.05% (pre-war) to 2.95% at oil peak** — a ~90bps forward-rate tightening. All three previously-forecasted cuts removed; small chance of a *hike* priced in.
- **The front-end yield curve was the worst-affected market.** 3-month swaption vol chart:
  - 1-year tenor: exploded to *near Liberation Day* levels.
  - 10-year tenor: **barely rose.**
- Rising front-end stress drove selling across financial assets, eventually pulling SPX down **to the 6475 [[JPMCollarTrade]] short-gamma strike.**
- **The three-greek squeeze (gamma + vanna + negative [[Charm]]):** as the market pushed through the put strike, market makers had to sell into weakness and chase rallies, amplifying chop.
- **Market low = 30 March 2026** — "the last day before they rolled the position coincided with the low in the market."

Muir's synthesis: *"The second part of the decline was driven by rising oil prices, which caused implied central bank tightening of monetary policy. This front-end yield curve stress caused all financial assets to sell off, driving the S&P 500 down to the point where the JP Morgan Option Whale put position kicked in, creating maximum chaos."*

### 4. First part of the rally (~31 March → 6 April)
- JPM position rolled on the day of expiry → dealer short-gamma removed → *"an enormous source of uncertainty"* cleared.
- Bounce was "not out of the ordinary" at the index level.
- **But under the hood, 2021-style retail squeezes began.** [[AvisBudgetCar]] (CAR) rallied manically from 1 April. Dow Jones Transports **+23.37% from the 30 March low** — and **over half of that rise was AVIS.** "During the first two weeks of the rally, the market was already showing signs of trading in a 2021 fashion, where retail-led squeezes dominated."

### 5. Second part of the rally (7 April ceasefire → 19 April)
- **7 April 2026: Iran and the US agreed to a two-week ceasefire.**
- Immediate response: S&P gapped *through* its 200-day moving average.
- **Nasdaq: 13 straight up days** — the longest streak since **15 July 2013**. Most consecutive-up-day runs happen from oversold conditions; this one started **less than 10% from all-time highs.**
- [[WarrenPies]] (3-Fourteen Research): the **only prior time** markets rallied 10% in 10 days *into new all-time highs* was **21 March 2000**. (Narrator: "*and that wasn't a good time to buy stocks.*")
- **Current S&P level: 7126.** Pre-war close (27 Feb 2026): 6879. **The market now trades above pre-war.**

## Key Claims

### 1. Two retrospective puzzles
Muir names two things that surprised him most:
1. **Long-dated crude as the forward-rate barometer.** *"The market was more sensitive to longer-dated crude than I had ever imagined. The Dec 2026 crude oil future was setting the tone for front-end interest rates, which then translated into other financial assets like equities."*
2. **The SPX option market as the tail wagging the dog.** Market bottomed on JPM-position roll date — "we have seen this phenomenon occur time and time again." This post-hoc confirms the [[JPMCollarTrade]] mechanics Muir himself laid out on 7 March in [[vix-inversion-buying-bonds-japan-jpm-option-whale]].

### 2. The peace-dividend bull case (via Jamie Dimon)
[[JamieDimon]] (JPM CEO) articulated the long-term bull case in a recent interview:
> *"I do think there is more geopolitical risk than we have seen since World War II. Can it play out in a way that's good? I think in the Middle East, it's possible they end with a situation with a higher chance of long-term peace. Because Saudi Arabia wants it. UAE wants it. America wants it. Israel wants it. And we've destroyed — for the most part — the really bad proxy actors there."*

Muir cites this as the "50-year Middle East prosperity" narrative that some top PMs argued for even during the war's darkest days. Market exuberance the week of 19 April "is at least getting partially priced in" — see [[PeaceDividend]]. Muir doesn't endorse; he acknowledges: *"we will never know where the market would have traded without the war, so maybe this is simply the market returning to where it would have been absent the conflict."*

### 3. The unprecedented pattern
[[BespokeInvestmentGroup]] stat cited: current price action is a set of "this has never happened before" events stacked on each other. Muir's rule of thumb: *"when a market behaves in a way that has never happened before, it worries me. Although the squeeze has been to the upside, the violence within parts of the market is not reassuring."*

### 4. Position reinforcement via "new reality" framing
- "This is just the new reality of an environment dominated by podshops and quant funds" — the early-war [[Degrossing]] signature is now the baseline risk-reduction pattern, not a risk-off flush.
- The [[JPMCollarTrade]] / "tail-wags-the-dog" phenomenon is recurring, not a one-off.
