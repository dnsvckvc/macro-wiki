## Summary
[[MichaelHowell]]'s 12 April 2026 note — published the same day as [[PauloMacro]]'s [[the-north-star-vs-liquidity]]. Both authors address the same phenomenon (the risk-asset bounce) and arrive at the same conclusion (it's not durable) from different frameworks. Howell calls it **"a phoney rally"** and warns against BTD (buy the dip). Global Liquidity peaked in late 2025 and is falling — the cycle dominates any geopolitical noise. The Fed has provided some support via RMP ($200bn increase in Treasury holdings YTD, pushing bank reserves ~$600bn above late-2025 lows), but "put into historical context, this is **not** an abundance of liquidity." The yield curve is flattening with a humped term structure — a pattern that "often predates a Risk Off move." Howell's core 2026 view is now playing out. He "strongly avoids a BTD strategy" and recommends shifting toward fixed income, paring credit, and owning monetary-inflation hedges like gold.

## Key Claims

### The rally is phoney
- A "deal" with Iran will trigger a relief rally, "but the key question is then what?"
- Global Liquidity peaked late 2025 and is falling, driven by a strengthening real economy and rising energy costs. This dominates geopolitical noise.
- Crypto (60% BTC, 30% ETH, 10% SOL basket) confirms: recent performance in-line with weak Global Liquidity. Chart warns of future volatility.
- S&P 500 vs. Fed Liquidity (advanced 25 weeks): "the trend in Fed Liquidity still looks weak, and is a monetary policy, at best, suited to a range-bound market."

### Fed support — real but insufficient
- RMP has expanded the Fed's Treasury holdings by ~$200bn YTD.
- Bank reserves have risen ~$600bn from late-2025 lows — now ~$200bn above the "adequate" threshold on Howell's reckoning.
- Howell suspects the Fed and Treasury "have together tried to cap the rise in bond volatility given its importance both for the continuing hedge fund [[BasisTrade]] and for credit creation in repo and collateral markets."
- But this doesn't change the cycle: "the trend in Fed Liquidity still looks weak."
- Fed Liquidity targets proposed by policymakers ([[StephenMiran]]'s paper) "look unrealistic to us."

### Fixed income flashing Risk Off
- US yield curve has flattened since end-2025: 2Y yield up 30–40bp.
- Curvature/convexity of the term structure has increased and outpaced the change in slope.
- "A flat and humped term structure often predates a Risk Off move."
- Yield curve slope is following previous movements in liquidity; the liquidity track points to further flattening in coming weeks.

### Asset allocation
- **Core holdings:** monetary-inflation hedges (gold). "Own hedges, don't trade them."
- **Tactical:** US late-cycle (energy, resources, consumer staples, utilities > tech, financials). China is early-cycle (technology could perform). Credits pared back substantially.
- **Bond duration rebuild.** Short and mid-duration government bonds preferred.
- **May not bottom until 2027.**

### Five things to monitor
1. Global Liquidity
2. Yield curve slope
3. MOVE index (bond vol)
4. SOFR spreads
5. Gold price
