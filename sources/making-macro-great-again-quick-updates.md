## Summary
[[PauloMacro]]'s 12 March 2026 update covering three topics: (1) the supply-chain disruption from the [[StraitOfHormuz]] closure is **"Covid on steroids"** but in an economy running at full activity (not lockdown), meaning problems compound far faster — anecdotes of diesel spiking 10% in Brazil disrupting soybean loadings and bunker-fuel shortages at Hormuz ports; (2) the [[SPRRelease]] of 400mmbbl is structurally inadequate (US portion: 172mmbbl out of 415mmbbls → drawdown to ~243mmbbl near tank bottoms; the SPR caverns become structurally unsound past those levels; the market "isn't even blinking anymore"); (3) **volatility and hedges vs. degrossing** — correlation is jacked (1m implied correlation at Liberation Day / SVB crisis levels) but VIX hasn't had "The Fear" yet → the [[DispersionTrade]] is about to lose control, and nobody has really degrossed, only bought hedges. Also flags deepening swap spreads (the [[BasisTrade]] unwinding), hyperscaler bonds (AMZN, CRM) trading badly, and private credit cockroaches (Cliffwater, Morgan Stanley redemptions, JPM markdowns, Deutsche Bank exposure). Restates the [[TurduckenOfMarketRisks]] definition.

## Key Claims
- **"This is Covid on steroids."** With Covid, economic activity had nearly stopped so demand for materials was below normal. Today activity is fully normal, so supply-chain problems are "much bigger much more quickly."
- Anecdotal evidence is *systemic*: Brazil diesel up 10% disrupting soybean logistics; bunker-fuel shortages at ports near Hormuz even with tankers waiting; these are not isolated — they're the "thousands of unseen effects" that assure a hard landing.
- The 400mmbbl global [[SPRRelease]] is "a drop in the bucket vs what is not moving." US SPR at 415mm, drawing 172mm → 243mm, near regulatory/structural limits of the salt caverns. Tank bottoms. Plus eventual refill = demand not in the curve.
- "This crisis will end when demand breaks, not when supply gushes. The more authorities try to avoid price discovery, the longer they prolong demand destruction."
- Houthis may have struck a US tanker; risk of missiles toward Saudi's Yanbu port (alternative Hormuz egress).
- **Correlation vs. VIX divergence:** 1-month implied correlation is at Liberation Day / SVB crisis levels, but VIX only hit "Bear Stearns VIX 30" fear — not "The Fear." Correlation has room to keep rising → [[DispersionTrade]] is about to lose control.
- Nobody has degrossed. They've bought hedges that aren't paying off. "At some point it won't be enough to own hedges… the decision will be made to sell."
- Deepening long-dated [[SwapSpreads]] → [[BasisTrade]] guys cutting books → explains why cash bonds are weakening.
- Hyperscaler bonds (AMZN, CRM issuances) trading badly. Private credit redemptions at Cliffwater and MS; JPM markdowns; Deutsche Bank exposure.
- Restates [[TurduckenOfMarketRisks]] in full: 1973 concentration + 2000 US Exceptionalism + 2007 opaque credit + 1987 0DTE instability. *"Injecting an oil crisis and global growth shock into this set of initial conditions is… historic."*
