## Summary
Second segment of MacroVoices Episode 525 (26 March 2026). [[MichaelEvery]] (Rabobank Global Strategist, economic and markets department) interviewed by Eric Townsend. The wiki's **ninth author** and first dedicated **geopolitical-strategic / realpolitik** voice. Every operates at the intersection of finance, economics, and geopolitics — a level up from the market-trading focus of most other authors. Key contributions: (1) the **fog of war + media-narrative warfare** framework — US and Israel repeatedly appear misaligned only to coordinate; media headlines can't be trusted; (2) the **Hormuz transit reality** — 6 ships vs. normal 138 daily regardless of Iranian toll-road rhetoric; (3) the economic impact is **exponential, not linear** — first Asia, then Europe, then US, with ironic *deflationary* fire sales mixed in; (4) the market is already **completely segmented** (Asian oil $150-160, Singapore jet fuel briefly $230); (5) **no one can just end the war** — US walking away would be a "1956 Suez-style crisis" for US hegemony; (6) **"reverse perestroika"** framework for Trumponomics; (7) **stablecoin statecraft** as the next-phase financial architecture emerging from the conflict.

## Key Claims

### The fog of war and media-narrative warfare
- *"There's no one you could talk to right now who can give you a complete answer on anything."*
- US and Israel have repeatedly fooled the world by appearing misaligned — Trump angry with Bibi, Bibi angry with Trump — and then striking in complete coordination (Operation Midnight Hammer, 2025).
- *"The algos trade the headlines. Therefore everyone else has to go along with the algos, and effectively the narrative becomes a reality until the reality is changed by a sharp application of physical force."*
- When Iran says "the Strait is open for a $2M toll," that's rhetoric, not reality. **6 ships transiting vs. normal 138.** Until transit gets to "high double digits," statements mean nothing.

### Economic impact is exponential, not linear
- Shortages of diesel, bunker fuel, jet fuel hit Asia first, then Europe, then US (less).
- Critical insight: **it's not CPI inflation — it's outright unavailability.** "No bunker fuel → ships don't go → global trade breaks down."
- **Ironic deflationary element:** people desperate for cash fire-sell inventory at any price. Thailand example: truckload of strawberries stranded on motorway without diesel, sold to passing vehicles at fire-sale prices.
- Already visible: hundreds of filling stations in Australia running out; panic buying across Asia.

### Market already completely segmented
- Brent ~$100, WTI ~$95 — but **Asian oil $150-160**, Singapore jet fuel briefly $230 (today $200).
- The single world market for energy has broken down. Al-Hajji's "price differentials, not levels" (per [[macrovoices-526-dr-anas-al-hajji]]) is the same observation from the sell-side.

### No one can just end the war
- **US can't walk away** without a "**1956 Suez-style crisis for the US**" — it won't be able to project power anywhere at the same scale again. Existential for Trumponomics and Every's "**reverse perestroika**" framework.
- **Israel can't give up** — Iran would accelerate bomb program (slow route or smuggle from North Korea).
- **Iran has incentive to keep escalating** to force US/Israel home, and would then bully the GCC even more — a geopolitical catastrophe for the West.
- **Even if resolved:** supply-side damage unknown. Qatar LNG down 17% for **3–5 years** minimum. Shut-in oil wells uncertain. If Houthis fire in Red Sea again (Saudi can't use Yanbu bypass), things get worse.
- Every's best guess: **"a couple of weeks"** to resolution, but with more escalation first. "Optimist wrapped in a pessimist." The tail risks are "extremely, extremely ugly."

### US leverage and the "co-control Hormuz" trial balloon
- If desperate partners come to the US begging for resolution, the US won't "make nice" — it will demand leverage. "What will you do for me?"
- Trump has openly suggested **the US should run Hormuz or co-control it with Iran.** Mocked in media, but "don't dismiss — the entire geopolitical architecture is in motion."

### Europe pivoting to China/Iran?
- Not fully impossible but two major obstacles:
  1. **Russia is in the Iran camp** helping fight US/Israel — Orwellian doublethink for Europe to pivot toward an alliance with Russia's ally.
  2. **Europe is deeply enmeshed** in American systems: Eurodollar, tech stack, NATO, LNG imports. Enormous transition cost.
- *"It's very easy in principle to say flip a switch. The real picture is much, much more complex."*

### Stablecoin statecraft as next-phase architecture
- Once one side takes de facto control of Middle East energy, **US-based (or Chinese) stablecoins will be rolled out as part of the new financial architecture.**
- Already in motion in the background. US version: freewheeling private sector. Chinese version: centralized Renminbi stablecoins, government-run.
- *"2026 has got a lot more to offer you yet."*
