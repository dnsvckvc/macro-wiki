## Summary
[[KevinMuir]]'s 27 March 2026 note announcing he is *"waving in"* gold again after a **27% correction from the highs** — a correction that confused many because it happened *during* an active Middle East war. Muir's explanation: it was a positioning unwind, not a fundamental repricing. Too many speculators were crowded long going into the conflict and sold what they were overweight; Gulf nations long gold faced cash calls and sold too. The structural long-term driver — [[PBOC]] and other central banks diversifying out of Western paper into gold, Muir's [[LedgerToRealWorldThesis]] — is unchanged and, if anything, *strengthened* by the confiscation of Russian assets and the new geopolitical regime. He calls the $4100 Monday-morning washout "a good chance" of being the low and is already long gold outright, considering adding gold miners. Says he's even more constructive on **gold vs. SPX** on a relative basis.

## Key Claims
- Gold corrected **~27%** from the highs — unusual during active war, which typically supports gold.
- The move was driven by positioning, not fundamentals: speculators forced to sell what they were overweight; Gulf sovereign holders needing cash; *"too many investors were too long gold and when war broke out, they sold what they were overweight."*
- Muir exited gold before the final mania phase: *"I can't sit through that kind of mania. Don't look to me for guidance for sucking the last juice out of a trade. I am more valuable highlighting the stuff that's on page 16 on the way to page 1 than navigating the page 1 trades."*
- **The fundamental reason for owning gold is unchanged** — arguably strengthened by recent geopolitical events. The thesis is about large central banks being *forced* to diversify into gold, not about inflation, DXY, or real rates.
- **[[LedgerToRealWorldThesis]]** (March 2022): the West's decision to confiscate Russia's financial assets permanently changed how central banks think about reserve composition. You cannot own paper assets denominated in a currency whose issuer can freeze them.
- The [[PBOC]]'s objective function is to **maximize total gold accumulation over the next decade** — *not* to chase the price. That means they rationally *stepped away* from the market during the speculative rally (not wanting to push price further) and will *resume buying* now that the shakeout has removed late speculators.
- Nothing has changed in the past two months to alter PBOC incentives. If anything, events have *increased* the urgency of diversification.
- Specific price call: the **$4100 Monday-morning washout** is likely the low of this correction.
- Relative trade: outright long gold, *even more constructive* on gold vs. S&P 500.
- "Best bull markets have the most violent corrections" — the 27% drawdown is classical bull-market-correction behavior.
- Acknowledges the usual pessimistic talking points (rising USD, rising short-term rates) but dismisses them as irrelevant to what drives gold over the long run.
