## Summary
**[[MarkoPapic]] and [[JacobShapiro]]'s *Geopolitical Cousins* podcast** with **[[RoryJohnston]]** — Johnston's **third wiki appearance** (after [[macrovoices-524-rory-johnston]] 19 March and [[macrovoices-528-rory-johnston]] 16 April). Recorded approximately 23 April 2026 (~8 weeks into the war; Brent at $106.44 prompt; references to "this morning's piece" in which Johnston steel-manned the sanguine market reaction; references Trump's "four days ago" power-plant rhetoric and Papic's "March 30th MAD" frame). The piece is framed as a comedy gimmick — Papic attempts to drive Johnston through four escalating stages of doom ("snarky tweet → Crown Royal → dousing with gasoline → self-immolation") — but produces **the wiki's most concrete near-term Brent price-path forecast from an oil specialist**, a significant **Papic-vs-Johnston disagreement** on who has more leverage (Trump vs. Iran), and two new wiki concepts ([[PrecautionaryDemand]] and [[MutualAssuredDestructionEnergy]]).

## Key Claims

### Johnston's Dec 2026 Brent price path
Most important new data in this source — **Johnston's explicit scenarios**:

| Hormuz reopens | Prompt Brent response | Dec 2026 Brent forecast |
|---|---|---|
| May 1 | Drops $20/bbl in one day on resumption → grinds higher | $95-$100 unless second IEA SPR or recession |
| Still closed June 1 | N/A | **$130-$150** |
| Still closed July 1 | N/A | **~$195-$200** |

> *"I think that I'm just following my brutal logic. And that, I think, showed that we hit 195 by the end of June if Hormuz was not open."*

Johnston explicitly acknowledges this is **more linear and less doomy than Papic's call**: *"I think you're more linear than I would have thought."* Papic's own client-facing call was $150-$170 if unresolved by mid-May.

### The 550mmbbl-to-1bn cumulative loss curve
- **As of this recording:** ~**550mmbbl** of oil that was supposed to be produced this year but now won't be.
- **If Hormuz reopens May 1:** the accumulated loss rises to **nearly 1bn bbls** because restart lags mean even an immediate-resolution scenario leaks another 450mmbbl through Q2.
- Cross-checks with [[HFIResearch]]'s 1bn-by-end-March / 1.2bn-by-end-April / 1.98bn-by-end-June curve in [[wctw-the-oil-market-breaking-point-is-here]] — Rory's number is lower, matching his slightly-less-aggressive modeling assumptions.

### The SPR capacity math
- Global strategic + commercial-mandated stocks = **~1.2bn bbls** total.
- First coordinated release = 400mmbbl (one-third of capacity) = **the largest in history, by more than 2x.**
- Rory's base case: **a second ~400mmbbl release is increasingly likely.** "Could do that two more times" — after which global strategic stockpiles would be drawn down by **two-thirds.**
- If that happens: deficit mostly absorbed → avoid "rip-roaring" spot prices near-term → *but* leave the world in a permanently more fragile state.
- **Key framing:** in Rory's model, SPR *draws* are treated as supply (shifts curve right); SPR *builds* are treated as demand → post-crisis SPR refill obligations will be a durable demand sink for years.

### The commercial-inventory cushion
- Global commercial stocks: ~**2.5-3bn bbls**.
- **Working capacity** — the portion that can actually be drawn without breaking the flow physics — is more like **1-2bn bbls.** Below that, the system can't keep functioning because you always need enough inventory to absorb timing mismatches.
- A billion-barrel loss is "a lot" relative to even this working buffer — and each month of continued closure accumulates another ~400mmbbl.

### The [[PrecautionaryDemand]] overshoot — the piece Rory published "this morning"
Rory's same-day Commodity Context piece (which Papic and Shapiro hadn't yet read) argues that the Dated Brent spike to **~$144** earlier in the war was most likely a *precautionary-demand* overshoot:
- Physical-market participants *bid forward* demand to secure ASAP barrels for fear of future unavailability — not an underlying flow-balance signal.
- After the ceasefire and initial tightness normalized, prices fell back.
- Implication: the *initial* price spike was arguably larger than the inventory-math justified, and the subsequent sell-off is now *confirming the bearish narrative* on the downside even though the inventory picture continues to deteriorate.
- This is Rory's "steel-man" of the market's sanguine reaction — it's not crazy; it's rational given the SPR math, the commercial stock cushion, and the (legitimate) expectation function that Trump eventually taco-es.

### Rory's self-identification reversal
> *"Everyone thinks of me now as this like alarmist kind of hair-on-fire permabull. My reputation in the oil market prior to this was that of a perma-bear… I wrote a lot of pieces going into this year on the coming oil glut… And I kind of joked, like, the only thing that would drive us to triple-digit oil would be like if someone crazily closed the Strait of Hormuz. And voila, that's exactly where we are."*

This reframes Rory's current forecasts as coming from a **structurally bearish analyst** who has simply updated on data — not a permabull confirming priors. Consistent with his self-disclosed initial bias in [[macrovoices-524-rory-johnston]] ("relatively Pollyanna-ish going in").

### Papic's March 30 [[MutualAssuredDestructionEnergy]] frame
Papic's major argumentative contribution:
- **30 March 2026:** Israel struck Iran's [[SouthPars]] gas facility; Iran hit Qatar LNG (Ras Laffan / [[SaadAlKaabi]] infrastructure) the same day.
- Within 24 hours of reciprocal upstream-facility strikes, the world saw that ~30% of global LNG and ~20% of crude could go offline within a single exchange.
- Papic: *"What we achieved is this moment of mutual assured destruction where it's very clear that within 24 hours we can lose… 30 percent of LNG on the planet."*
- Implication: a kinetic re-escalation by either side is now *nuclear-equivalent* in its consequences — both sides know it. Papic uses this to argue that the war is effectively stalemated kinetically even if Hormuz remains closed.
- **Rory endorses** the MAD framing but disagrees about who has more leverage — Rory thinks Trump's political leverage *decays faster* than Iran's economic leverage. *"I think Trump is going to bend and blink first to that pressure versus Iran."* Papic counters that regime change is "off the table" and Trump has *already* blinked.

### Papic's contrarian-within-wiki view
This is where the podcast generates its cleanest author divergence *inside a single conversation*:
- **Papic (reframed from earlier):** Regime change off the table after 30 March MAD; Trump is blinking already; "canisters and cannibalism" is the line Iran dare not cross because if it does, *the whole world lines up against Iran* (Bangladesh famine, Indonesian unrest, etc.).
- **Rory:** Iran's strategic patience is more durable than Trump's attention span; Hormuz is "a wasting strategic asset" but Iran has room to keep it closed longer without suffering sufficiently.
- **Shapiro:** agrees with Rory — Trump has less runway than Papic thinks. *"He has to do it sometime in the next week or two for the Xi Jinping summit."*

### The Russia-Ukraine procyclicality dynamic
Johnston's underreported-in-wiki observation:
- Ukrainian attacks on Russian oil infrastructure in the Black Sea and Baltic have been *escalating* during the Iran war.
- **Pro-cyclicality mechanism:** higher oil prices → greater Russian export windfall → stronger Ukrainian incentive to attack Russian infrastructure → tighter global balances → higher oil prices. An upward cascade.
- Mid-March 2026: Russian oil industry saw a **~40% reduction in export capacity** — the largest single disruption since the fall of the Soviet Union. Mostly recovered within weeks (no Hormuz-equivalent choke point in Russia's favor).
- Trump admin had progressed substantially in tightening sanctions on Russian/Iranian/Venezuelan oil *before* the Iran war — then reversed both Iranian and Russian floating-storage sanctions to facilitate any-grade supply to the market. (Bessent said "will not extend" two days before quietly extending.)
- Consequence: pre-war Russian discount of 30+ bbl has collapsed to single digits; Indian buyers are back at ~normal Russian volumes; Urals landing at premiums to Brent in India.

### The "Rory's tanker map → Trump retweet" incident
Johnston alludes twice to Trump having reposted *his* tanker-flow map, which Papic interprets as having convinced Trump that the US has enough oil to export to the world:
- Rory: *"Because Trump took my map and now I'm worried I changed the course of world history."*
- Papic: *"I think he did, brother. I don't like that, Marco. That makes me want to do it myself."*
- This is a small but telling anecdote about how Trump's oil-market mental model gets constructed from ad-hoc social media consumption rather than the physical-trader intelligence that Vitol, Trafigura and similar have been providing directly to the White House per Papic.

### Rory's US/North America framing
- **"North America is now the most energy-secure place on Earth by a long shot"** — but that doesn't mean there's extra oil to export to the world. US is a net crude importer even though it's a refined-products exporter.
- The apparent "flood of VLCCs heading to the US" is just normal balancing: the US exports more WTI while importing Latin American crude, with refining runs possibly cut if the math pinches.
- Crucial: **US diesel exports currently go mostly to Latin America**, which will be outbid as Asia and Europe pay more → US refining might redirect diesel to higher-paying markets, producing Latin American shortages.
- *"Poor get poorer"* — whoever has the lowest preventative spend for barrels loses access first.

### The "April 9 price blow-up"
- Both Rory and Papic reference April 9 (or April 9-ish Monday) as a **major trader-washout event** — a $15+/bbl single-day drop that "was really painful" and blew out many physical longs.
- Compositional effect: traders most willing to run long physical risk through the crisis have been *selected out* of the market by this and similar episodes. Remaining market participants are more cautious, reinforcing the "sanguine" price reaction that looks like complacency but is actually **survivorship bias** among traders still holding positions.

## Contradictions
- **Papic-vs-Rory on leverage decay:** Papic thinks regime change is already off the table and Trump has blinked; Rory thinks Trump's patience runs out first. Both are internally consistent; the disagreement is about timing/magnitude rather than mechanism. Resolves empirically as the crisis proceeds.
- **Rory's Dec 2026 Brent path ($130-$150 by June 1, $195-$200 by July 1)** vs. [[HFIResearch]] "no price for outright shortages" past April (in [[wctw-the-oil-market-breaking-point-is-here]]). Rory's path is more bounded than HFI's; Rory is explicit that he's applying linear draw logic and a second SPR release as plausible. Not a contradiction — a calibration difference where Rory is less doomy than HFI.
- **Rory's precautionary-demand overshoot framing** is compatible with [[AdamRozencwajg]]'s "forward curve hasn't repriced" and [[AdiImsirovic]]'s "$30 spread was panic" — three authors saying the *shape* of the initial price spike was an overshoot for specific-market reasons, while the underlying supply balance remains structurally bullish. This is now the wiki's most consistent cross-author framing of the physical-vs-paper divide.
- **Russia sanctions flip-flop:** the Bessent "two days" episode is a specific new data point not previously in the wiki; consistent with existing framings of Trump-admin incoherence.
- **Rory's 1bn-by-May-1 number** is lower than [[HFIResearch]]'s 1.2bn-by-end-April estimate; timing artifact (HFI is 20 April, Rory is 23 April) and modeling-assumption differences — not a substantive disagreement about the direction of the curve.
