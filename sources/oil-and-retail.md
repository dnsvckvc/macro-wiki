## Summary
[[PauloMacro]]'s 21 April 2026 note — the wiki's **most recent source** and the first to think past a Schroedinger's-Strait resolution. Two connected arguments: (1) **The "Big Beautiful Deal" is a bearish-for-risk-assets scenario even if it happens**, because any deal Iran would sign requires ceding control of Hormuz, which [[SaudiArabia]] and the [[UAE]] will not accept — so the GCC monarchies keep the currently shut-in ~3mmbpd Saudi + ~1.6mmbpd UAE in the ground indefinitely rather than subservient to Iran as OPEC price-setter. Either way, shut-in is the outcome; the deal-case is just more permanent. Invokes [[ShrubsRazor]] (the funniest, most absurd outcome is a peace deal that makes crude *rip* on news failure). (2) **The retail oil-short positioning is the "Retail Trader's Guide to Mass Suicide" in action.** [[SCO]] (ProShares 2x UltraShort WTI) has grown from ~$100mn AUM at war's outbreak to >$1bn; [[USO]] (US Oil Fund) has $1.8bn AUM and is short ~10.7mn shares ($1.4bn notional at 7.9% borrow on only ~$1.2mn available at IB, while SCO is 10mn shares at 2.7% borrow). This is Bruce Kovner's "consensus the market is not confirming" in a bottle: retail has pressed $1bn into a structurally broken 2x inverse product with historic vol drag + ~$5/mo negative roll in the most backwardated oil market in history, while shorting the long-oil ETF to the point that unit redemptions can't keep up with borrow demand. Creates convexity on the bull side: weak hands to run over.

## Key Claims

### 1. Even a peace deal is bearish for risk assets
- Any deal Iran will accept requires "handing Iran the grail of controlling the Strait." No adversary willingly relinquishes the instrument of "Never Again" deterrence.
- Iranian control of Hormuz is **structurally unacceptable** to Saudi and the UAE: it replaces OPEC (Saudi-controlled) with Iran as the world's oil price-setter and subordinates the GCC monarchies to Tehran, with tribute extraction implied.
- **Saudi export math (April 2026):** dropped from ~7mmbpd baseline to ~4mmbpd. UAE has shut in ~1.6mmbpd. The GCC is already running the "max exports via Yanbu and Fujairah indefinitely — *and not a drop more*" strategy that the shut-in would formalize.
- **The arithmetic favors shut-in:** `4mmbpd × $150/bbl = $750mn/day` > `7mmbpd × $80/bbl = $560mn/day`. "Not a hard decision actually." Saudi slashes budgets, Project 2030 becomes Project 2050, and the shut-in barrels stay in the ground.
- **The US security umbrella becomes meaningless** if Trump signs such a deal, and the GCC no longer owes Trump lower oil prices. "Better to send crude skyward."

### 2. Shrub's Razor in this setup
Per [[ShrubsRazor]]: the funniest, most absurd outcome is a peace deal signed, crude *ripping* in an epic news-failure as the market re-prices GCC shut-ins from temporary (e.g. until July) to **indefinite**.

- "The markets can look through a perceived temporary energy disruption. They cannot look through an indefinite mass shut-in precipitating a global recession."
- The "Schroedinger's Strait" currently priced is both open and closed. Either resolution is bullish crude / bearish equities:
  - **Strait opens with Iran in control** → GCC keeps several mmbpd in the ground indefinitely → oil moons, markets crater.
  - **Iran drags things out** → several hundred mmbbl of further lost production → US draws inventories aggressively for export → oil rips, markets crater.
- "The odds of a rapid resolution ***and*** Iran giving up power over the Strait are both very low *and also priced in*."

### 3. The retail oil-short absurdity — [[SCO]]
- **SCO** = ProShares UltraShort Bloomberg Crude Oil ETF. Seeks 2x the **inverse daily** performance of the Bloomberg Commodity Balanced WTI Crude Oil Excess Return Index.
- Holdings: cash collateral + **short WTI June26, Dec26, and Jun27 futures.**
- *Absurdity tell:* the ticker reads "Short Crude Oil" but 'CO' is the ticker for Brent futures — yet the product is short *WTI* futures at 2x exposure. Paulo: *"twice as stupid and therefore absurdly hilarious."*
- **AUM has exploded from ~$100mn at war outbreak to over $1bn today.** Retail fund flows have been pressing in since the war broke out.
- **[[VolatilityDrag]] + negative carry:** like the 2x MSTR long/short ETFs of 2024–25, the compounding chop of large alternating daily moves combined with extreme negative carry (~$5/mo roll yield in an historically backwardated curve) has caused the product's price to go nowhere despite a significant correction in flat-price crude.
- Conclusion: *"retail has 'invested' almost $1 billion in a 2x short oil position which… has literally gone nowhere despite a significant correction in the flat price. You are looking at quite literally the dumbest speculative money in the market thinking they are buying the lows, to the tune of a billion dollars."*

### 4. The [[USO]] short-interest asymmetry
- USO (US Oil Fund) — simply long front-month WTI — has **$1.8bn AUM**, down $1bn from its March high.
- As of 31 March 2026, **short interest in USO was 10.7mn shares** = ~$1.4bn notional (i.e., retail is short ~80% of the fund's AUM).
- At Interactive Brokers: **10mn shares of SCO borrow at 2.7%** annualized, but **only 10k shares of USO ($1.2mn)** at **7.9%**.
- Interpretation: retail is so aggressively short USO that **unit redemptions in a futures ETF cannot keep up with short demand** — and is simultaneously pressing SCO long on the 2x inverse side. Borrow-cost asymmetry in opposite directions on two vehicles betting on the same thing (down-oil) is quantitative evidence of extreme one-sided retail positioning against the pros.

### 5. Why this matters — convexity on weak hands
Paulo cites **[[BruceKovner]]**: *"What I am really looking for is a consensus the market is not confirming. I like to know that there are a lot of people who are going to be wrong."*

- "Black belt oil professionals across the board" are not capitulating because "the market is down" — the other side of their view is **leveraged retail making zero money while pressing $1bn into broken oil short products.**
- Echoes the pre-war positioning extremes documented in [[oil-has-turned-a-very-big-corner]]: retail bearishness on oil is now back at post-war Covid-grade stupid-money extremes, *after* a multi-week crude correction from [[WTI]] ~$115 to ~$93.
- Sets up convexity: weak hands getting run over in either resolution path.
