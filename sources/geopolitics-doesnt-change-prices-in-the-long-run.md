## Summary
[[KevinMuir]]'s 12 March 2026 short essay — six days after the Papic podcast ([[my-buddy-marko-broke-the-market]]) — is his **explicit rebuttal** to the over-application of the [[FadeGeopoliticalAngst]] framework to the Iran/US/Israel war. Muir's frame: *most* geopolitical events don't have lasting price impact (Papic is right); but **some events are truly transformative**, and those cannot be faded. He credits the one-liner to *"The Last Libertarian in San Francisco"* (a mystery-guest friend): *"Everyone loves to say that geopolitics doesn't move asset prices, but that's just wrong. Gold over the past four years would have something to say about that!"* The piece then walks through the [[Gold]] 2022–2025 trading as the counterexample: gold initially rallied on the Russia invasion, faded within a month, but the Fed's 2022 tightening cycle (real yields and DXY both soaring) *should* have crushed gold and instead gold diverged massively from the model — the model broke because geopolitics (Russia-reserve confiscation) had fundamentally changed the structural buyer ([[PBOC]] and non-aligned central banks running the [[LedgerToRealWorldThesis]] playbook). This is the piece [[MarkoPapic]] himself references in the podcast — *"the market takes a while to realize the long-term pricing change (see [Muir's] gold piece)"* — and the piece [[PauloMacro]] echoes in [[the-north-star-vs-liquidity]] when he says *"just don't make that forecast because geopolitics never matters."* The pre-war S&P at 7126 may yet be the Iran-war analog of gold at $1,800 in summer 2022 — fade-able on the surface, transformative underneath.

## Key Claims

### 1. The structure of the argument — transformative exception
- **Rule:** most geopolitical events have no long-term price impact. [[FadeGeopoliticalAngst]] is correct most of the time.
- **Exception:** some events are *transformative* — they change the structural drivers of a market, not the flows around the event. Those cannot be faded.
- **Task:** do the analysis to identify which bucket you're in. This is structurally the same as [[MarkoPapic]]'s own "it's a book, not a pamphlet" caveat in [[my-buddy-marko-broke-the-market]] — the framework includes the exception clause.

### 2. The [[Gold]] 2022 case study
- **24 February 2022:** Russia invades Ukraine. Gold initially rallies.
- **March 2022:** Gold hits a high.
- **Summer 2022:** Gold is *down on the year*. On the surface: classic fade, classic confirmation of *"geopolitics doesn't matter."*
- **Real explanation:** Russia was forced to sell gold to pay for goods as it was cut out of the global financial system — an idiosyncratic liquidity flow, not a verdict on gold's fundamentals.
- **The hidden transformation:** The West's decision to confiscate Russia's reserves permanently changed non-aligned central banks' reserve calculus. This is the [[LedgerToRealWorldThesis]].

### 3. Muir's regression model — how he *measured* the break
[[KevinMuir]] maintained a multiple regression of gold vs. (a) 5-year TIPS yield and (b) DXY, trained on 2007 → end-2022 data.
- From 2007 to late 2022 the model explained the majority of gold's variance. Mechanically: rising real yields made non-yielding gold less attractive; rising DXY made dollar-denominated gold relatively expensive.
- **Late 2022 onward:** as the Fed launched the most aggressive tightening cycle in decades (sending both real yields and DXY soaring), the model would have predicted gold to *collapse*. Instead, gold diverged massively upward from the model.
- Gold bulls were telling Muir they'd buy at **$1,250** based on the model output.
- Muir's counsel for the next four years: *"Throw that model out because GEOPOLITICS HAD BROKEN IT."*
- The divergence — as subsequently confirmed — was the [[PBOC]] / [[LedgerToRealWorldThesis]] buyer.

### 4. The implication for the Iran war
- Every broker/asset-manager chart showing "stock returns after geopolitical events" is reproducing some version of the canonical [[MarkoPapic]] chart — including a lot of implicit "don't worry about it" framing.
- **Muir:** "You might believe that the Iran/US/Israel military conflict will subside. I understand that call. **But just don't make that forecast because geopolitics never matters. Gold would like to disagree.**"
- i.e. if you think the Iran war is not transformative, say so explicitly and give the reason — don't just hide behind the average.

### 5. Framing device — "Damn, I wish I said that"
- Kim Mitchell (Canadian rocker, afternoon-drive radio shift) used to introduce great songs with *"Damn, I wish I wrote that."* Muir adapts the phrase to credit *"The Last Libertarian in San Francisco"* for the gold one-liner.
- Self-deprecating acknowledgment that the insight was someone else's, even though Muir has been articulating the underlying thesis since March 2022.
