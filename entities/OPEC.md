# OPEC

The Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries — the oil-producer cartel whose price-setting role is the background condition for every scenario in the wiki's oil thesis. [[SaudiArabia]] is its *de facto* leader; [[UAE]] is a core member; Iran is a member whose strategic autonomy is the crux of the current crisis.

## Relevance in the wiki
- **OPEC spare capacity** ([[oil-has-turned-a-very-big-corner]]): [[PauloMacro]] and [[HFIResearch]] argue real spare capacity is **1–2mmbpd**, not the 3–5mmbpd consensus — 17 of 22 OPEC+ countries achieved peak production *before* Covid. This is foundational to the bull case.
- **OPEC-vs-Iran power shift under a deal** ([[oil-and-retail]]): any peace deal that gives Iran Hormuz effectively **replaces Saudi/OPEC with Iran as the world's oil price-setter.** This is why [[SaudiArabia]] and [[UAE]] would keep the shut-in 3mmbpd + 1.6mmbpd in the ground indefinitely rather than accept such a deal.
