# Michael Howell

Founder of **CrossBorder Capital** and the **GLIndexes** proprietary Global Liquidity Index. Author of *Capital Wars*. Known as the "Liquidity King" for his track record in calling market turns based on liquidity-cycle analysis. The wiki's first source outside the Paulo Macro / Kevin Muir axis.

## Framework
Howell tracks a roughly **65-month (~5.4-year) sine-wave cycle** in global liquidity for advanced economies. His key distinction is **level vs. momentum**: absolute liquidity can remain high enough to support the real economy while the *rate of change* turns negative, which is what drives risk-asset weakness. He aggregates data across dozens of economies into a single Global Liquidity Index.

## Current view (March 2026, per [[the-liquidity-tide-goes-out]])
- The [[GlobalLiquidityCycle]] peaked **Q4 2025 / early 2026** and momentum is now fading.
- Absolute global liquidity ~$188T but growth is slowing.
- In advanced economies, liquidity is becoming *negatively correlated* with business activity — money flowing toward production/capex rather than financial assets.
- Central bank divergence: Fed may ease, but ECB/BoE/BoJ continue balance-sheet shrinkage → net global liquidity tightening.
- Trillions in debt rollovers (2026–2028) absorbing liquidity.
- Risk assets face headwinds; defensive rotation likely.

Howell's [[GlobalLiquidityCycle]] is the macro overlay. Paulo's short-term counter-trend injections (e.g., Bessent's TGA drawdown) occur *within* Howell's broader downswing.
- **Independent timing confirmation:** Howell's cycle peak (Q4 2025 / early 2026) coincides precisely with Paulo's staggered index ATHs (NQ100 Oct 2025), the start of the [[RollingVARShock]] (gold in late January 2026), and the TGA-drain quant quakes of 4Q25.
- **"Shift to real economy"** maps to Paulo's "Can't Print Molecules" and [[KevinMuir]]'s oil-spike macro thesis — the physical world is absorbing liquidity that used to inflate financial assets.

## Quantitative framework: [[DebtLiquidityRatio]] (per [[risks-of-another-financial-crisis-part-1]])
Beyond the cycle, Howell's second major contribution is a structural risk metric: the ratio of total debt to available Global Liquidity. Modern crises are "debt *refinancing* crises" — 70–80% of all primary transactions now refinance existing debt. A **[[WallOfDebt]]** of ~$45T in advanced economies ($65T globally) is coming due by 2030 just as the liquidity cycle turns down. The ratio is projected to breach the 2.0x danger threshold in 2026 and retest the 2.3x (2010 Eurozone crisis peak) by 2030. He has built a Merton distance-to-default analog to track crisis probability — and it's rising.

## 25 April 2026: The Stealth Accord theoretical foundation (per [[treasury-qe-warsh-fed-eclipsed]])
**Howell's eighth wiki appearance** (chronologically *before* the 27 April applied piece below). Three landmark wiki frameworks:
- **[[StealthAccord]]** — the *de facto* 2025/26 regime change that has merged monetary policy and debt management back together (reversing the formal 1951 Accord). *"The Fed is no longer in charge of growth: the US Treasury is."*
- **[[TreasuryQE]]** — the operational mechanism: bill skew (currently 22.2% of total debt) + volatility-targeting buybacks. *"Bill issuance is stealth monetization."*
- **[[CollateralMultiplier]]** — the quantitative core: Global Liquidity ($190T) = Collateral Pool ($150T) × Multiplier (~1.1×); MOVE/multiplier correlation = **−0.67**; **each 10pt fall in MOVE → ~$3T (1.5%)** of additional Global Liquidity. Combined with the 27 April $30bn buyback rule, this gives a ~100× leverage ratio between Treasury buybacks and resulting liquidity creation.
- **The dealer-balance-sheet primary-constraint reframe:** *"The critical issue is not the volume of banks' reserves. It is the capacity of dealers' balance sheets to warehouse Treasuries and intermediate repo flows."* Dealer-banks face Basel III / eSLR caps; *"scarce intermediation capacity co-exists with the Fed's provision of ample reserves."*
- **Warsh constraint sharpened from "increasingly risky" to "no longer possible":** *"Drastically shrinking the Fed's balance sheet… is no longer possible. The system's linchpin has moved from reserves to dealer balance sheet capacity and the MOVE index."*
- **Two structural fragilities:** (1) **moral hazard** — markets expect buybacks, encouraging excessive leverage in [[BasisTrade]]; (2) **collateral fragility** — if MOVE spikes despite buybacks, the multiplier collapses *mechanically*. March 2020 COVID precedent.
