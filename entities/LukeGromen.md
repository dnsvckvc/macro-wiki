# Luke Gromen

Founder of **Forest for the Trees (FFTT)**, a boutique macro research firm focused on identifying developing economic bottlenecks. Known for his decade-long thesis on US fiscal fragility and the dollar system. The wiki's **twelfth author**.

## Unique contributions to the wiki
- **Suez 1956 as base case** (not tail risk) — elevates Michael Every's framing.
- **"One-factor market"** — is Hormuz open or closed? Everything else is secondary.
- **True Interest Expense = 102% of US receipts** through March 2026. The fiscal math is past the breaking point.
- **Net International Investment Position** framework — sovereigns will liquidate dollar assets ($70T gross / $27T net) for food/energy. "Treasury bonds and stocks sit below food and energy on Maslow's hierarchy."
- **Trump's yield bogey is 4.4%**, not 4.6% — every break triggers intervention; Bessent did a $15B single-day Treasury buyback (biggest ever).
- **37% of net UST issuance over 4 years = Cayman Islands hedge funds** (Fed white paper Oct 2025) — foreign demand is actually levered basis-trade, not patient capital.
- **Long-term UST bond futures priced in gold: −90% since 2014** — the one-way macro trade.
- **Supply-chain lag confirmed from his own sources:** Toto stock down 7% on raw-material halt, Fifth Fleet evacuation, CIA office hit specifically at Riyadh embassy.

## Famous axiom
*"Government deficits don't matter until they do. And then they matter a whole lot."*

## Stance relative to wiki
- Short run: cash + bullion (explicitly cites Alden's "too hard" camp on sequencing).
- Long run: energy, uranium, electrical infrastructure, industrials, grocery stores, gold, Bitcoin (later in year via IGV correlation).
- Most alarmed on fiscal/bond dynamics in the wiki; most explicitly synthesizing across multiple other authors.
