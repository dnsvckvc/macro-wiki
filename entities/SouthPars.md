# South Pars

The Iranian portion of the **world's largest gas field** in the Persian Gulf — shared with Qatar, which calls its side "North Field." Together, North Field / South Pars is the single most important LNG-feedstock asset on the planet.

## Strategic significance
- Qatar's North Field produces the crude that feeds the Ras Laffan LNG liquefaction complex — the world's largest, operated under the supervision of Energy Minister [[SaadAlKaabi]].
- Iran's South Pars is Iran's primary domestic gas-supply source and a major source of petrochemical feedstock.
- **The two sides are geologically connected.** Damage to production on one side can contaminate the gas cap and affect the other side's wells. This geological coupling is part of what makes reciprocal strikes so destructive and why [[MarkoPapic]] treats the 30 March 2026 reciprocal strikes as the defining [[MutualAssuredDestructionEnergy]] moment.

## The 30 March 2026 strike
- Israel struck South Pars; Iran struck Qatari LNG infrastructure (Ras Laffan and related) in reciprocity.
- Exchange crystallized the MAD dynamic: ~30% of global LNG and ~20% of global crude can go offline in a single 24-hour reciprocal exchange.
- [[PauloMacro]], [[MarkoPapic]], [[JimBianco]], and [[RoryJohnston]] all converge on this episode as the upper bound of realistic kinetic escalation during the April 2026 phase of the war.
