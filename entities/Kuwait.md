# Kuwait

OPEC founding member and GCC producer. Like [[Iraq]], Kuwait's export infrastructure is structurally Hormuz-dependent — it operates the Mina al-Ahmadi and Mina Abdullah terminals on the Gulf, with no bypass route to the Red Sea or Indian Ocean.

## April 2026 position (per [[wctw-the-oil-market-breaking-point]])
- **~2.35mmbpd of production shut in** as of 26 March 2026 — second-largest absolute shut-in after [[Iraq]].
- [[HFIResearch]] groups Kuwait with Iraq as a structurally slow-restart producer: *"countries like Iraq and Kuwait will take 3-4 months to bring production back to normal."*

## Why Kuwait compounds the forward-balance problem
- No bypass infrastructure; no alternative outlet.
- Restart lag of 3–4 months means Kuwaiti barrels would not return to the forward curve until well into Q3 2026 even under the "ends this week" scenario.
- The combined Iraq + Kuwait offline production is ~6mmbpd — roughly **two-thirds of the world's pre-war effective OPEC+ spare capacity**, under the 1–2mmbpd HFI-audited spare number ([[OPEC]]).

## Role in the Nash game
- Kuwait is a tacit partner in the GCC's Nash-2 stance described by [[PauloMacro]] in [[oil-and-retail]]: any deal that hands Iran Hormuz replaces Saudi/OPEC pricing with Iran pricing, which Kuwait finds as structurally unacceptable as Saudi and UAE. Budgetarily, Kuwait's sovereign wealth cushion and fiscal breakeven make it more tolerant of extended shut-in than Iraq.
