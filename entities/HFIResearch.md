# HFI Research

Independent oil research shop. Previously cited in the wiki only as a data source behind [[PauloMacro]]'s analysis; since [[wctw-the-oil-market-breaking-point]] (26 March 2026), HFI is a **first-class wiki author (fourteenth)** writing in its own voice via the *What Caught The Wire* (WCTW) column. Uses Kpler satellite/shipping data. Publishes scenario frameworks with explicit numerical ranges and price path forecasts.

## The analytical identity
- **Physical-market-first.** Every HFI claim is grounded in Kpler tanker flows, OPEC production audits, and onshore inventory builds. No macro-narrative speculation untethered to physical barrels.
- **Scenario-based forecasting.** HFI publishes price paths as functions of binary or trinary resolution dates, not point estimates. The 9 March piece (cited inside [[wctw-the-oil-market-breaking-point]]) enumerated four scenarios tied to tanker-flow resumption dates; the 26 March piece enumerates three scenarios tied to war-resolution dates.
- **Trade disclosure.** HFI publishes its book. Currently long USO May 15, 2026 $215-$245 call spread + USO May 15, 2026 $135 call — an aggressively upside-skewed long-oil position consistent with the breaking-point thesis.

## Referenced in the wiki for

### Spare-capacity + inventory audit ([[oil-has-turned-a-very-big-corner]])
In Paulo's January 2026 pre-war note, HFI is cited for:
- **Global onshore crude inventory analysis** — most of the floating barrels land in China; ex-China global onshore stocks only up ~70mmbbl YoY despite the IEA's surplus narrative.
- **OPEC+ spare capacity audit** (December 2025) — 17 of 22 OPEC+ countries achieved peak production *before* Covid; only Gabon, Libya, UAE, and Kazakhstan are producing above their 2013–25 peaks. Real spare capacity is far less than the consensus 3–5mmbpd; HFI estimates Saudi peaks around 11mmbpd. This audit is central to Paulo's argument that the market is sleeping on a structural supply shortfall.

### The Breaking Point thesis ([[wctw-the-oil-market-breaking-point]], 26 March 2026)
HFI's 26 March WCTW piece is the wiki's canonical statement of the [[OilMarketBreakingPoint]] concept. Key contributions:
- **Precise shut-in production numbers as of 26 March:** [[Iraq]] −3.60, [[Kuwait]] −2.35, [[SaudiArabia]] −3.05, [[UAE]] −1.80, Bahrain −0.18 = **10.98mmbpd total.**
- **Saudi Red Sea export arithmetic:** East-West pipeline maxed at ~4mmbpd via Yanbu.
- **UAE bypass arithmetic:** Habshan–Fujairah pipeline maxed at ~1.8mmbpd.
- **Three scenarios incorporating the 400mmbbl SPR:**
  - Ends this week → −50mmbbl net → Brent mid-to-high $80s avg.
  - Ends mid-April → −210mmbbl → Brent mid-to-high $90s avg.
  - Ends end-April → −370mmbbl → **Brent $110–$120 avg.**
- **The breaking point:** if not resolved by mid-April, IEA needs another ~400mmbbl SPR release or oil goes to demand-destruction ($200+).
- **[[EnergyAspects]] upside acknowledgment:** 930mmbbl total / 340mmbbl cumulative May-December losses; HFI explicitly notes their own analysis is too conservative because it missed the 3-4 month restart lag for Iraq/Kuwait.
- **Independent Nash derivation:** *"It's up to the Iranians, and given this being the case, it will push for the breaking point in the oil market… All it needs to do is 'survive' for another 3 weeks before things start to break."* Third author after [[PauloMacro]] and [[MarkoPapic]] to arrive independently at the same game-theoretic conclusion, via pure oil-market math.

### Post-breaking-point follow-up ([[wctw-the-oil-market-breaking-point-is-here]], 20 April 2026)
HFI's public follow-up declaring the mid-April threshold crossed. Key updates:
- **11–13mmbpd total supply outage** now has to manifest as crude draws, product draws, or demand destruction. No fourth option.
- **Cumulative loss curve:** ~1bn bbls by 25 March → ~1.2bn end-April → ~1.59bn end-May → ~1.98bn end-June.
- **US commercial crude storage** tracking to ~400mmbbl by end-July, approaching operational minimum (370–380mmbbl), **including ~139mmbbl cumulative US SPR release** — HFI's specific number puts a floor under Paulo's salt-cavern-structural-limit argument.
- **Export-ban cascade forecast:** Trump admin will ban product exports first, crude exports second if margins compress — *"disastrous for US shale and Canadian oil producers."*
- **IRGC gunfire observation (18–19 April):** first mass tanker turnaround since HFI began tracking. HFI *explicitly walks back his own earlier insurance-closure attribution* (originally sympathetic to [[DrAnasAlHajji]]'s thesis) in favor of direct IRGC physical enforcement. *"I was shocked… the worst has yet to come."*
- **First HFI citation of [[PauloMacro]]:** HFI endorses Paulo's recommendation of [[RobertPape]] and specifically Pape's *Why the Ceasefire Keeps Failing.* Flow of reference is now bidirectional — previously Paulo cited HFI for data, now HFI cites Paulo's frameworks.
- **Trade book pressed harder:** added USO May $135 calls, USO June $125 calls, and initiated BNO July $50 and $60 calls (first Brent-proxy exposure). Consistent with conviction update post-breaking-point.
- **Price regime:** HFI *withdraws his own scenario-3 price target* ($110–$120 for end-April closure). *"We cannot provide an accurate oil price forecast. We will have crossed too deep into the Rubicon… no price for outright shortages."* Demand destruction at COVID-lockdown magnitude now the only rebalancing mechanism.

## Why HFI matters to the wiki's composite thesis
- Provides the numerical backbone for Paulo's qualitative claims. Paulo cites HFI for spare capacity and onshore inventories; HFI's Breaking Point concept and scenario arithmetic give Paulo's Nash/Turducken/Tom-Hanks frameworks an actionable date.
- Provides an **ex-ante date-specific forecast** (mid-April breaking point) that is directly testable — unlike most macro calls in the wiki.
- Provides the GCC-incumbency read that [[PauloMacro]] later quantifies in [[oil-and-retail]]: HFI says the Gulf countries cannot accept an Iranian toll-fee framework going forward; Paulo says the same via `4mmbpd × $150 > 7mmbpd × $80` arithmetic.
