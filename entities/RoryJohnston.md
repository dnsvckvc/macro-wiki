# Rory Johnston

Founder of **Commodity Context** (commoditycontext.com). Oil-market analyst focused on fundamental supply/demand with monthly data reports on OPEC global balances and North American detailed balances. Publishes the weekly *Oil Context Weekly* (currently renamed *Oil and Iran War Context Weekly*). Host of the **Oil Ground Up** podcast (Clear Commodity Network / Oil & Gas Global Network). The wiki's **tenth author**, and in his host capacity, brings [[AdiImsirovic]] (fifteenth author) into the corpus via [[oilgroundup-adi-imsirovic]].

## Unique contributions to the wiki
- **The Venezuela / Delcy Rodriguez template** that the Trump administration expected to replay in Iran. Trump's playbook: massive military buildup → quick regime flip → declare victory. Worked for Venezuela (Maduro kidnapped Saturday, Delcy Rodriguez interim-president-making-a-deal by Monday). Failed for Iran because Iranian political culture is different. "Arguably the biggest miscalculation."
- **Trump admin had no plan:** IEA SPR release 2 weeks late; marine insurance facility improvised; no advance SPR refill.
- **Historical contrast with the 1980s tanker wars** (450 ships attacked, 55 sunk, flow never halted) vs. now (90-95% transit reduction).
- **Ships aren't moving because traders expect Trump to TACO tomorrow,** not just because of insurance (5% premium, but arbs easily cover).
- **3 months minimum to renormalize** even if resolved today; 340-400mmbbl air pocket yet to hit land in Asia.
- **Trump goal-shifting** on oil prices — losing feedback sensitivity.
- **Export-controls risk** — Jones Act waiver as precursor.

## Self-disclosed initial bias
Before the war: "relatively Pollyanna-ish" — believed the Strait closure was unlikely, like Al-Hajji. Now describes it as the biggest oil event he's ever analyzed. "This will make the 1970s look like child's play."

## Second appearance (16 April, [[macrovoices-528-rory-johnston]]) — key updates
- **Transit reality:** 7 ships/day, high of 16 on Apr 12 (vs. pre-crisis 120-140). **Zero non-Iranian VLCCs since Saturday.** Iran is actively filtering who gets through.
- **Policy pivot on Iranian oil:** US shifted from allowing flow (weeks 1-5, like the 2022 Russia price cap) to **maximum-pressure blockade**. Adds 2mmbpd to shut-in total (13 → 15mmbpd).
- **Air pocket arrival by region:** East Africa w3, East Asia w4, Europe w5, **North America w6 (now)**.
- **Unprecedented spreads:** Brent futures $100 vs. Dated Brent $132 — $32 spread, never seen even in COVID. Physical delivered premia ~$20 on top. HSBC CEO: $286/bbl cargo to Sri Lanka.
- **Bab el-Mandeb threat:** closure would force Cape of Good Hope detour (+2 weeks).
- **US Gulf Coast = global swing supplier** (China blocked exports). Tankers inbound because others pay more.

## Third appearance: Geopolitical Cousins (per [[geopolitical-cousins-rory-johnston]], ~23 April 2026)
Rory's most detailed forward-price forecast in the wiki:
- **Dec 2026 Brent** path conditional on Hormuz resolution date:
  - Reopens May 1: drops $20/bbl in one day, grinds higher to **$95-$100 by December** unless second IEA SPR release or recession
  - Still closed June 1: **$130-$150**
  - Still closed July 1: **~$195-$200**
- **Steel-mans the sanguine market** via his same-day Commodity Context piece: the $144 Dated Brent spike was most likely a [[PrecautionaryDemand]] overshoot; that peak *pulled forward* future demand, so the subsequent sell-off is now "confirming" the bearish narrative on the downside even though fundamentals continue to deteriorate.
- **Self-reframes as former perma-bear:** *"Everyone thinks of me now as this hair-on-fire permabull. My reputation in the oil market prior to this was that of a perma-bear."* His current forecasts are a data-updated extension of the pre-war bearish view, not a positional flip.
- **TACO paradox:** *"I actually think I've always thought this was going to end with taco. But every time [Trump] does, the market sells off and it reduces the pressure on him to finish the war."*
- **Trump's leverage decay > Iran's leverage decay:** core prior. Rory's disagreement with Papic inside the same conversation on who blinks first.
- **Rory's tanker map / Trump retweet incident:** Trump re-posted Rory's map of inbound VLCCs to the US, which Papic argues has "changed the course of world history" by convincing Trump he has time.
- **April 9 trader washout:** Rory and Papic both identify this as the key $15-$20 single-day drop event that blew out the most willing physical longs — residual survivors are compositionally more cautious ("sanguine" market as selection bias).
- **Russia-Ukraine pro-cyclicality:** Ukrainian attacks on Russian oil infrastructure are increasing with higher oil prices; mid-March saw 40% export-capacity reduction (largest since USSR collapse). Self-reinforcing upward cascade.

## Oil Ground Up host role (per [[oilgroundup-adi-imsirovic]])
Johnston's Oil Ground Up interview with [[AdiImsirovic]] brings the wiki's first dedicated physical-market-microstructure specialist into the corpus. Johnston's own contributions in that conversation:
- **Calibration disagreement with Imsirovic on post-war flat price:** Rory pushes back on Imsirovic's $50-$60 end-of-summer forecast (*"I would be surprised to see $50 crude now, given that even already we've lost 450 million barrels of Gulf supply"*).
- **Self-developed split-regression framework for term structure:** back-of-curve flat price → inventory level (revealed-scarcity channel); front-minus-back spread → supply/demand flow balance (theory-of-storage channel). Imsirovic endorses as "got the whole thing."
- **Northwest Europe refinery stress observation:** many refineries running negative net margins after hedging futures but paying dated+quality premium on physical crude. If persistent → run cuts → product scarcity → [[BrickAndMortarTrade]] cycle accelerates.
