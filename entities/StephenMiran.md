# Stephen Miran

Chair of the Council of Economic Advisers under the Trump administration. Co-author of a Fed research paper (*"A User's Guide to Reducing the Federal Reserve's Balance Sheet,"* Fed working paper 2026019) proposing to cut the Fed's balance sheet by $1.2–2.1T (mid-point $1.7T).

## Referenced in the wiki for
[[MichaelHowell]] in [[could-fed-liquidity-plunge-by-half]] argues Miran's proposal would cut Fed Liquidity to ~$1.5T (roughly half recent levels), which the historically tight S&P 500 / Fed Liquidity relationship suggests would "plainly spell disaster for asset prices." Howell calls the paper "a technical ideal rooted in nostalgia for a smaller Fed footprint" that ignores the coordination problem with the Treasury, private-sector demand volatility, and the inelasticity of the reserve demand curve at low levels.

Miran's proposal aligns with [[KevinWarsh]]'s agenda to reduce the Fed's footprint — both flagged by Howell as "increasingly risky" given the structural fragility of the Treasury market and the approaching [[WallOfDebt]].
