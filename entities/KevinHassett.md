# Kevin Hassett

US economic policy figure in the Trump administration; frequent public spokesperson on inflation, rates, and oil prices.

## Referenced in the wiki for
Per [[RoryJohnston]] in [[oilgroundup-adi-imsirovic]], Hassett has publicly used the Brent futures curve as a flat-price forecast — e.g., *"the futures curve says oil will be $70 a barrel by next summer."*

[[AdiImsirovic]]'s pushback: the futures curve is not a flat-price forecast. It encodes the *seasonal storage incentives* necessary to carry inventory across time (winter-vs-summer distillate/gasoline/jet). Mistaking it for a forecast is a common policymaker error and leads to poor framing of the post-crisis price path.

## Why this matters
- The administration's rhetoric about future oil prices is shaped by this misreading.
- When a policymaker quotes "the futures curve says X," market participants should read it as a framing statement, not a substantive forecast — both because of the storage-theoretic misreading *and* because Trump's jawboning ([[NachoTaco]] / [[TrumpPumps]]) has had a more direct effect on spot price via $15-$20/bbl tweet-driven reversals than any administration rate-path guidance.
