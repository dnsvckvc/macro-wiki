# Dr. Anas Al-Hajji

Founder and managing partner of **Energy Outlook Advisors**. Middle East energy expert with deep operational contacts across the Gulf states, OPEC, and the broader energy intelligence community. The wiki's **seventh author** and the only dedicated geopolitical-energy-intelligence specialist — providing the on-the-ground operational view that complements the financial/trading perspectives of the other authors.

## Unique contributions
- **The insurance-closure thesis:** argues the [[StraitOfHormuz]] may have been closed by the US (via insurance companies making coverage prohibitive) rather than by Iran. Internally consistent with Trump's own claims that Iran's navy and radars are destroyed. *"To believe Iran closed the Strait is a bigger conspiracy theory."*
- **China knew in advance:** stopped importing US LNG/oil and built massive inventories months before the war. Al-Hajji wrote about this in June/July 2025.
- **National Security Strategy (November 2025)** — four months before the war, used language implying the Strait's closure was anticipated.
- **The shortage math:** after all mitigation, still short 10-12 million bpd; residual ~8mmbpd after demand destruction. Demand destruction at **Brent ~$160**. Global recession/stagflation by May.
- **Price differentials > price levels:** Asia paying $170+ while US at ~$90. The US is now the lowest-cost energy competitor — exactly per the national security strategy.
- **Post-crisis doctrine:** every country will link energy to national security (not economics). *"Trump unwittingly gave the climate change people the biggest gift of their lives."*
