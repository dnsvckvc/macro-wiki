# Ilya Bouchouev

Options-and-commodities trader, academic, and author of works on oil options trading. Described by [[AdiImsirovic]] as *"a good friend… great book… excellent teacher."* Referenced in the wiki primarily as Imsirovic's intellectual counterpart on theory-of-storage debates.

## The backwardation-as-natural-state position
Per Imsirovic in [[oilgroundup-adi-imsirovic]], Bouchouev holds the **Keynesian normal-backwardation** view:
- Backwardation is the natural state of commodity futures curves.
- Producers have a stronger hedging demand than end-users, selling forward production at a discount to expected spot → systematic producer-selling pressure keeps futures below expected spot → backwardation is equilibrium.
- This is structurally different from Imsirovic's classical [[TheoryOfStorage]] position ("contango is natural; backwardation is 'wrong way round'").

## Why the debate matters
- Both views are internally consistent and make the same empirical prediction for the April 2026 crisis: the current backwardation is historically extreme and driven by physical scarcity.
- Bouchouev's view implies the baseline oil market is *always* somewhat backwardated; Imsirovic's view implies contango is the null.
- The debate has not been definitively settled in the academic commodity-futures literature.
