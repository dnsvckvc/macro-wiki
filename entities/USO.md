# USO — United States Oil Fund

Long front-month WTI crude oil ETF. The most widely known retail vehicle for direct crude exposure.

## April 2026 state
Per [[oil-and-retail]]:
- **AUM $1.8bn.** Down **~$1bn from its March high** (i.e., the war-era run-up had pushed USO's AUM past ~$2.8bn).
- **Short interest as of 31 March 2026: 10.7 million shares = ~$1.4bn notional.** This is 80%+ of fund AUM sitting as short interest — retail is simultaneously using SCO to leverage-short oil *and* shorting the long-oil ETF outright.
- **Borrow asymmetry at Interactive Brokers:** only 10,000 shares (~$1.2mn) available to borrow, at **7.9% annualized**. Compare to [[SCO]] where 10 million shares are available at 2.7%.
- Implication: *"unit redemptions in a futures ETF are having trouble keeping up with short demand"* — a structurally strange condition because an ETF can in principle mint/redeem creation units freely. The gap between redemption pace and borrow demand tells you how crowded the short trade is.

## Context — historical echo
USO was a centerpiece of the April 2020 [[CovidOilCrash2020]] episode: long front-month WTI in deep [[Supercontango]] forced the fund to restructure exposure across multiple contracts as WTI went negative. In 2026, the product is on the other side of the same commodity-ETF vol: front-month USO is *underperforming* long crude thanks to physical-market backwardation and the roll convention; that underperformance (plus retail press into [[SCO]]) has helped drain assets.

## Why it matters
Combined with [[SCO]]'s $1bn+ of retail length, the USO short-interest build is [[PauloMacro]]'s headline "Max Stupid" [[BruceKovner]]-framework read on contemporary oil positioning: retail is pressing both sides of the short-crude trade in vehicles that each have structurally negative expected value against the current curve. This is the wiki's quantitative contemporary echo of the Lehman/Covid-extreme positioning documented pre-war in [[oil-has-turned-a-very-big-corner]].
