# Ole Hansen

Head of Commodity Strategy at [[SaxoBank]] for 18 years. **Sixteenth author in the wiki**; first source to bring an explicit *passive-allocator* / *cross-commodity* lens to the Iran/energy regime — covering crude, refined products, fertilizer, agriculture, base metals (copper), precious metals (gold), and softs (cocoa, cotton, sugar) in a single integrated framework.

Wiki-defining contribution from his MacroVoices #529 appearance ([[macrovoices-529-ole-hansen]]):

- **The slide-4 Bloomberg Spot vs. Total Return decomposition** — the wiki's clearest quantitative case for [[RollYield]] as a portfolio driver. 2016–2021 (mostly contango): spot +52% / TR +14%. 2021–2026 (backwardation regime): spot +57% / TR +83%.
- **Brent floor moves from $70 to $80** structurally; "two to three months from a peace deal" before normalization can begin.
- **The third wave of the [[CommoditySupercycle]] = energy transition.** Currently 5 years in; possibly halfway, possibly longer; supply-side response capacity is the binding constraint.
- **Agriculture as the next leg** (2H 2026 → 2027) following metals (last year) and energy (early this year). Fertilizer-disruption asymmetry hits nitrogen-intensive crops (wheat, corn) hardest, soybeans least.

Publishes daily on home.saxo.com/insights, on X as `@ole__hansen` (note the double underscore), and on Substack. Eric Townsend describes him as *"one of the most insightful people in the commodity market"* and credits him with publishing what is *"the best analysis there is of the commitment of traders reports."*
