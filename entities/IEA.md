# International Energy Agency (IEA)

Paris-based intergovernmental energy forecaster founded in 1974 following the 1973 oil shock. Publishes the monthly *Oil Market Report* and coordinates Strategic Petroleum Reserve releases among member countries in supply emergencies.

## Role in the April 2026 crisis
- **Coordinated the 400mmbbl global [[SPRRelease]]** in early March 2026 after the closure of the [[StraitOfHormuz]] — the emergency action that tamped [[WTI]] back from $115 to ~$93.
- Per [[HFIResearch]] in [[wctw-the-oil-market-breaking-point]]: if the conflict is not resolved by mid-April, the IEA will need to **coordinate a second ~400mmbbl release** to avoid demand-destruction-level prices ($200+). No such second release has been announced as of 23 April 2026.

## Pre-war credibility problem
Per [[PauloMacro]] in [[oil-has-turned-a-very-big-corner]], [[AdamRozencwajg]] in [[macrovoices-527-adam-rozencwajg]], and [[OleHansen]] in [[macrovoices-529-ole-hansen]]:
- The IEA had forecast a **~4mmbpd surplus** going into 2026 based on overstated assumptions about OPEC spare capacity and understated demand.
- Rozencwajg: *"If there had been 2-3mmbpd of surplus, inventories should have built by ~720mmbbl. They barely budged."* The surplus was fiction.
- Hansen: *"The biggest supply glut in living memory as touted by the IEA, which potentially was too high compared to where the market was actually signaling."*
- The pre-war IEA narrative is part of the reason consensus was positioned at Lehman/Covid-grade bearish on oil right before the structural bull case printed.
