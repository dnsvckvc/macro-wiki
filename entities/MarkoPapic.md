# Marko Papic

Chief Geopolitical Strategist at **[[BCAResearch]]**. Canadian; based in Los Angeles. Author of **[[GeopoliticalAlpha]]** (2020). Joined BCA in 2011 to start the firm's geopolitical service (now run by Matt Kirk, while Papic runs the "geo-macro" synthesis desk). Co-hosts the **Geopolitical Cousins** podcast with Jacob Shapiro.

## Framework — [[GeopoliticalAlpha]] / [[FadeGeopoliticalAngst]]
Papic's research argues geopolitical events rarely have lasting impact on markets because (1) the market always overstates risk, trading "what's possible" instead of "what's probable"; (2) the event itself gives policymakers an excuse to pivot/stimulate. But the framework requires active analysis of **material constraints on policymakers** — it is not a mechanical fade. Papic coined the term *"geo-macro"* to describe the synthesis.

Papic's client joke about himself: *"My boss told me that you would fade your own death."* Papic response: *"I'm the Norman Angell of our era"* — accepting the parallel to [[NormanAngell]]'s *[[TheGreatIllusion]]* (1909) as a warning that frameworks can become dangerous once the market adopts them reflexively.

## Pre-2026 track record
- Since 7 October 2023 (Hamas attack), Papic was fading the Middle East consistently — including multiple 2024 and 2025 rocket/escalation episodes.
- Told clients: *"Two countries can nuke each other and it won't affect the rest of the world at all."*
- Key reasoning: the *fulcrum* of Middle East market impact is [[StraitOfHormuz]]. Israel-Iran missile exchanges don't matter unless Hormuz is threatened.
- This stance was correct across all prior episodes and reinforced the market's adoption of the "fade" reflex.

## **The 6 January 2026 long-Brent pivot** (per [[my-buddy-marko-broke-the-market]])
Papic's first "don't fade" call on the Middle East since 2023. On 6 January 2026 he told BCA clients to go **long Brent.** Triggers — all material-constraint changes:
1. **US used the "R-word": regime change.** Iran's regime now existentially threatened.
2. **Gulf Arab states have plausible deniability** for wanting the regime gone — Iran can no longer escalate against only Israel.
3. **Internal Iranian discord in December 2025** weakened regime cohesion.
4. **Technological asymmetry:** Iran can't defend its skies, but can impact shipping with drones (Houthi Red Sea precedent with "even less technology").

**Market-positioning reinforcement:** Papic flags that oil was extremely one-sidedly short going into January 2026 — retail and hedge-fund commodity desks all positioned for "oversupply / supply glove / economic fundamentals don't support long oil." Papic: *"Unless you expect a global recession, how's oil going to go to 30? Saudi Arabia will just cut production. There is kind of a floor. So then what's the harm in being long?"* Independent convergence with [[PauloMacro]]'s 28 January [[oil-has-turned-a-very-big-corner]] thesis — same trade, same timing, arrived via different analytical paths.

## Independent derivation of [[NorthStar]] game theory
Recorded 6 March 2026 — one day after Paulo's [[tom-hanks-has-covid]] and before Paulo's [[make-1973-great-again]] (8 March):

> *"If I was running Iran, I would not eat the taco. I would say I need to update the utility function of the West so they don't attempt this nonsense for the next 50 years. I need to remind them because apparently they have forgotten why Strait of Hormuz matters and why."*

This is **structurally identical to Paulo's "Never Again" / Pearl-Harbor framing** of Iran's [[NashEquilibrium]] — arrived at independently. Papic's abstraction ("update the utility function of the West") is more first-principles than Paulo's Pearl-Harbor analogy; the two framings converge on the same trade.

Papic also names Trump's [[NachoTaco]] pattern with sharper structure: Trump doesn't "chicken out" — he *"lands the plane. The deal doesn't get him to the moon, but he gets to go to the top of his house."* But this game has a counterparty: *"In every previous situation where President's delicious taco was eaten whole by his counterparts — in this particular case the Islamic Republic might say 'take your taco and shove it exactly where you should shove it.'"*

## Scoreboard through 20 April 2026

### Vindicated
- **Long Brent from 6 January** — oil rallied hard; this trade has been a core wiki reference point ever since.
- **Iran won't eat the taco** — no deal has occurred that reopens Hormuz; the 7 April two-week ceasefire did not change the underlying physical situation.
- **[[FadeGeopoliticalAngst]] / [[GeopoliticalAlpha]] on equities** — the load-bearing meta-call. S&P closed pre-war 27 Feb at 6879; closed 17 April at **7126, above pre-war**. Nasdaq 13 up days. [[KevinMuir]]'s own bull-pillar #1 in [[outlining-the-bull-and-bear-arguments]] concedes: *"the buy the geopolitical angst trade worked in spades… just a little late in kicking in."* This is the framework's biggest empirical win of the cycle.

### Missed
- **"Mere days" Hormuz duration call.** Papic expected Iran's drone closure to last 5-6-7 days before Iran folds under US military pressure ("back to the Abbasid Caliphate"). Actual closure is 50+ days and counting; US pivoted to sanctions + Navy blockade rather than a regime-destruction campaign. [[JimBianco]]'s Mosaic Doctrine in [[macrovoices-527-jim-bianco]] operationalizes why Iran can sustain pressure: standing orders across distributed drone/missile sectors make the closure self-sustaining under partial degradation.
- **"Take profits on half"** of the Brent long at 6 March was premature — crude continued higher.

### Net
Papic got the framework-level call correct (this war does not have lasting equity impact) and missed on the tactical Hormuz duration. [[PauloMacro]] inverts that scoreboard — correct on Hormuz duration, not yet vindicated on the equity crash leg of his [[NorthStar]] thesis. The two authors agree on the *game theory* of Iran's [[NashEquilibrium]] but disagree on whether that game theory flows through to an equity crash — and through 20 April, the market has priced Papic's side of that divergence.

## Views on the market (March 2026)
- **The market is too efficient at fading geopolitics.** Concedes Muir's thesis: the pendulum has swung too far toward "nothing ever happens" because retail and many traders treat [[GeopoliticalAlpha]] as a hack rather than a framework.
- **Macro backdrop is more pernicious than summer 2024** — *"a lot of cockroaches in the private space that nobody really knows where they're going to end up."* Echoes Paulo's credit-cycle-already-turning thesis from [[intensely-concerned-for-risk-with-a-catch]].
- **Imagined White House dialog:** Hegseth/Trump argue the market is fine; Bessent pushes back that the underlying factors (labor market, oil, inflation) are "getting red hot even if that front burner of the stock market is not flashing red." Prefigures the real-world analysis Paulo and Muir later document in detail.

## Geopolitical Cousins views (per [[geopolitical-cousins-rory-johnston]], ~23 April 2026)
Papic's most recent wiki appearance, co-hosting with [[JacobShapiro]] and guest [[RoryJohnston]]. Papic's updated positioning:
- **Regime change is off the table.** *"When was the last time we uttered the words regime change? Jacob, you were 100 percent right. I'm on an exact same path as you guys are, except now they got exactly what closing Hormuz was intended to do, which is illustrate to the United States of America, you can't do regime change in Iran. And that is no longer a goal."*
- **Coined the [[MutualAssuredDestructionEnergy]] framework.** The **30 March 2026** Israeli strike on [[SouthPars]] plus Iran's reciprocal strike on Qatari LNG ([[SaadAlKaabi]]'s Ras Laffan) showed that ~30% of global LNG and ~20% of global crude could go offline in a 24-hour exchange — which now precludes kinetic re-escalation on both sides.
- **"Canisters and cannibalism"** — Papic's coinage for the demand-destruction endpoint; the point at which *every major country on Earth* would line up against Iran regardless of who started the war. Also the line Iran dare not cross because doing so destroys Iran's diplomatic leverage structurally.
- **Iran has won; time to cash in.** Papic's core argument: Iran has achieved the deterrent it set out to achieve. Holding Hormuz closed much longer becomes a "wasting strategic asset" — the longer it stays closed, the more the world builds structural workarounds (China/rare-earths analogy; new GCC pipelines being announced).
- **Trump has already blinked.** Papic disagrees with Rory (and Shapiro, who sides with Rory) on who has more leverage. Papic cites Trump's April climb-down from "I will not extend the ceasefire" (Thursday) to *"oh, I was just kidding"* (Friday) as the operative blink.
- **Papic's Brent call is more aggressive than Rory's:** $150-$170 by mid-May if unresolved. Rory's data-grounded path is $130-$150 by June 1 — the conversation makes explicit that Papic is *less linear and more doomy* on the time horizon than Rory.
- **Praise for Johnston's prior work:** *"I've always enjoyed your presence on social media. I think you're really thoughtful."* Papic also takes credit/responsibility for the Rory-map-Trump-retweet incident.
