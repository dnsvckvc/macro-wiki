# Christopher Waller

Federal Reserve Board of Governors member; appointed in late 2020. One of the most dovish FOMC voices through 2024–2025 — repeatedly dissenting in favor of rate cuts.

## March 17–18, 2026 FOMC inflection

At the March 17–18 FOMC meeting (held three weeks into the Iran/US/Israel war), Waller — along with [[MichelleBowman]] — **withdrew his dissent** on the unchanged-policy decision. Two of the three previously-dovish dissents disappeared in a single meeting. A follow-up Waller speech shortly after the meeting confirmed the Fed was on hold.

Per [[KevinMuir]] in [[why-did-the-front-end-get-hammered]]:
> *"On March 17th and 18th, there was a two-day FOMC meeting. At the time, I said it was an impossible meeting for Powell to stick handle through, and that proved a correct take. The FOMC meeting largely affirmed the hawkish front-end tightening. Most importantly, Waller and Bowman (two of the three previous doves that had dissented on the unchanged monetary policy decision), rescinded their dissents, in essence, affirming the implied tightening. If there was any doubt, a speech given by Waller shortly thereafter, confirmed the Fed was on hold."*

## Why this matters

The withdrawals matter because they removed the most plausible institutional pushback against the hawkish front-end repricing the market had already executed. Front-end markets had already priced out all three 2026 cuts and added a small hike chance — a Fed dove pushing back at the meeting could have softened that. Instead, the doves capitulated, confirming the market's hawkish read.

The 30 March 2026 SPX low (per [[making-sense-of-the-last-two-months]]) is partly attributable to this hawkish FOMC, layered on top of the [[JPMCollarTrade]] roll mechanics.
