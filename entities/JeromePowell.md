# Jerome Powell

Chair of the Federal Reserve. His term as **chair** expires **15 May 2026**, though he retains the option to remain on the board of governors.

## Relevance in the wiki
- Per [[PauloMacro]] in [[the-anatomy-of-a-crash]], the Powell lame-duck window is a systemic risk factor in the March–April 2026 crash setup: the Fed is "of two minds" on the oil crisis (per [[NeelKashkari]]), Powell doesn't leave until May, [[KevinWarsh]] (Trump's rumored successor pick) has not been confirmed, and the entire institution is in multi-month "institutional inertia" just as the market most needs a decisive backstop. Paulo: *"How does the Fed print oil?"*
- Per [[LynAlden]] in [[macrovoices-525-lyn-alden]], Warsh's confirmation is **"on hold pending criminal investigation of Jay Powell"** — a striking additional variable. Powell's recent comments suggest he may increase his odds of staying on the board (not as chair). Alden argues the war has *narrowed* the Powell-vs-Warsh difference anyway: no credible Fed chair can cut into rising oil inflation.
