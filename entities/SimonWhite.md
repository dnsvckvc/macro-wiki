# Simon White

Macro strategist at **Bloomberg**. Writes the *Macroscope* column on the terminal (Tuesdays and Thursdays) and the *MarketsLive* blog. The wiki's **eleventh author**.

## Key contributions to the wiki
- **The three-act inflation play** (1970s analog). Act 1 first high = pandemic. Act 2 premature all-clear = last 2 years. Act 3 comeback-of-the-vengeance = starting now with Iran/Yom-Kippur. Timing match "almost to the month."
- **Inflation is the most mispriced thing in markets.** CPI swaps expect 3.5% peak → 2.8% within 12 months. 10Y breakevens barely moved. "Team transitory is back in force."
- **Food shock beats energy shock in CPI-weighted impact.** Fertilizer proxy leads food CPI by ~6 months. Concrete mechanism confirming the wiki's Wheat thesis.
- **Kevin Warsh = Arthur Burns, not Paul Volcker.** Dovish central banker into an inflationary regime. Arthur Burns invented core inflation so he could argue there's less to worry about.
- **Rewriting the risk-off playbook:** dollar won't rally as hard (equity flows much bigger now, unhedged); commodities can rally *through* a commodity-induced recession; gold is a dual inflation/tail hedge.
- **Private credit = subprime 2007 echo.** Bank lending to NBFIs has "mushroomed." CDO-squared analog. *Explicit calibration disagreement with [[LynAlden]]* on contagion severity.
- **Yield curve call:** steepen (OPEC 1 / Burns style), not flatten (OPEC 2 / Volcker style).
