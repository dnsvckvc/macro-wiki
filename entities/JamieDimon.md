# Jamie Dimon

CEO of JPMorgan Chase. Cited in the wiki as a prominent voice for the **[[PeaceDividend]]** bull case — the argument that the Iran/US/Israel war, once resolved, could usher in a period of long-term Middle East peace.

## On-the-record view (per [[making-sense-of-the-last-two-months]])
In an interview shortly before [[KevinMuir]]'s 19 April 2026 retrospective:
> *"I do think there is more geopolitical risk than we have seen since World War II. Can it play out in a way that's good? I think in the Middle East, it's possible they end with a situation with a higher chance of long-term peace. Because [[SaudiArabia]] wants it. [[UAE]] wants it. America wants it. Israel wants it. And we've destroyed — for the most part — the really bad proxy actors there."*

Dimon's view is that short-term risk is elevated but the long-term outcome could be a "50-year prosperity" scenario for the Middle East, with the "bad proxy actors" removed.

Muir uses Dimon's framing to acknowledge that the April rally "is at least partially pricing in" a peace-dividend scenario — without endorsing it himself.

## Relevance in the wiki
- Represents the dominant institutional-bull articulation of the post-war thesis — the inverse of [[PauloMacro]]'s [[NorthStar]] framework.
- Dimon's employer — JPMorgan Asset Management — is also the operator of the [[JPMCollarTrade]] ("JPM Option Whale") that sits at the center of the March 2026 market mechanics.
