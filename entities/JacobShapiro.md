# Jacob Shapiro

Geopolitical analyst and co-host of the **Geopolitical Cousins** podcast with [[MarkoPapic]]. The wiki's reference for Shapiro derives from his role in [[geopolitical-cousins-rory-johnston]] as the "Charles Barkley" (Papic's term) of the show — the interlocutor who stress-tests the guest's framework from the non-expert-but-well-informed side.

## Positions taken in the cousins-Rory episode
- **Agrees with Rory (vs. Papic):** Trump's attention span and political runway are shorter than Iran's strategic patience. *"He has to do it sometime in the next week or two for the Xi Jinping summit."*
- **Raises the Iran-escalation-ladder question:** even if regime change is "off the table" rhetorically, Trump's 4-days-prior threat to "knock out every single power plant and every single bridge in Iran" is functionally equivalent in how Iran perceives it — ratcheting Iran's incentive to keep Hormuz closed longer.
- **Flags the Russia-Ukraine oil-attack interaction:** Shapiro's question about Zelensky hitting Russian oil infrastructure during the Iran crisis is what prompts Johnston's "pro-cyclicality" framework for Ukrainian attacks (the higher oil goes, the more Ukraine has incentive to attack Russia, which tightens balances further).
- **Raises the cascade risk:** what if multiple disruptions stack (Hormuz closed + Gulf hurricane + Russia disruptions)? Names this as where the scenario genuinely becomes "doomer."

## Relationship to the wiki's framework
Shapiro is the first wiki source explicitly naming the **cascade-risk vs. single-shock** distinction for the April 2026 oil market. Johnston's response (*"the longer Hormuz remains closed and the more barrels we lose out of that billion barrels of inventory loss, that's our buffer"*) formalizes how serial shocks compound against a depleting commercial-stocks buffer.
