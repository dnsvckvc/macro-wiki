# Iraq

OPEC member and (prior to Feb 2026) the second-largest producer in the Arabian Gulf after [[SaudiArabia]]. Iraq's exports run almost entirely through the [[StraitOfHormuz]] via the Basrah terminals — it lacks a functioning bypass pipeline to the Red Sea or Indian Ocean, making its production uniquely exposed to the Hormuz closure.

## April 2026 position (per [[wctw-the-oil-market-breaking-point]])
- **~3.60mmbpd of production shut in** — the largest absolute shut-in of any GCC / GCC-adjacent producer as of 26 March 2026.
- [[HFIResearch]] explicitly notes that Iraq and [[Kuwait]] will need **3–4 months to bring production back to normal** once a resolution arrives — the salt-water injection and well-pressure dynamics of Iraqi fields make quick restarts structurally difficult. This is the main reason HFI's storage-sensitivity analysis *under-estimates* the true supply loss.

## Why Iraq matters disproportionately
- No functioning bypass: unlike Saudi (Yanbu) and UAE (Fujairah), Iraq's only export channel is Hormuz.
- The 3–4 month restart lag means every week of the conflict compounds the forward-curve damage — even a 27-March ceasefire would leave Iraq's full production offline through June/July.
- [[AdamRozencwajg]]'s 930mmbbl-total / 340mmbbl-May-to-December forward-loss math ([[macrovoices-527-adam-rozencwajg]]) is driven substantially by the Iraq/Kuwait restart-lag that HFI flags.

## Role in the air-pocket propagation
- Iraqi crude had been a baseload supplier to Asian refineries (China, India). Its disappearance is the largest single input into [[RoryJohnston]]'s region-by-region air pocket timeline (East Asia week 4, per [[macrovoices-528-rory-johnston]]).
