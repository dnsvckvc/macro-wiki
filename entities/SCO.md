# SCO — ProShares UltraShort Bloomberg Crude Oil ETF

A 2x daily-inverse crude oil ETF issued by ProShares. Benchmark: the **Bloomberg Commodity Balanced WTI Crude Oil Excess Return Index**. Seeks to deliver 2x the inverse daily performance of the index.

## Holdings
- Cash / collateral
- **Short WTI Jun26, Dec26, and Jun27 futures** (at time of [[oil-and-retail]], 21 April 2026).

## Ticker irony
"SCO" reads as "Short Crude Oil." But 'CO' is the exchange ticker for **Brent futures**, yet the product is short **WTI** futures at 2x exposure. [[PauloMacro]]: "twice as stupid and therefore absurdly hilarious."

## April 2026 relevance — retail positioning tell
Per [[oil-and-retail]]:
- **AUM exploded from ~$100mn at war outbreak to over $1bn.** Retail fund flows have been leaning in hard since the war broke out.
- Despite a significant correction in flat-price crude (WTI $115 → $93 via [[SPRRelease]]), SCO price has **gone nowhere** thanks to [[VolatilityDrag]] on large alternating daily moves + extreme negative carry (~$5/mo roll cost in the most backwardated oil curve in history).
- Short-interest/borrow read: **10 million SCO shares available to borrow at 2.7% annualized** at IB. Retail is long SCO in such size that professionals can short it cheaply.
- Paired with [[USO]] short-interest (opposite asymmetry — 10k shares at 7.9%), forms a quantitative signature of a one-sided retail oil-bear position.

## Why it matters to the wiki thesis
Paulo reads the SCO/USO pair as the clearest contemporaneous [[BruceKovner]]-style "consensus the market is not confirming" signal: retail has pressed $1bn into a structurally broken 2x oil short while pro positioning remains long crude and ships are not moving through [[StraitOfHormuz]]. Sets up convexity on the bull side — weak hands get run over in either a Big-Beautiful-Deal or North-Star-continuation resolution.
