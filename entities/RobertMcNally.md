# Robert McNally

Founder of **Rapidan Energy Group**, former National Security Council energy advisor, and a widely-cited oil-market consultant. Known for emphasizing oil demand's inelasticity and the historical pattern of oil spikes preceding recessions.

## Referenced in the wiki for
In [[we-need-to-talk-about-interest-rates-and-oil]], [[KevinMuir]] quotes a McNally Bloomberg clip on oil and recessions:

> *"An oil price spike is usually present at the scene of the crime. They either caused it, or contributed to it. Oil demand is really inelastic. It's not like going to the movies or buying cigars. It is necessary for life. If the pump price is higher, you still have to drive to work. The only way to get demand down is to stop the work — to slow economic activity. So oil keeps on rising until it causes a demand slowdown, and then, when that happens, demand collapses."*

Muir uses the quote to frame the 2026 supply-shock episode as structurally similar to the 2008 and other recession-preceding oil spikes.
