# Lyn Alden

Founder of **Lyn Alden Investment Strategy**. Engineer by background. Author of *Broken Money* (about the intersection of money and technology). One of the most widely-followed macro analysts of her generation, known for synthesizing monetary, geopolitical, and technological analysis. The wiki's **eighth author** and **first partial counterpoint** — bearish overall but explicitly less alarmist than the wiki's baseline on several specific points.

## Unique contributions to the wiki
- **The multipolar-world framework.** US is exiting peak unipolar hyperpower status (peaked late 1990s); the Iran war is a "milestone" in a Dalio-style empire shift. Global reserve currency is the last thing to decline due to network effects.
- **Why gold sold off during the war** — multiple factors: asymmetry was gone after the pre-war rally; sovereigns sell liquidity they have, not what they want; Bitcoin held up because it was already delevered.
- **$150 oil is sustainable** (same nominal as 2008, inflation-adjusted lower); $200+ starts breaking things.
- **Inflation persistence depends on money supply growth, not supply shocks.** Current spike should mostly fade absent fresh monetary expansion.
- **Private credit bank contagion is limited.** Banks' $1.9T exposure to NBFIs is only 7-8% of $25T total assets, risk-reduced.
- **Warsh ≈ Powell right now.** The war has narrowed the difference; FOMC is 12 voters, not a dictatorship.

## Stance relative to the wiki
- **Bearish on:** the war as a milestone event; Russia benefits; Europe hit hardest; food inflation coming (agrees with wiki's Wheat thesis); K-shaped economy getting worse.
- **Less alarmist than wiki baseline on:** inflation persistence (supply-driven should fade), private credit bank contagion (limited), oil thresholds ($150 sustainable vs. Paulo's $200+/$8-gas framework).
