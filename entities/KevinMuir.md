# Kevin Muir

Independent macro trader who publishes as **"the MacroTourist"** on Substack. Writes contrarian market commentary with a focus on positioning, sentiment, the VIX curve, bond-market dynamics, and cross-region/cross-asset allocation. Friends and active interlocutor with [[PauloMacro]].

## Current book (March–April 2026)
- **Short** US stock indices, will not cover until the [[VIX7v2Spread]] blows out to −3 or −4.
- **Long** US fixed income across the whole curve, overweight 2Y/5Y:
  - 5s started as a hedge against his short HY credit position.
  - Added 5Y call options for convexity.
  - Owns "the whole curve" including the long-end — a contrarian punt based on *"no one owns the long-end, I bet it flies."*
  - Added **Dec 2026 CORRA** futures (three BoC hikes priced in) against the Canadian economy getting crushed after the first hike.
  - Recommends retail investors look at [[HarleyBassman]]'s **TUA ETF** (2Y Treasuries levered to 10Y duration, now carrying positively).
- **Short** High Yield credit (5s-position is partially a hedge against this).
- **Long** Japanese equities — "truly in for the long haul"; will only add, never trim. Energy worries already priced in.
- **Long** Japanese yen — admits he's been "too bullish way too early"; losing money but holding; will signal publicly when he gives up.
- **Long** [[Gold]] outright (re-entered 27 March 2026 after the 27% correction; $4100 washout likely the low) and even more constructive on gold vs. SPX. Considering adding gold miners.
- Implicitly long **Japan / Canada / Brazil** equity alternatives to [[MAG7]] (from [[what-not-to-buy]]).

## Views on record
- MAG7 is cheap on P/E (~22.5x NTM) but on [[PriceToFreeCashFlow]] has never been more expensive; [[AIInfrastructureInvestment]] is breaking the asset-light thesis; the 2023 layoff lever is gone ("you can't fire servers"). Worst-performing bucket over six months despite record retail ownership. ([[what-not-to-buy]])
- Applies [[BruceKovner]]-style reasoning (over-owned, consensus-long, failing to rally = warning sign) to MAG7 positioning. ([[what-not-to-buy]])
- Watches the **[[VIX7v2Spread]]** rather than the front-month VIX for capitulation signals. As of early March 2026 the spread had *only just* flipped inverted — nowhere near his cover trigger of −3 to −4. ([[vix-inversion-buying-bonds-japan-jpm-option-whale]])
- **Long fixed income is now a positive-carry trade** for the first time in a long time. Front-end paper trades above Fed Funds, so he's "getting paid to take the 'Fed will be easier than expected' risk." Frames the precedent via [[PaulTudorJones]] in 1987 and Morris Sachs / Greenwich Capital in LTCM. ([[vix-inversion-buying-bonds-japan-jpm-option-whale]])
- **[[JPMCollarTrade]] / JPM Option Whale:** authored the canonical dealer-mechanics explanation — the 6475 long-put strike puts dealers at maximum short-gamma when spot sits on the strike; the three-greek squeeze (gamma + vanna + negative [[Charm]]) on Friday 6 March is the mechanic Paulo later builds on. *"This is currently a HUGE SOURCE OF INSTABILITY."* ([[vix-inversion-buying-bonds-japan-jpm-option-whale]])
- Quoted by Paulo on the same trade: *"You have to have some frickin' balls to be a dealer managing that position right now."* ([[jumping-the-shark-again]])
- **Gold thesis is unchanged:** the 27% correction into March 2026 was a positioning unwind (crowded specs + Gulf cash calls) not a fundamental break. The long-run driver is the [[LedgerToRealWorldThesis]] — [[PBOC]] and other non-aligned central banks forced to diversify into gold after the 2022 Russia asset confiscation. "Someday, the PBOC will own enough gold that they will no longer be a tailwind to this market, but that's a long time in the future, and in the meantime, I want to own what they are buying." ([[buying-gold-again]])
- **Already expected a US recession before the war** because of the precariously balanced labour market (hiring frozen pending AI-disruption clarity). The 28 Feb 2026 US/Israel offensive and resulting [[WTI]] path ($67.50 → $115 → $93) and global front-end rate back-up "just added certainty" to that forecast. ([[we-need-to-talk-about-interest-rates-and-oil]])
- **Walks through the [[OilPriceRecessionDebate]]** (Bernanke/Gertler/Watson vs. Hamilton/Herrera, with Lucas Critique and [[LutzKilian]]): argues the market will not let us run the experiment because it has already re-priced central-bank policy hawkishly on its own. *"We are getting both an energy price spike and an interest rate rise!"* Much more sympathetic to Hamilton than Bernanke. ([[we-need-to-talk-about-interest-rates-and-oil]])
- **Global CB hawkish shift is Muir's confirmation trade:** US has priced out 2.5 cuts for 2026; BoC flipped to 100%/60% chance of 2/3 hikes; ECB to 100% of 3 hikes; BoE to 100% of 3 hikes. Two of three Fed doves withdrew dissents at the most recent FOMC. Muir's Dec 2026 CORRA position (fading BoC hikes) from [[vix-inversion-buying-bonds-japan-jpm-option-whale]] is now directly against the crowd. ([[we-need-to-talk-about-interest-rates-and-oil]])
- **Oil spikes are unequivocally bad for stock markets.** Cites [[RobertMcNally]] on oil-demand inelasticity and [[MichaelCembalest]]'s chart showing both the 2000 DotCom top (oil +245% 1999–2001 → SPX −49%) and the 2007 top (→ SPX −56%) were accompanied by massive oil rises. ([[we-need-to-talk-about-interest-rates-and-oil]])
- **Munger-invert check on his own thesis:** what would need to go right? (1) oil falls back to $50 — extremely unlikely given [[StraitOfHormuz]] is closed and shipping damage will take months; (2) Bernanke is right that oil itself doesn't cause the slowdown — Muir fades this; (3) central banks hold the line — Muir fades this too. *"I will fade all three of those outcomes."* ([[we-need-to-talk-about-interest-rates-and-oil]])
- Metaphor for rising energy prices: *"Angela Lansbury before the crime has been committed. You don't know when, but you know someone is about to die."* ([[we-need-to-talk-about-interest-rates-and-oil]])
- **6 March 2026 *Watch Your Bids* podcast with [[MarkoPapic]]** ([[my-buddy-marko-broke-the-market]]): one week into the war, Muir teases Papic with the "my buddy Marko broke the market" accusation — that Papic has been so successful teaching [[FadeGeopoliticalAngst]] that the market now refuses to price geopolitical risk even when warranted. Papic accepts the parallel to [[NormanAngell]] / [[TheGreatIllusion]] and reveals his 6 January long-Brent pivot was driven by US "R-word" regime-change rhetoric + Iranian drone asymmetry. **Papic's "Iran must update the utility function of the West" framing on this call is structurally identical to [[PauloMacro]]'s later [[NorthStar]] — independent derivation.** Muir's contribution: uses Ed Seykota's *"markets are the same now as they were five to 10 years ago because they keep changing"* to argue Papic's framework needs one more adaptive step — fade is now the consensus, so fade the fade.
- **12 March 2026 short essay** ([[geopolitics-doesnt-change-prices-in-the-long-run]]): six days after the Papic podcast, Muir writes the wiki's most explicit *applied* articulation of the transformative-event exception inside [[FadeGeopoliticalAngst]]. Credits [[LastLibertarianSanFrancisco]] for the one-liner: *"Gold over the past four years would have something to say about that."* Walks through his own gold-vs-TIPS+DXY regression that broke in late 2022 when the [[LedgerToRealWorldThesis]] took over as the structural driver. Counselled clients for four years to *"throw that model out because GEOPOLITICS HAD BROKEN IT."* Gold bulls were waiting at $1,250 based on the model; Muir was right to stay long. **Pre-registers the hypothesis** for what to watch if the Iran war is similarly transformative for equities: a slow-motion regression break, not a Feb-28-style crash event. The clock runs in years, not days. References his own March 2022 origin piece *Moving From The Ledger To The Real World*.
- **19 April 2026 retrospective ([[making-sense-of-the-last-two-months]], part 1 of 2):** Decomposes the two-month war episode into four stages. Key mechanical findings: (1) **First decline was de-grossing, not beta selling** — the new signature of a podshop/quant-fund-dominated market; Gold $1,400 collapse explained by weak-long de-levering + Gulf States/Turkey selling to fund war; L/S worst losses since Liberation Day while SPX barely down. (2) **Dec 2026 WTI became the barometer for Dec SOFR year-end** — forward-rate repriced 2.05% → 2.95% (all 3 cuts removed + small hike chance), while 3-mth 1-yr swaption vol *matched Liberation Day levels* and 10-yr barely moved. (3) **30 March 2026 low = JPM roll date** — post-hoc confirmation of the 7 March [[JPMCollarTrade]] mechanics. (4) **7 April US/Iran two-week ceasefire** gapped SPX through 200-day MA; **Nasdaq 13 straight up days** (first since 15 July 2013); [[WarrenPies]]: "rally 10% in 10 days into new ATH" only prior instance was **21 March 2000.** Muir is "flabbergasted": *"When stuff that has 'never happened before' happens, it makes me worried that the underlying conditions are more unstable than participants understand."* Explicit stance: *"I was never bearish because of the war. I have separate concerns about the economy."*
- **20 April 2026 bull/bear articulation ([[outlining-the-bull-and-bear-arguments]], part 2 of 2):** First explicit intellectual walk-back in the wiki. Muir steel-mans a 6-point bull case (buy geopolitical angst per [[MarkoPapic]]/[[FadeGeopoliticalAngst]]; rising NTM EPS = "bulls' most powerful argument"; [[OneBigBeautifulBill]] fiscal → [[DontFightTheFiscal]]; US hurt least by energy shock so repatriation flows reverse; admin needs hot mid-term economy; AI worries overblown) and a 6-point bear case (permanent global military-spending crowd-out; EPS–FCF divergence / P/FCF at decade high; [[EquityRiskPremium]] at 2025 low; 2026 $30bn tax refunds << 2021 $200bn+$300bn; 21.4k/mo avg jobs all edu/health + U Mich Consumer Sentiment at quarter-century low; globalization reversal + most-over-owned US assets in history). **Concedes stocks "may have lost their discounting mechanism"** (administration actively promoting stocks; participants see all glasses half-full). **Concedes the bull case is stronger than he initially thought.** Calls current action "emotional noise, muddling the issue." **But maintains the trade: short US tech / long rest of the world ex-US.** *"The bear case desperately needs a missing catalyst."* Also provides the clearest pre-war relative-performance data: YTD into 27 Feb — MAG7 −6.86%, Nasdaq −1%, Canada +8.6%, World ex-US +9.8%, Nikkei +17%, Brazil +17.2%.
