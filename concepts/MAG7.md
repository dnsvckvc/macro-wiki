# MAG7 (Magnificent 7)

The group of US megacap technology stocks that led the post-COVID bull market. Typically: Apple, Microsoft, Alphabet, Amazon, Meta, Nvidia, Tesla.

## Bull case (as of April 2026)
- ~20% correction into the Iran/US/Israel war.
- NTM P/E compressed to ~22.5x, historically a bounce level.
- Relative P/E vs. broad market also at a historically supportive level.
- EPS estimates growing; sell-side consensus is to [[BuyTheDip]].

## Bear case — per [[KevinMuir]] in [[what-not-to-buy]]
- Worst-performing major equity bucket over the prior six months despite record retail ownership — a [[BruceKovner]]-style warning sign.
- [[AIInfrastructureInvestment]] is consuming free cash flow; FCF is collapsing.
- On a [[PriceToFreeCashFlow]] basis, MAG7 **has never been more expensive**, contradicting the P/E "cheap" narrative.
- The 2023 profit lever — mass layoffs — is unavailable because committed AI infra is fixed cost: *"You can't fire servers."*
- Risks becoming a [[BrokenThemes]] trade: late converts get stuck.

## Price-action corroboration — per [[PauloMacro]]
- In [[jumping-the-shark-again]]: MAG7 is *already* in the [[AnatomyOfACrash]] pattern and is leading SPX lower.
- In [[economic-data-copper-rollovers-and-more]]: [[Amazon]] (a MAG7 component) is flagged in a [[RolloverSyndrome]] spot.
- Paulo's guess at the [[TomHanksHasCovidMoment]] trigger: force majeures at MAG7 / hyperscalers revealing the "perceived diversification" illusion of a top-heavy S&P and Nasdaq.

## The post-ceasefire rally (per [[making-sense-of-the-last-two-months]], 19 April 2026)
- **Nasdaq ran 13 consecutive up days** after the 7 April US/Iran two-week ceasefire — the longest streak since **15 July 2013.**
- Most consecutive-up-day runs occur *from deeply oversold conditions*; this one started less than 10% from all-time highs.
- [[WarrenPies]] (3-Fourteen Research): *"rally 10% in 10 days into new all-time highs"* — the only prior instance in US history was **21 March 2000** (dotcom final leg). Muir: *"and that wasn't a good time to buy stocks."*
- MAG7 leads the index-level recovery back above pre-war close (6879 → 7126) even though [[KevinMuir]]'s [[what-not-to-buy]] FCF thesis and [[PauloMacro]]'s [[AnatomyOfACrash]] stage-3 overlay remain unchanged.

Muir's trade structure (short US tech / long ex-US) is currently against the tape but unchanged — the pre-war thesis was flow-driven and he argues the war-era reversal is "emotional noise."
