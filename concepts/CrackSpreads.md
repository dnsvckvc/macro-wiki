# Crack Spreads

The refining margin — the per-barrel spread between the value of refined products (gasoline, diesel, jet) and the crude oil input. Reflects how much refiners earn to convert crude into products.

## Why [[PauloMacro]] treats them as "the tell"
Per [[crack-spreads-are-the-tell]], cracks act as a *second derivative* indicator for crude oil. Shocks propagate from the pump (demand side) or wellhead (supply side) and show up first in refining economics before hitting crude itself.

- **April 2020 (demand shock):** Cracks collapsed to ~$5–10/bbl in March, then inflected higher in the first two weeks of April — days before [[WTI]] went negative on 20 April 2020. Rising cracks were the tell that demand destruction had stabilized at the pump and the pain was about to ricochet back to the wellhead.
- **April 2026 (supply shock):** Cracks peaked near $60/bbl on 20 March 2026 and have been falling for two weeks. By [[InvertAlwaysInvert]] logic, this mirrors the 2020 inflection — but in reverse. Falling cracks now signal demand destruction at the pump feeding back up the chain, forcing all available product (any grade, any owner, including SPR) to market rather than shutting in. Paulo's forecast: crude *moons* rather than crashes.

## Physical-market mirror (per [[wctw-the-oil-market-breaking-point-is-here]] + [[oilgroundup-adi-imsirovic]])
The same mechanism shows up from two independent starting points:
- **[[HFIResearch]]'s positive-feedback cycle** (20 April 2026): elevated crude → compressed margins → lower refined products → lower product storage → higher refining margins → higher throughput → higher crude.
- **[[AdiImsirovic]]'s [[BrickAndMortarTrade]]** (mid-April 2026): refineries paying any price for physical barrels because shutdown means weeks of lost throughput; Northwest European refineries running net *negative* margins after hedging futures but paying dated+quality premium on physical crude.
- These are the *physical-market* and *refinery-economics* sides of Paulo's second-derivative argument. Three authors, three vantage points, one feedback loop.
