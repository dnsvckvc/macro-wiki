# Brick and Mortar Trade

[[AdiImsirovic]]'s coinage in [[oilgroundup-adi-imsirovic]] for the dynamic that produces the April 2026 $30+ Dated-Brent-over-futures spread: in an oil crisis, capital flees to *physical barrels* the same way it flees to bonds or real estate in a financial crisis.

## The analogy
> *"It's a little bit the equivalent of equity markets, when suddenly everyone gets really scared of financial crisis 2008. And everyone puts money into either bonds or brick and mortar… Everyone's going for physical. You buy physical. You know you're going to get it. You know it's going to go into refinery. You know what you're getting."*

## The mechanism
A refinery can't afford to run out of crude:
- Below 50-60% operating capacity, refineries must shut down.
- Restart after shutdown takes **weeks** and is extremely expensive in every sense (thermal stress on units, lost throughput, product-slate disruption).
- **When a refinery's purchasing desk tells its traders "get me the oil," the traders go out and pay whatever it costs.**
- "And did they overpay? Yeah, probably they did. But certainly there was a panic. And market went totally crazy."

## The self-reinforcing cycle in April 2026
Combined with the cycle [[HFIResearch]] identifies in [[wctw-the-oil-market-breaking-point-is-here]]:
1. Physical buyers pay extreme premium over futures → **Dated-Brent-vs-futures spread explodes**.
2. Refineries can't hedge: their margin calculation via futures shows $30 margin, but the physical crude they actually bought shows a **net negative margin** (paying $50 over futures).
3. Marginal refineries cut runs → product storage draws.
4. Lower product storage → higher product prices → higher refining margins → higher crude demand → back to step 1.

This is the same mechanic Paulo described as [[CrackSpreads]] rolling over in [[crack-spreads-are-the-tell]], observed from the financial-derivative side. Imsirovic describes it from the physical-trade side. Two authors, two starting points, one feedback loop.

## The Northwest Europe stress test
Per Rory's observation endorsed by Imsirovic: particularly Northwestern European refineries are already running **effectively negative margins** because of the Dated-Brent premium + quality premium for distillate-rich crude. Example (Imsirovic, reconstructed from a "European refinery that we shall not name"):
- Derivatives team hedging: ~$30 margin via futures (looks great).
- Physical team buying: paying $50 over futures on Focados (Nigerian distillate-rich crude).
- **Net:** $30 - $50 = -$20 margin on the physically-bought-hedged-on-paper barrel.
- If persistent → run cuts → product scarcity → the cycle accelerates.

## Why the trade signal matters
The Brick and Mortar Trade is a **behavioral-microstructure diagnostic** for crisis severity:
- Normal EFP (exchange of futures for physicals): 4-5 cents/bbl.
- Crisis EFP in April 2026: 14-20+ cents/bbl — a **4-5× normal** ratio.
- The EFP ratio is a clean single-number proxy for how much the market is paying for the *optionality of physical delivery* over paper exposure — divorced from curve shape or flat price.

Matching signals:
- Dated Brent - Futures spread at $30+ (historic).
- Focados quality premium at $15+ over Dated.
- June-December futures spread at $20 backwardation inside the paper curve.
- Physical delivered premia of ~$20 on top of Dated Brent → ~$150 delivered physical while "oil is $95."
