# Price-to-Free-Cash-Flow (P/FCF)

Valuation metric: market cap divided by free cash flow. Generally considered harder to manipulate than P/E because it reflects cash actually available after capex, rather than accounting earnings.

## Why it's load-bearing in [[what-not-to-buy]]
[[KevinMuir]] uses P/FCF to flip the standard "MAG7 is cheap" narrative:
- On NTM P/E, [[MAG7]] looks historically cheap (~22.5x).
- On P/FCF, MAG7 has **never been more expensive** in the post-COVID era.
- The divergence is explained by [[AIInfrastructureInvestment]] crushing FCF while earnings (which exclude much of the capex burden) still grow.

Takeaway: when capex intensity is rising fast, P/E flatters valuation; P/FCF reveals the hit.
