# Supercontango

An extreme form of contango in the crude oil forward curve where near-dated futures trade at a very deep discount to later months. Economically, it pays storage: buyers can take physical delivery, store the crude, and sell forward at a large profit. April 2020 produced the archetypal supercontango, driving a wave of floating storage trades on VLCC tankers.

## Why it matters in the wiki
[[PauloMacro]] uses the April 2020 long-tanker supercontango trade as the structural cautionary tale for today's long-[[RefineryStocks]] trade: a seductive "outsized returns" setup that implodes when the regime flips. In 2020 the trade worked until it didn't; today's refinery-long trade is tempting for the same reason and — per Paulo — is about to reverse as [[CrackSpreads]] roll over.
