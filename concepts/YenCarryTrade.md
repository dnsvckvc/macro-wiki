# Yen Carry Trade

The most-cited carry trade in macro: **borrow in yen at near-zero rates, invest in higher-yielding USD assets** (Treasuries, equities, EM debt). Holders include Japanese institutions, global hedge funds, and retail FX speculators. Decades-long persistence has made it structurally embedded in cross-asset price formation.

## The classic relationship — USD/JPY ↔ US 10Y

Conventional macro logic: higher US yields make the carry more profitable → more USD demand → stronger dollar / weaker yen → higher USD/JPY. The empirical correlation has been remarkably tight for decades, with USD/JPY tracking the US 10Y yield as one of the cleanest cross-asset relationships in macro.

## The 2026 break (per [[jgbs-usdjpy-us10y-volatility]])

[[PauloMacro]] flags that the historical USD/JPY ↔ US 10Y relationship **has begun diverging since around Liberation Day (~9 April 2026)**:
- **JGB 10Y** broke out to new closing highs (overnight 26-27 April).
- **USD/JPY** range-bound 158-160 for nearly two months.
- **USD/JPY implied vol** collapsed to near 4-year lows.

Paulo's hypothesis: the divergence is structurally analogous to the gold-vs-real-yields break that began with Russia's reserve confiscation in February 2022. The 2007–2022 gold/real-yields regression broke completely from 2022 onwards because the [[LedgerToRealWorldThesis]] became the new structural driver. **Paulo pre-registers the same template for USD/JPY ↔ UST 10Y starting April 2026** — possibly years to play out fully.

> *"Maybe 2022-23 was to gold vol what 2025-26 is to yen and 10Y vol?"*

## The "Upside Down" carry-unwind scenario

Paulo's central worry: the *next* major risk-off doesn't look like the *prior* ones. The conventional pattern (yen rallies + USTs rally) may flip:
- **Conventional risk-off:** equities sell off → yen carry unwinds → JPY rallies → USTs rally (flight to quality).
- **Upside-Down risk-off:** equities sell off → JPY rallies → **USTs *sell off*** (foreign holders no longer add Treasuries, hot-money [[BasisTrade]] holders sell instead).

The carry unwind alongside a UST selloff would be unprecedented in the post-2008 regime — and structurally compatible with [[LukeGromen]]'s observation that 37% of net UST issuance over the past four years has gone to Cayman Islands hedge funds running the basis trade ([[macrovoices-528-luke-gromen]]). When hot money sells, the conventional flight-to-quality bid simply isn't there.

## Why carry hasn't blown up yet (as of 27 April 2026)

Paulo: *"Despite the parade of quant blowups, factor unwinds, mechanical investor whiplash, and hedge fund losses across various strategies in 2026, I am struck that carry has not had a chance yet to get in on all the fun."* The 2026 unwind sequence so far:
- Gold positioning unwind (late January).
- Quant quakes (4Q25, ongoing).
- L/S worst losses since Liberation Day (early March).
- STIR specialist hedge fund losses (March).
- **But no major carry-trade unwind yet.**

Paulo treats this as the next domino — and the suppressed USD/JPY vol (4-year lows) as the mispricing.
