# Refinery Stocks

Equities of companies whose earnings are primarily driven by refining margins (i.e. [[CrackSpreads]]). As crack spreads widen, refiner profitability and stock prices typically rise; when cracks compress, the opposite.

## Role in the wiki
[[PauloMacro]] in [[crack-spreads-are-the-tell]] flags long refinery stocks as the April 2026 analog of the long-tanker/[[Supercontango]] trade of April 2020: a tempting "outsized returns" setup that is about to reverse as cracks roll over off the 20 March 2026 peak of ~$60/bbl. His warning is specifically about the *seductive logic* of the trade being structurally similar — not about the refiner fundamentals themselves.
