# The Great Illusion (1909)

Book by [[NormanAngell]]. Its central argument:
> *"The economic cost of war was so great that no one could possibly hope to gain by starting a war, the consequences of which would be so disastrous."*

Widely read in pre-WWI Britain. World War I began five years after publication. The book became synonymous with "an argument that was analytically correct but operationally disastrous because policymakers and actors do not always behave as the economic-rationalist framework predicts."

## Wiki relevance
Invoked in [[my-buddy-marko-broke-the-market]] as a parallel for [[GeopoliticalAlpha]] / [[FadeGeopoliticalAngst]]:
- [[MarkoPapic]] self-deprecatingly: *"When World War Three starts and nobody is positioned correctly, they'll blame my book the way they did with The Great Illusion."*
- [[KevinMuir]]'s thesis is that Papic's success has created a *Great Illusion* moment for markets: everyone has internalized "fade geopolitical risk" to the point that the one event that actually changes things is now *unhedged*.
