# Don't Fight the Fiscal

[[KevinMuir]]'s April 2026 riff on **Marty Zweig's** famous *"Don't Fight the Fed."* Coined in [[outlining-the-bull-and-bear-arguments]] as shorthand for the claim that **fiscal policy — not monetary policy — is now the dominant driver of risk-asset returns.**

> *"Although many market pundits still focus too much on monetary policy, if there is anything we all should have learned over the past few years, it's that fiscal trumps monetary policy."*

## The underlying identity
Muir pairs the coinage with the Kalecki-style identity: **the government's deficit is the private sector's credit (profit).** So long as the US runs outsized fiscal deficits and continues to lean harder on fiscal stimulus than the rest of the G7, private-sector profits — and thus equity earnings — are structurally supported.

## April 2026 application — both ways
- **Bull:** [[OneBigBeautifulBill]] is rolling out with the no-tax-on-tips, no-tax-on-social-security, and CAPEX capitalization tax provisions designed for maximum current-year benefit into the mid-terms. Muir treats this as the strongest fiscal-side reason to question his short-US-tech thesis.
- **Bear:** In practice the direct-stimulus comparison is far smaller than 2021. 2021 had $200bn in cheques + $300bn in extra unemployment insurance. 2026's tax refunds are ~$30bn. *"Still a lot of money, but nowhere near the 2021 stimulus."* Odds of $2,000 cheques "declined dramatically" as the war lengthens and Congress must approve more military funds first.

So Muir uses the same framework to calibrate both sides: the direction of fiscal is supportive, but the magnitude is below the hype.

## Relationship to other wiki concepts
- **Contrasts with [[MichaelHowell]]'s [[GlobalLiquidityCycle]]:** Howell documents net-tightening *global* liquidity (ECB/BoE/BoJ still shrinking; cycle peaked Q4 2025). US-specific fiscal is a domestic counterweight inside a downshifting global liquidity regime.
- **Reinforces Paulo's [[LiquidityMechanics]]** framing of the April 2026 bounce: short-term TGA drawdown + fiscal injections explain why the rally happened despite the North-Star oil supply shock.
