# Covid Oil Crash 2020

The March–April 2020 collapse in crude oil triggered by the Covid demand shock. Canonical sequence:

- Early 2020: WTI drifting from ~$60 toward $30.
- 11 March 2020: "Tom Hanks has Covid" marks the final avalanche in risk; [[CrackSpreads]] collapse to ~$5–10/bbl.
- Early April 2020: Cracks inflect higher, breaking back above $10 — the "tell" that demand destruction had stabilized at the pump and the shock was about to ricochet up the chain.
- 20 April 2020: [[WTI]] goes negative, forcing the US shale industry to shut in and effectively stopping the world's marginal barrel.

Magnitude: a dislocation of >10mmbpd of crude, driven by demand evaporating.
