# Global Liquidity Cycle

[[MichaelHowell]]'s proprietary framework tracking a roughly **65-month (~5.4-year) sine-wave cycle** in global liquidity for advanced economies. Measured via the CrossBorder Capital / GLIndexes Global Liquidity Index, which aggregates central bank actions, commercial bank lending, cross-border capital flows, Treasury operations, and private credit creation across dozens of economies.

## Core distinction: level vs. momentum
- **Level** = total liquidity available ($188T as of early 2026). Supports the real economy.
- **Momentum** = rate of change in liquidity. Drives risk-asset performance (stocks, crypto, speculative positioning).
- You can have a growing real economy *and* falling risk-asset prices simultaneously if the level is high enough for real activity but growth is decelerating. This is the regime Howell argues we entered in early 2026.

## Current cycle position (March 2026)
- Peaked **Q4 2025 / early 2026**. Momentum now inflecting lower.
- Aligns with the multi-year upswing from late 2022 maturing per the ~65-month pattern.
- Short-term boosts (PBOC, Fed/Treasury operations) provide temporary support but cannot reverse the broad downswing.
- Rising **bond volatility** and a **stronger USD** are structural headwinds.
- **Central bank divergence:** Fed may ease; ECB, BoE, BoJ continue balance-sheet shrinkage → net global tightening even if the US turns accommodative.
- **Debt rollovers:** trillions due 2026–2028 absorb liquidity as refinancing occurs at higher costs.

## How it relates to Paulo's US-level plumbing
The Global Liquidity Cycle is the **macro overlay**; [[PauloMacro]]'s [[LiquidityMechanics]] (TGA/RRP/bank reserves) is the **US micro-plumbing**. The relationship:
- Howell explains *why* the broad tide is going out (the ~65-month cycle turned; central banks outside the US are still shrinking).
- Paulo explains the *specific US mechanics* (Bessent TGA management, Fed RMP, bank-reserve thresholds) that create short-term counter-trend injections within Howell's broader downswing.
- The April 2026 risk rally that Paulo attributed to TGA drawdown + short-covering ([[the-north-star-vs-liquidity]]) is a micro-plumbing counter-trend move *within* a maturing global cycle that is no longer supportive at the momentum level.

## Yield curve phase taxonomy (per [[bond-markets-point-the-way]])
Howell maps four yield-curve phases to the liquidity cycle, each with a generic investment regime:
1. **Expansion:** bull steepening — Risk On, early cycle.
2. **Speculation:** bear flattening (short rates rising faster than long) — **current phase**. Positive economic surprises still possible, but increasingly fragile.
3. **Turbulence:** bull flattening (long rates fall faster than short) — **next in sequence**. Definitively Risk Off. Associated asset allocation: government bonds, gold, defensives; equities and credit underperform.
4. **Retreat:** bear steepening — late recession.

Bond term premia are already falling across most major markets (except Japan/China), confirming the approach of *Turbulence*.

## The four phases of the [[DebtLiquidityRatio]] (per [[risks-of-another-financial-crisis-part-1]])
1. **Post-GFC Liquidity Boom (2009–2025):** CB injections + ZIRP/NIRP → debtors termed out borrowings → [[DebtLiquidityRatio]] collapsed to 1.58x in 2021 (>2σ below average) → "Everything Bubble."
2. **Inflection (Present):** Absolute liquidity still rising ($190T, +$17.2T over past 12 months). But growth rate peaked end-Q3 2025, and the real economy is competing for funds. *"All money that is anywhere must be somewhere."*
3. **The Coming Squeeze (Projected):** The [[WallOfDebt]] (~$45T advanced, ~$65T global by 2030) forces the debt/liquidity ratio above 2.0x in 2026 and toward 2.3x by 2030. Next liquidity trough projected ~2027.

## The Three Engines of Global Liquidity (per [[crypto-gold-pboc-vs-fed]], 27 April 2026)

Howell's late-April crystallization of *what creates incremental Global Liquidity*:

| Engine | Status (April 2026) | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| **US Federal Reserve** | Stalling — RMP "front-loaded" ahead of April 15 | [[ReserveManagementPurchases]] + balance-sheet ops + bank-reg changes (eSLR) |
| **[[PBOC]]** | Disappointing — easing has stalled | Cumulative Chinese central-bank stimulus |
| **[[MOVEIndex]]** | "Decent but remains vulnerable" | Low bond vol → Treasury buybacks (~$30bn per 10pt) + supports [[BasisTrade]] |

*"Two of the three main engines of Global Liquidity creation are stalling (US Fed and PBoC), and while the third (MOVE Index) looks decent again, it remains vulnerable."*

This is the operating taxonomy for the *incremental* dial; the [[DebtLiquidityRatio]] is the *systemic* dial.
