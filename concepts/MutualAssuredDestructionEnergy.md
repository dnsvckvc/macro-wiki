# Mutual Assured Destruction (Energy)

[[MarkoPapic]]'s framing (in [[geopolitical-cousins-rory-johnston]]) for the **30 March 2026 Israeli-Iranian reciprocal upstream-facility strike exchange** as the moment when both sides achieved de facto energy-sector mutually-assured-destruction — functionally analogous to the nuclear MAD doctrine of the Cold War.

## The 30 March 2026 exchange
- **Israel** struck [[SouthPars]] — Iran's portion of the world's largest gas field (shared with Qatar as "North Field").
- **Iran** hit Qatar LNG infrastructure (Ras Laffan, [[SaadAlKaabi]] facilities).
- Within ~24 hours, the world saw that **~30% of global LNG capacity** and **~20% of global crude** could go offline in a single reciprocal exchange.
- The exchange was publicly legible — not secret — which is what made it cement into MAD doctrine rather than fade as an isolated episode.

## Papic's framing
> *"On March 30th, when Israelis struck South Pars and Iran struck LNG… what we achieved is this moment of mutual assured destruction where it's very clear that within 24 hours we can lose, what, 30 percent of LNG on the planet? And 20 percent or thereabouts crude. So this is why… getting me to write that the war is going to restart kinetically, you're almost asking me to forecast a nuclear exchange."*

Key implications Papic draws:
1. **Kinetic re-escalation is effectively off the table.** Both sides know what happens within 24 hours; neither will voluntarily cross the upstream-facility red line again.
2. **Regime change is off the table.** Even the most hawkish US officials Papic has recently talked to in DC have stopped saying the words; they now talk about *"fissile material"* seizures at most.
3. **Trump's April light-footprint military posture (Papic's "3 out of 10") is deliberate** — further escalation is prohibitively costly given the MAD framing.
4. **The war is stalemated kinetically** but unresolved politically/economically. Duration will be determined by whose leverage decays faster, not whose military prevails.

## [[RoryJohnston]]'s endorsement (with a twist)
Johnston agrees the upstream-facility risk has receded:
> *"If the market was pricing in any tail risk of the truly catastrophic upstream facility tax, I think that is mostly behind us now. For me, again, it just comes down to a timing of how long does it take to build the pressure to get [Trump] to make a concession he needs to make."*

Johnston disagrees with Papic's **who-blinks-first** corollary — Rory thinks Trump's political leverage decays faster than Iran's economic leverage. Both frameworks are compatible with the MAD observation; they diverge on which side is the first to cash in.

## How this fits with the wiki's broader thesis
- **Complements [[JimBianco]]'s Mosaic Doctrine** in [[macrovoices-527-jim-bianco]]: Bianco explains why Iran's distributed drone/missile networks *survive* US offensive strikes; MAD explains why the US *won't* go to full-force offensive strikes in the first place. Together they describe why Hormuz stays closed.
- **Complements Iran's [[NashEquilibrium]] / [[NorthStar]]:** Iran's optimal strategy (keep Hormuz closed) is reinforced by MAD — the US's optimal counter-response (regime-destruction campaign) is now too costly because Iran can reciprocally destroy energy infrastructure across the Gulf.
- **Constrains Paulo's [[TomHanksHasCovidMoment]] trigger search:** if MAD has removed upstream-facility attacks from the expected outcomes, the next common-knowledge break is more likely to come from *duration* (mass inventory drawdown visible in OECD data) than from *additional shock* (new kinetic escalation).

## The Iran strategic-patience corollary
Papic argues Iran now benefits from **not** escalating further: keep Hormuz closed + take no additional strikes on upstream facilities = maximum leverage accumulated with minimum counter-risk.
- *"If you're Iran, the best possible scenario for you guys, because what's actually happening here is that you build leverage over time. The longer the straight remains closed, we lose 13 million barrels every day, 400 million barrels every month."*
- Johnston counters that Iran's escalatory room remains (Houthis / Bab el-Mandeb / Kharg Island / Abqaiq) — but agrees MAD has constrained the *upstream* vectors.
