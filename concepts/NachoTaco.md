# NACHO / TACO

Pair of trader memes describing [[DonaldTrump]]'s jawboning style and how traders read through it.

- **TACO** — "Trump Always Chickens Out." A pre-existing meme used by traders to short-circuit Trump escalation rhetoric. [[PauloMacro]] argues in [[jumping-the-shark-again]] that applying TACO to Iran is mis-modeling the game: under a [[NashEquilibrium]], Iran's path doesn't depend on Trump's posture at all, so "Trump chickens out" or "Trump escalates" both lead to the same economic outcome.
- **NACHO** — "Not Actually Changing Hormuz Opening." Coined by [[LeShrub]] in late March 2026 after a 4:15pm post-close Trump jawbone attempt to "jam" markets. A variant proposed by another trader: *"Not A Chance Hormuz Opens."*

Both memes point at the same gap: rhetorical pumps that leave the physical-world situation unchanged. Closely tied to [[TrumpPumps]] — the decaying after-hours market-jam phenomenon.

## The original "Jumping the Shark" analysis ([[jumping-the-shark-when-a-taco-is-not-a-taco]])
Paulo's 10 March 2026 essay — the first "Jumping the Shark" piece — is a detailed deconstruction of why TACO doesn't apply to this war:
- The original Liberation Day TACO (April 2025) produced a +10% SPX rally after a −20% decline. This latest "Mission Accomplished" TACO produced only **+83bps**. The market reaction and shelf-life of TACOs is collapsing.
- Liberation Day was a crisis of Trump's own making that he could undo by reversing his own policy. The Iran war is **not a single-player game** — it requires Iran's cooperation to resolve, and Iran is in a [[NashEquilibrium]] where their optimal strategy doesn't change regardless of Trump's moves.
- **"Commodities are not a cartoon."** Equities and credit can ignore reality because flow (passives, buybacks) drives prices divorced from fundamentals. Commodities converge into the real world — the "forcing function in commodities is the price-insensitive buyer who needs the physical when it simply is no longer available."
- Trump "has wandered into my lane" — commodity markets he "neither appreciates nor understands, and cannot control for long."

## The TACO Misconception (per [[the-north-star-vs-liquidity]])
Paulo's 12 April 2026 piece names the core error: the **TACO Misconception** — the belief that because TACO worked during Liberation Day and other Trump-created crises (which were entirely within his power to reverse), it must work during the Iran war. But this crisis is *not* in Trump's control and Iran's optimal strategy is a [[NashEquilibrium]]. The misconception persists because investors "don't change their minds easily on concepts that have worked so well" — and it will persist until the [[TomHanksHasCovidMoment]] creates [[CommonKnowledge]] that breaks the cartoon.

## The TACO expectation is itself priced in (per [[macrovoices-524-rory-johnston]])
A meta-level observation from [[RoryJohnston]]: even with war insurance premiums jumping from 0.25% to ~5% of vessel value, shipping companies can easily cover that cost with the arbitrage across the Strait. **They're still not moving** — and his explanation is that the market has priced in an imminent TACO:
> *"The base case expectation is that Trump backs out here, that we see another TACO. Why spend $5 million and risk your ship and crew if this could be over tomorrow?"*
This means the TACO Misconception is operational *at the physical-shipping level*, not just the financial-market level. The expected TACO is keeping commerce frozen even when the economics of transit would otherwise clear. If Paulo is right that this crisis is in a [[NashEquilibrium]] with no TACO coming, the longer this drags on, the more violently shipping will eventually reprice.

### Concrete instance: the Saturday 3-VLCC spike (per [[macrovoices-528-rory-johnston]])
On "ceasefire weekend" (~12 April), **3 VLCCs** transited the Strait — the first real volume-weighted move. Markets took it as resolution. Oil fell ~$10/bbl. S&P rallied back above pre-war levels. But it wasn't sustained — **zero non-Iranian VLCCs have transited since.** This is the [[MichaelEvery]] "media narrative warfare" thesis confirmed operationally: a single day of VLCC movement was enough to convince markets of resolution, even though the underlying physical situation didn't change.

## Papic's "lands the plane" reframing (per [[my-buddy-marko-broke-the-market]], 6 March 2026)
[[MarkoPapic]] offers a more structural description of the TACO pattern:
> *"He doesn't really chicken out. He gets a deal. And the deal, you know, the deal doesn't get him to the moon, but he gets to go to the top of his house."*

Papic prefers the term *"lands the plane"* — implying a genuine negotiation with a sub-optimal but acceptable outcome, not cowardice. The mechanism is symmetric across the Canada / NATO / China / tariffs / crypto cycles: Trump escalates, counterparties negotiate, deal gets done.

**But the Iran game has a different counterparty.** Papic: *"In every previous situation where President's delicious taco was eaten whole by his counterparts — in this particular case, the Islamic Republic might say 'take your taco and shove it exactly where you should shove it.'"* Papic is the earliest voice in the wiki to explicitly identify this as the break from every prior TACO episode.

## April 7 two-week ceasefire is a real event (per [[making-sense-of-the-last-two-months]])
The wiki had previously treated ceasefire talk as TACO-style rhetoric. [[KevinMuir]]'s 19 April retrospective records a concrete institutional step: **on 7 April 2026, Iran and the US agreed to a two-week ceasefire.** S&P 500 gapped through its 200-day MA in the immediate response; Nasdaq then ran 13 consecutive up days (longest streak since 15 July 2013). This is different from a TACO headline — it's a formal pause — but the underlying Nash game is unchanged:
- [[PauloMacro]]'s 12 April ([[the-north-star-vs-liquidity]]) and 21 April ([[oil-and-retail]]) pieces both post-date the ceasefire and maintain the [[NorthStar]] — Iran hasn't relinquished Hormuz, no barrels have been added, [[SaudiArabia]]/[[UAE]] remain shut-in, and Johnston's [[macrovoices-528-rory-johnston]] documents zero non-Iranian VLCCs transiting in the days after.
- The ceasefire narrative validates [[PeaceDividend]] partial pricing-in, but the physical and positioning data contradict it.
