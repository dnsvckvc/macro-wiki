# One Big Beautiful Bill

The Trump administration's 2026 omnibus fiscal package, designed for maximum current-year benefit into the autumn mid-term elections.

## Key provisions cited in the wiki
Per [[KevinMuir]] in [[outlining-the-bull-and-bear-arguments]]:
- **No tax on tips** and **no tax on Social Security benefits** — direct consumption support.
- **Attractive capitalization tax rates for CAPEX spending** — accelerated write-offs to pull forward corporate investment.
- The package was "designed for maximum benefit for the current year (to help with mid-terms)."

## Wiki relevance
- **Bull pillar** in Muir's bull-case articulation — operates through [[DontFightTheFiscal]]: the government's deficit supports the private sector's profits.
- **Bear calibration:** the direct-stimulus component (~$30bn in tax refunds) is a small fraction of the 2021 stimulus ($200bn cheques + $300bn UE). The CAPEX capitalization provisions are structural but slow-burning; don't produce an immediate manic consumption impulse.
- Congressional dynamics with the war: as the Iran war lengthens, the administration "will need to go to Congress to get more money for the military" — which crowds out additional direct-stimulus bills like hypothetical $2,000 cheques.
