# "Tom Hanks Has Covid" Moment

[[PauloMacro]]'s archetype for the discrete event that flips a market from "denial" to "crash." Named after 11 March 2020, when the Tom Hanks Covid diagnosis became the headline that crystallized the pandemic into [[CommonKnowledge]] and triggered the risk avalanche and [[CrackSpreads]] collapse described in [[CovidOilCrash2020]]. First defined in [[tom-hanks-has-covid]] (5 March 2026) through a first-person retelling of Paulo's experience on the trading floor in February–March 2020.

## Structure
- The underlying reality is already true and worsening.
- Markets still trade on narrative momentum and rhetorical pumps.
- A single *visible* event — not necessarily the most important event — causes everyone to see the same thing and, crucially, to see that *everyone else* is seeing the same thing.
- From that moment, denial is no longer a viable strategy and the repricing is rapid.

## Application (March 2026)
- In [[tom-hanks-has-covid]] (5 March 2026), Paulo identifies **China telling top refiners to suspend diesel and gasoline exports** as his candidate for the "Tom Hanks Has Covid" moment in energy: the world's second-largest economy with 3–4 months of crude storage is signaling this isn't a 5-day event. "Snapcount" in energy begins.
- In [[jumping-the-shark-again]] (27 March 2026), the market is still *waiting* for its Tom Hanks Has Covid moment **in equities**. Paulo's updated guess: force majeures tied to [[MAG7]] and Hyperscalers — which would simultaneously reveal the "perceived diversification" illusion of a top-heavy S&P and Nasdaq. But "it could be anything. A lone wolf attack somewhere. It will be like porn — I'll know it when I see it."

## Still waiting (12 April 2026, [[the-north-star-vs-liquidity]])
Paulo waits *"in suspended animation… with Liquidity & Positioning on one side propelling the financial plate-spinning cartoon, and Can't Print Molecules on the other side… wondering when the two collide and fuse in a Tom Hanks Moment."* The TACO Misconception — the belief that this crisis is within Trump's control to resolve — is the narrative layer that delays the common-knowledge break. The [[NorthStar]] says the moment is inevitable; [[LiquidityMechanics]] explains why it hasn't arrived yet.
