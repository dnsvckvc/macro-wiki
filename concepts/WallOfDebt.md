# Wall of Debt (Debt Maturity Wall)

The bunching of debt maturities — particularly from the COVID era when interest rates were at rock bottom and borrowers termed out their liabilities — coming due for refinancing over the next several years.

## Scale
Per [[MichaelHowell]] in [[risks-of-another-financial-crisis-part-1]]:
- **Advanced economies:** ~$45T in debt maturing by 2030 (roughly 2.5x annual new capex).
- **Global total:** ~$65T by 2030.
- This is not new borrowing — it is the refinancing of *existing* debt, now at higher rates and in more volatile conditions.

## Why it matters
- **Debt grows exponentially; liquidity grows cyclically.** The Wall of Debt arrives just as the [[GlobalLiquidityCycle]] turns down (peaked Q3 2025), creating the structural mismatch that drives the [[DebtLiquidityRatio]] above the 2.0x danger threshold.
- Markets must absorb this refinancing wave while:
  - Liquidity momentum is fading
  - The real economy is competing for the same pool of funds
  - Central banks outside the US continue balance-sheet shrinkage
  - Bond volatility (MOVE) is elevated, reducing the value of the collateral that underpins the lending
- The result: forced liquidation as portfolio duration gets stretched beyond sustainable levels.

## How it connects to the wiki
- Paulo's [[CreditCycle]] work (private credit at 80–85% of NAV; CLO software loans getting killed; Minsky sequencing of sell-what-you-can) describes the *early symptoms* of the Wall of Debt arriving.
- Paulo's [[BasisTrade]] unwinding (hedge-fund net futures short down >25%; [[SwapSpreads]] deeply negative) is the Treasury-specific expression of the broader refinancing stress Howell quantifies.
- Muir's observation in [[we-need-to-talk-about-interest-rates-and-oil]] that the bond market is being destroyed by the oil-driven inflation shift provides the *catalyst* that makes the Wall of Debt more painful: COVID-era debt termed out at near-zero rates must now be refinanced at structurally higher yields.
