# Stealth Treasury/Fed Accord (2026)

[[MichaelHowell]]'s 25 April 2026 framework ([[treasury-qe-warsh-fed-eclipsed]]) for the *de facto* regime change beginning in 2025: the **US Treasury has quietly replaced the Federal Reserve as the marginal driver of US and Global Liquidity.** Setting Fed funds is now *"a pantomime, a sideshow."*

## The historical analog

The **1951 Fed-Treasury Accord** ended an era in which the Fed was effectively a debt-management agency for the Treasury (capping rates to support war-debt issuance). It established the modern norm: Fed sets monetary policy independently, Treasury manages debt issuance independently.

The **2025/26 "Stealth" Accord** quietly reverses this:

| | 1951 Accord | 2025/26 Stealth Accord |
|---|---|---|
| Fed's role | Monetary policy independent | **Financial stability / repo backstop** |
| Treasury's role | Debt management | **Growth driver** |
| Coordination | Formal separation | **Informal merger** |
| Public framing | Explicit independence | **Neither admits to monetizing debt** |
| Tools | Fed funds + reserves | **Bill skew + volatility-targeting buybacks** |

> *"The 1951 Fed-Treasury Accord separated monetary policy from debt management. The 2025/26 'Stealth' version merges them back together."*

> *"Neither party, Fed or US Treasury, admits to monetizing debt. But these policies facilitate and encourage precisely that."*

## Why "Stealth"

There is no signed agreement. The regime change is observed *from the data*: Fed QE (balance-sheet expansion + compositional changes) is declining through 2026 while [[TreasuryQE]] (bill issuance + buybacks) is rising strongly. Public messaging from both institutions still emphasizes traditional roles.

The political-economy attractiveness for the administration:
- Bypasses Fed independence concerns.
- Operates without Senate confirmation (the Treasury Secretary is the lead operator).
- Compatible with Trump-era public preferences (smaller Fed footprint, "Fed shouldn't have so much influence").
- Achieves rate-cut-equivalent stimulus *without* the Fed actually cutting.

## The dealer-balance-sheet primary constraint

The Stealth Accord exists because of a binding paradox the *prior* wiki framing did not fully capture:

> *"The critical issue is not the volume of banks' reserves. It is the capacity of dealers' balance sheets to warehouse Treasuries and intermediate repo flows."*

Dealer-banks face Basel III / eSLR caps that prevent them from simultaneously absorbing heavy Treasury issuance *and* funding private markets. *"Scarce intermediation capacity co-exists with the Fed's provision of ample reserves."* The Accord routes around this by suppressing the [[MOVEIndex]], which expands the [[CollateralMultiplier]], which mechanically expands Global Liquidity *without* needing dealer balance-sheet expansion.

## Implication for Chair Warsh

[[KevinWarsh]] inherits a Fed that is *no longer in full control of US growth*. His prior 2010s opposition to QE has been overtaken by the structural mechanism: the system's linchpin has moved from reserves to dealer balance sheet capacity and the MOVE index. *"Drastically shrinking the Fed's balance sheet, as mooted, and nostalgically returning to a 'scarce reserves' regime is no longer possible."*

Even a hawkish Warsh cutting the Fed balance sheet would only further empower the Stealth Accord — making Treasury QE *more* relatively important.

## Fragility

The Accord is "ingenious" but fragile (per [[treasury-qe-warsh-fed-eclipsed]]):

1. **Moral hazard** — markets expect Treasury buybacks whenever vol rises → encourages excessive leverage in [[BasisTrade]]. Hedge funds are the marginal buyers of Treasuries (foreign demand has faded — see [[LukeGromen]]'s Cayman Islands data).
2. **Collateral fragility** — if MOVE spikes despite buybacks (geopolitical shock or dealer solvency event), the [[CollateralMultiplier]] collapses *mechanically*. March 2020 COVID is the precedent.

> *"The Accord suppresses MOVE to manage volatility and prevent spikes — but that very suppression creates the complacency that could make the next spiral worse."*
