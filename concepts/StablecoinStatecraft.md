# Stablecoin Statecraft

[[MichaelEvery]]'s framework for the emerging financial-architecture layer that will follow the Iran/US/Israel conflict. Once one side takes de facto control of Middle East energy, **stablecoins** — either US-dollar-backed or Renminbi-backed — will be rolled out as the instruments of the new architecture. Already in motion in the background.

## Two competing models
- **US version:** freewheeling private sector. Multiple dollar-backed stablecoins issued by private companies; state-tolerated rather than state-operated. The dollar's global reach extended into blockchain rails.
- **Chinese version:** centralized Renminbi-backed stablecoins, government-run. Tightly controlled, deployed as an alternative settlement layer for countries seeking to reduce dollar dependence.

## Why it matters
- Whatever comes out the other side of the conflict, **the financial architecture won't look like it did before.** This is the payments/settlement layer of the multipolar world [[LynAlden]] describes.
- The conflict concentrates attention and capital flows in a way that creates the window to deploy new monetary rails. Trump administration is "cognizant of it and thinking about how to use it to its advantage."
- Connects to the broader [[NorthStar]] thesis: if the war permanently changes the architecture around Middle East energy, the financial plumbing will follow.
