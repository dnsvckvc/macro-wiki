# Anatomy of a Crash

[[PauloMacro]]'s foundational framework for distinguishing a genuine market crash from a routine correction. Laid out in full in [[the-anatomy-of-a-crash]] (14 March 2026). A key Paulo tenet: *markets don't crash from euphoric sentiment; they crash from hyperextended positioning and unstable conditions right when participants begin to grow concerned.*

## The 4-stage pattern
1. **Initial correction and a relief bounce.**
2. **Retest of the low**, often with a minor breach and an intraday "tail."
3. **A 1–2 day final bounce** ("all clear, we can rally because everyone is bearish").
4. **Collapse.**

## Historical examples Paulo cites
- **1987 Black Monday** (Dow) — the canonical case.
- **2021 bitcoin crash** $60k → $30k.
- **1929** — data limited but the pattern holds.
- **1997 Hang Seng** / Asia Crisis — demonstrating the pattern is not US-specific.
