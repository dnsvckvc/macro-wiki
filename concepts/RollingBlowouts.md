# Rolling Blowouts

A [[PauloMacro]] framework originally developed in summer 2018 after observing a sequence of seemingly unrelated ruptures in specific corners of global markets. The claim: rolling blowouts in disparate strategies / regions are *not* unrelated — they are the first phase of an incoming [[RiskOff]], and "Rolling Blowouts have only ever ended in one way — Risk Off."

## The 2018 template
- **January 2018:** Bitcoin blew up.
- **February 2018:** Volmageddon — short-vol and risk-parity strategies torched.
- **March 2018:** FAANG drawdown, Facebook data scandal, Libor ripped.
- **April 2018:** UST 10Y yield through 3% for the first time in 4+ years.
- **May 2018:** Turkey, Argentina, Brazil imploded.
- **June 2018:** Italy BTPs and Spanish yields exploded.

Paulo's 2018 conclusion: even if the S&P made new all-time highs (it did), a dramatic Risk Off lay ahead.

## Relationship to [[RollingVARShock]]
Rolling Blowouts is the *macro* version across markets/regions; [[RollingVARShock]] is the *micro* version inside hedge fund strategies. In [[the-anatomy-of-a-crash]] Paulo argues the two have now stacked on top of each other: rolling blowouts across corners of the market plus a rolling VAR shock inside hedge fund books — converging into the [[AnatomyOfACrash]] setup.
