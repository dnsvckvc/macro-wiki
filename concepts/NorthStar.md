# North Star

[[PauloMacro]]'s name for his guiding navigational principle throughout the Iran/US/Israel war: **see everything through the lens of [[NashEquilibrium]].** Iran's optimal strategy does not change regardless of Trump's moves, ceasefire headlines, diplomatic channels, or TACO rhetoric. The North Star is the "kernel of truth at the center of this giant cartoon."

## The concept
When the market floods with noise — TACO headlines, SPR releases, Bessent jawboning, liquidity injections, ceasefire rumors — the North Star cuts through by asking one question: *has Iran's optimal strategy changed?* If no, the energy crisis is still baked in and the rest is timing.

## Why it matters
- **Short-term:** Liquidity mechanics ([[LiquidityMechanics]]) and positioning wash-outs can and do drive multi-week rallies that confound the bearish thesis. Paulo himself didn't call the late-March/early-April bounce.
- **Medium-term:** But the TGA tailwind reverses after April 15 tax receipts, hedges have been dumped (largest weekly ETF short-covering in a decade), and the barrels have been counted — "we are past the point of no return for a major global energy crisis."
- **Long-term:** Shut-in production is lost forever and cannot be made up without quarters/years of time. The only variable the North Star controls is timing and acuteness — not whether the crisis happens.

## The TACO Misconception
The North Star's sharpest edge is against the TACO reflex. TACO worked during Liberation Day because that crisis was of Trump's own making and within his power to reverse. The Iran war is **not in Trump's control**. Under Nash, Iran's cooperation is not forthcoming at any price Trump can offer. Investors don't change their minds easily on concepts that have worked — hence the market jams risk higher on ceasefire headlines, and the misconception persists until the [[TomHanksHasCovidMoment]] creates [[CommonKnowledge]] that breaks the cartoon.

## Confirmed independently
[[RobertPape]] (University of Chicago), author of *Bombing to Win* and *Dying to Win*, arrived at the same conclusion via his "Escalation Trap" framework: *"Iran does not need to win militarily. It needs to sustain disruption at a level that forces the global economy to adjust under constraint."*
