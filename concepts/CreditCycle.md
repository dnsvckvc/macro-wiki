# Credit Cycle

The regular expansion and contraction of credit availability and leverage in an economy. A *credit cycle turn* — from expansion to contraction — typically precedes or coincides with recessions: credit tightens, defaults rise, consumer spending weakens, and asset prices re-rate.

## Earliest canary: CCC divergence (per [[liquidity-drains-redux]], August 2025)
Six months before the war, the junkiest credit was already diverging:
- CCC spreads at the **26th percentile** while BBB/BB/B sat at the **0–7th percentile**. The higher-quality tiers were at all-time tights; CCCs were not following.
- BBB-BB spread differential was widening — "credit starting to get the message ahead of equities."
- CCC issuance running at <10% of summer HY supply vs. a historical 15–20% average (less relative demand for the junkiest paper).
- Paulo: *"I've been waiting a long time to see pricing divergences in credit. This could be a big one worth watching."*

## Software ↔ Private Credit reflexive boomerang (per [[intensely-concerned-for-risk-with-a-catch]])
The load-bearing nexus for how the credit cycle turn propagates through equities:
- SaaS/software companies were ground zero for PE buyouts — "asset-light, high margin, high return, stable, low-churn businesses."
- ~25% of BDC portfolios are tech-exposed (BofA). Software is 15–20% of private credit (Barclays/JPM, likely understated because many SaaS names are classified by end-market).
- **Apollo cut its private credit funds' software exposure almost by half in 2025** (from ~20%).
- Software loans in CLOs are also getting killed (senior secured floaters seeing huge moves).
- The boomerang: public software cracks → private credit marks down → BDC investors redeem → forced selling of public credit → loop back to software. *"A contagion boomerang is in full effect."*
- Bloomberg: "from 2015 to 2025, more than 1,900 software companies were taken over by PE buyers in transactions valued at $440bn+."
- This was documented on **7 February 2026** — three weeks before the war. The war only accelerates and compounds this pre-existing reflexive loop.

## Early confirmation: NFP big miss (per [[oil-jobs-bond-failure-and-other-fun]])
On 6 March 2026, NFP came in as a "nasty big miss" — confirming labor-market weakness that pre-dated the war. The bond market tried to rally on the weak data and *got smoked* — a **news-failure pattern** where the expected response (bonds rally on weak jobs → Fed cuts) fails because the inflationary oil shock overwhelms the recessionary signal. This produces the **USD Up, Bonds Down, Equities Down** regime where no traditional asset class offers shelter.

## Paulo's thesis (April 2026)
A recurring [[PauloMacro]] view, stated explicitly in [[economic-data-copper-rollovers-and-more]]: **the US was already entering a credit-cycle turn before the Iran/US/Israel war broke out.** February PCE data is his evidence — headline inflation in line but household income and spending drifting lower. The war compounds an existing pressure; it is not the root cause. Inflationary pressures should not be expected to fade into the contraction.

## Mechanism detail (from [[the-anatomy-of-a-crash]])
Paulo's earlier "Credit Enters Snapcount" series flagged CLOs, BDCs, and private credit as the big shoe to drop. The Minsky-style sequencing he now observes:
- Illiquid private credit stakes are being marketed at **80–85% of NAV** in the secondary market as endowments and pensions need cash for payouts.
- A few redemption requests at NAV force unlisted funds to sell **what they can, not what they want** — the *better* portion of the portfolio.
- Investors who don't redeem get stuck with the garbage. "The first one to panic gets all his money back. The last one is left picking up the pieces."
- As redemptions gate, investors sell *public* credit instead: IG and HY spreads rise, and — newly — IG/HY **implied vol is leading Treasury MOVE vol higher**. In 2022 rate vol led (it was a rate problem). This time credit vol leads. Paulo: *"This is both a credit issue and a rate issue!"*

## Private credit cockroaches emerging (per [[making-macro-great-again-quick-updates]], 12 March 2026)
- Redemptions at **Cliffwater** and **Morgan Stanley** private credit funds.
- **JPMorgan** taking markdowns on private credit holdings.
- **Deutsche Bank** revealed exposure.
- Hyperscaler bonds (AMZN, CRM new issuances) trading badly — the market is not absorbing large IG tech paper well.
- Paulo: "no shortage of negative headlines around those cockroaches in private credit."

## Bonds as accelerant — not stabilizer (per [[make-1973-great-again]])
In a jobs recession + energy crisis, bonds do not provide portfolio stabilization:
- Tax receipts collapse while spending skyrockets (unplanned war spending).
- Deficit/GDP blows out — making "Iraq/Afghanistan look like a picnic."
- Middle East sovereign wealth turns seller of foreign assets (cash calls from collapsing revenue).
- This is Paulo's "No Fly Zone" scenario: term premia expand, the long end takes the hit, and traditional portfolio managers have few places to hide.
- Herbert Hoover memoirs: *"the refugee gold and the foreign government reserve deposits were constantly driven by fear hither and yon over the world… a loose cannon on the deck of the world in a tempest-tossed era."*
- **Nuance:** this analysis applies primarily to the **long end** (term premia). Muir's 2Y/5Y long-bond positioning may still benefit from eventual rate cuts; the long-end contrarian punt is the part that's challenged by the fiscal deterioration path.
