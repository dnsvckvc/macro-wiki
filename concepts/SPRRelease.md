# Emergency SPR Release (March 2026)

An emergency **global** Strategic Petroleum Reserve release that tamped [[WTI]] back from its $115 panic high to approximately $93 following the 28 Feb 2026 US/Israel military offensive against Iran and the closure of the [[StraitOfHormuz]].

## Context
Per [[KevinMuir]] in [[we-need-to-talk-about-interest-rates-and-oil]]: the release was described as a *global* emergency coordinated release rather than a US-only action. It succeeded in breaking the initial panic spike but did not address the underlying supply shock — Hormuz remains closed, the global energy shipping complex is damaged, and the SPR is a finite buffer.

## The math doesn't work (per [[making-macro-great-again-quick-updates]])
[[PauloMacro]] details why the 400mmbbl global release is structurally inadequate:
- Of the 400mmbbl total, the US portion is **172mmbbl**.
- US SPR currently at **415mmbbl**. Drawing 172mm → **~243mmbbl** remaining.
- The SPR caverns are **salt domes**: they work by injecting water to float oil out, but prolonged pumping activity dissolves the salt and makes them structurally unsound. Regulatory/structural limits are **150–250mmbbl** — so 243mm is effectively **tank bottoms**.
- Once there, there's not much room to keep going — and the SPR has to be refilled at some point, creating *additional* future demand not in the curve.
- Draw rate of 1.2–1.4mmbpd is "a drop in the bucket" vs. 20mmbpd not transiting Hormuz.
- The market "isn't even blinking anymore" at SPR-release headlines.

## Why it matters for the wiki
- Reinforces the [[PauloMacro]] framing in [[jumping-the-shark-again]] that "all available product — any grade, any owner, including SPR" is being forced to market: a supply-shock regime destroys the shut-in option. The SPR release is exactly that dynamic at policy level.
- Tamping price to $93 is not the same as resolving the shock — it just delays the [[CrackSpreads]] rollover mechanic that Paulo argues will drive crude higher from here, not lower.
- Per [[the-north-star-vs-liquidity]]: "The boats *coming out* don't matter because they have already been counted and sit in the oil balances. It's the boats *going back in* to sufficiently drain onshore Gulf storage that will dictate when oilfields restart, and restarts are all that matter because **shut-in production is lost forever** and cannot be made up without many quarters/years of time." The SPR release counts as "boats coming out" — already factored in.
