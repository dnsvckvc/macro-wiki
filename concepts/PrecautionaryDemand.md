# Precautionary Demand

Term from the academic commodity-markets literature: **physical-market participants bidding up spot or prompt prices to secure barrels now for fear of future unavailability**, even above what current flow balances justify. Unlike ordinary demand, precautionary demand is *behavioral and anticipatory* — it brings forward future consumption risk into today's bids.

## Johnston's application (per [[geopolitical-cousins-rory-johnston]])
[[RoryJohnston]]'s same-day Commodity Context piece (the "sanguine market steel-man") argues the Dated Brent peak of **~$144** earlier in the war was most likely a precautionary-demand overshoot:
- Physical traders saw the closure of [[StraitOfHormuz]] and **bid forward demand aggressively** — buying any barrel they could secure, even at deep premia — to insure against future unavailability.
- This produced the $30-$32 Dated-Brent-over-futures spread that [[AdiImsirovic]] calls "panic" and that [[RoryJohnston]] quantifies in [[macrovoices-528-rory-johnston]].
- Once the initial ceasefire + SPR releases absorbed the most-severe tail risk, precautionary demand receded — barrels that were "pulled forward" into early-April deliveries no longer needed to be secured at panic premia.
- **Result:** the price sold off from the peak, *confirming the bearish market narrative on the downside* even though the underlying inventory picture continues to deteriorate month by month.

## Why this matters for the wiki's thesis
Precautionary demand is the mechanism that reconciles three otherwise-tense observations:
1. [[AdamRozencwajg]]'s *"forward curve hasn't repriced"* (futures stuck around $95-$100 even as physical deliveries cost $150+).
2. [[AdiImsirovic]]'s *"markets are working as well as they can under these crazy conditions"* (the $30 spread is panic, not dysfunction).
3. [[HFIResearch]]'s *"cumulative loss is 1.2bn and rising"* (the fundamental hole in the market is getting worse).

All three can be true simultaneously if the **initial price spike was a precautionary-demand overshoot** that *pulled forward* what would otherwise have been the fundamental bid — and now the gradual inventory damage has to accumulate enough *visible* stock-draw evidence in OECD commercial inventories before the market re-prices to the underlying balance.

Johnston: *"I actually think in some ways my error, because I was saying I came out with a hundred two hundred dollar oil very quickly. I'm like, holy crap, guys, this is the big one. I'm not an alarmist. But I do think fundamentally, it's not going to react until we see it in the inventory data in visible OECD markets."*

## The trader-composition / selection-bias corollary
Precautionary demand explains the *first* spike; what explains the sustained suppression is a **compositional effect on traders**. Per Rory + Papic in [[geopolitical-cousins-rory-johnston]]:
- Multiple Trump-tweet-driven $15-$20 single-day drops (notably **April 9**, which they name explicitly as the worst).
- Those washouts blew out physical-long positions on the way down.
- **Survivors** are traders unwilling to run long physical risk even in the face of the accumulating fundamentals — because the *tactical* cost of a Trump tweet outweighs the *fundamental* upside they can hold.
- Net: the "sanguine" market is partly a survivorship-biased residual, not a true consensus that the fundamentals don't matter.
