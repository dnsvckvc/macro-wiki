# Wheat

Agricultural staple and what [[PauloMacro]] calls **"the geopolitical VIX"** — its price chart looks like a VIX chart because wheat spikes reliably in every major global conflict.

## Why Paulo is long wheat ([[make-1973-great-again]])
1. Rising food and hunger is the *highest-impact, highest-probability* cause of social unrest in human history (Arab Spring's root cause was food prices).
2. Energy touches everything: oil at $150/bbl destroys petrochemical feedstocks → fertilizer prices spike → wheat follows.
3. **Food export bans have already started:** Kuwait declared a food export ban in the first week of the war; Paulo expects other nations to follow suit. Each export ban triggers the next as governments scramble for self-preservation.
4. The Russia/Ukraine war was *not* particularly disruptive to actual wheat production/exports, yet it was enough to send wheat to the teens. This episode is far worse for fertilizer/feedstock supply.
5. Potential spring-wheat quality issues in the US from severe January cold in the upper Midwest.
6. Wheat is "barely off the mat" — the setup has "significant asymmetry and convexity."

## 1973 analog
- Wheat traded $1–2/bushel for years.
- In summer 1973 (pre-Yom Kippur), wheat exploded from $2.60 to $5.20 (Jul–Aug).
- Corrected back to ~$4.20 on the eve of the war.
- Then ripped another **50% to $6.35** by February 1974.
- The wheat curve was **steeply backwardated** — highly unusual for a short-cycle storage crop, confirmed by Stanley Kroll's 1974 *Professional Commodity Trader* charting diary.
