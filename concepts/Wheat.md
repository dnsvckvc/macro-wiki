# Wheat

Agricultural staple and what [[PauloMacro]] calls **"the geopolitical VIX"** — its price chart looks like a VIX chart because wheat spikes reliably in every major global conflict.

## Why Paulo is long wheat ([[make-1973-great-again]])
1. Rising food and hunger is the *highest-impact, highest-probability* cause of social unrest in human history (Arab Spring's root cause was food prices).
2. Energy touches everything: oil at $150/bbl destroys petrochemical feedstocks → fertilizer prices spike → wheat follows.
3. **Food export bans have already started:** Kuwait declared a food export ban in the first week of the war; Paulo expects other nations to follow suit. Each export ban triggers the next as governments scramble for self-preservation.
4. The Russia/Ukraine war was *not* particularly disruptive to actual wheat production/exports, yet it was enough to send wheat to the teens. This episode is far worse for fertilizer/feedstock supply.
5. Potential spring-wheat quality issues in the US from severe January cold in the upper Midwest.
6. Wheat is "barely off the mat" — the setup has "significant asymmetry and convexity."

## 1973 analog
- Wheat traded $1–2/bushel for years.
- In summer 1973 (pre-Yom Kippur), wheat exploded from $2.60 to $5.20 (Jul–Aug).
- Corrected back to ~$4.20 on the eve of the war.
- Then ripped another **50% to $6.35** by February 1974.
- The wheat curve was **steeply backwardated** — highly unusual for a short-cycle storage crop, confirmed by Stanley Kroll's 1974 *Professional Commodity Trader* charting diary.

## Independent confirmation from [[AdamRozencwajg]] ([[macrovoices-527-adam-rozencwajg]])
Rozencwajg provides the fundamentals/geology-level confirmation of the razor-edge thesis:
- Grain demand has been "off the charts" for 15 years due to rising incomes in developing markets → protein consumption → **7x the grain per calorie** (sun → grain → animal → food chain).
- Yields have been record year after year (better fertilizer, better seeds, longer growing season from warming). Markets approach every year on a "razor's edge" between off-the-charts demand and off-the-charts yields.
- Fertilizer disruption from the [[StraitOfHormuz]] closure could break the yield side of the equation. *"If anything happens to impact the yields, this market will be very tight in a hurry."*
- This is "a classic asymmetry" and "convexity" — exactly the framing Paulo uses.

## The fertilizer-food lag (per [[macrovoices-524-simon-white]])
[[SimonWhite]] provides concrete data for the transmission mechanism:
- **In the 1970s, food's contribution to CPI was *larger* than energy's** during both OPEC 1 and OPEC 2.
- Food inflation was actually rising *before* the energy shock in 1973.
- Today: food contribution to US CPI is **already higher than energy.**
- **Fertilizer proxy leads food CPI by ~6 months.** Inputs: urea, ammonia, sulfur, potash. All flow through or are produced near the Strait (Iran is a major urea/ammonia producer; huge sulfur flows through Hormuz).
- Mechanism: higher fertilizer → farmers reduce applications → lower yields → food prices rise → second-round effects into core inflation → sticky stagflation.
- Implication: the food shock is ~6 months behind the energy shock. If Hormuz stays compromised, 2026 H2 food CPI will be the sequel.
