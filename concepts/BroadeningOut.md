# Broadening Out

The market narrative that "breadth is improving" as more stocks participate in the rally (typically: small caps outperforming large caps after a period of extreme concentration). Often presented as a bullish sign. [[PauloMacro]] in [[intensely-concerned-for-risk-with-a-catch]] demonstrates it is actually a **very late-stage bull phenomenon** that historically precedes one final jam higher by large caps before the ultimate top.

## Historical pattern
1. **2000 DotCom:** Russell 2000 outperformed NQ100 in January 2000 (Broadening Out moment). Then NQ100 had **one last run** to its March 2000 all-time high — which caught Druckenmiller buying hours from the top. In the ensuing bear, small caps outperformed relatively but *still lost money in absolute terms*.
2. **1972 Nifty Fifty:** Smaller names caught up in 4Q72 (Broadening Out). Then there was a **final rotation back into the Nifty Fifty** just before the 1973–74 bear market. Again, small caps outperformed relatively in the bear but lost money absolutely.
3. **2021:** Speculation faded (SPAC/Cathie Wood peaked Jan 27, 2021 per the Crowded L/S factor blowout), but the S&P kept running for another year, topping in January 2022.

## Lesson
- Broadening Out looks bullish but is actually a distribution signal: the large-cap leadership is being sold to speculators while smart money rotates.
- **Don't buy small caps for absolute returns in a bear** — wait for the bear to run its course, then buy once speculation has been wrung out.
- If [[MAG7]] has already had its "final multi-week run" vs. small caps (per the BofA chart showing MAG7 topped vs. energy in December 2025), the Broadening Out analog suggests the ultimate top may already be in for large caps.

## Application (April 2026)
In [[the-north-star-vs-liquidity]], Paulo asks: *"Could it be as simple as consensus gets what it wants, and we are now in the final move higher in megacap US tech while small-midcaps lag on a flight to perceived defensives in a 'cleanest dirty shirt' US rotation back?"* — acknowledging the possibility that the Broadening Out → final-ramp pattern is the right read.
