# Shrub's Razor

A "philosophical trading principle" coined by [[PauloMacro]] in honor of [[LeShrub]] in [[oil-has-turned-a-very-big-corner]] (28 January 2026):

> **"The funniest, most absurd outcome is also the most likely one."**

Builds on Le Shrub's observation that "once you realize it's all nonsense, it starts to make sense," combined with Occam's Razor (simplest explanation = correct one).

## Applications in the wiki
- **Trump crude export ban** ([[oil-has-turned-a-very-big-corner]]): Trump — the most pro-free-market president in history — reinstituting the US crude export ban (which Obama repealed in 2015) would be the ultimate irony. Therefore, per Shrub's Razor, it's the most likely outcome if oil rallies hard. This is why Paulo prefers long Brent (BNO) over WTI (USO).
- **Iran's strategy** ([[the-north-star-vs-liquidity]]): Paulo references Shrub's Razor when describing how Iran's optimal strategy under the [[NorthStar]] / [[NashEquilibrium]] just happens to be the most absurdly escalatory one that nobody expects to persist.
- **The "peace deal makes crude moon" scenario** ([[oil-and-retail]], 21 April 2026): the funniest outcome is a "Big Beautiful Deal" that hands Iran control of Hormuz, which forces [[SaudiArabia]] and [[UAE]] to keep their shut-in ~4.6mmbpd in the ground *indefinitely* rather than accept Iran as OPEC price-setter. Crude then *rips* in an epic news-failure — a peace deal that crashes risk assets because the market can look through a temporary energy disruption but not an indefinite mass shut-in.
