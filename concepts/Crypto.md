# Crypto

In wiki context, **cryptoassets are treated as a Global Liquidity barometer** — the most *liquidity-sensitive* asset class — rather than as a separate investment thesis.

## Howell's BES$ basket and lead time ([[crypto-gold-pboc-vs-fed]])

[[MichaelHowell]] formalizes a tradable Crypto basket:

> **BES$ = 60% BTC + 30% ETH + 10% SOL**

Properties (as of April 2026):
- **Global Liquidity leads BES$ by 13 weeks** in the long-term log-change regression.
- **6-week log-change correlation = 0.57 since 2024.**
- *"Crypto has proved to be an excellent barometer of Global Liquidity conditions and their to-and-fro motion has lately closely matched the swings in Global Liquidity."*

Implication: with **two of the three engines of Global Liquidity stalling** ([[GlobalLiquidityCycle]]), Howell's projection is *"watch out for a tougher crypto market."*

## Crypto in Howell's four-signal watchlist

Crypto and gold are co-listed as the **fourth warning indicator** on Howell's [[GlobalLiquidityCycle]] dashboard:

> *"Gold bullion and crypto prices — do not break lower."*

A meaningful break lower in crypto = direct read on a fading Global Liquidity tailwind — the most liquidity-sensitive asset class is the canary.

## Crypto as canary in [[PauloMacro]]'s framework

The role is consistent across authors. In [[liquidity-drains-redux]] Paulo cites crypto as the canary that breaks first when [[ScottBessent]]'s TGA management drains bank reserves through the [[LiquidityMechanics]] channel. The 4Q25 Bitcoin weakness was the early warning that produced the [[RollingVARShock]].

The cross-author convergence:
- **Paulo:** crypto is the most liquidity-sensitive asset → first to break on US bank-reserve drains.
- **Howell:** crypto is the most liquidity-sensitive asset → leads (or near-leads) Global Liquidity moves.
- Same conclusion via different scales: Paulo from the US micro-plumbing, Howell from the global aggregate.
