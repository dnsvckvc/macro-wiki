# VIX 7th vs 2nd Contract Spread

A VIX futures curve signal [[KevinMuir]] watches as his preferred **capitulation** indicator — the spread between the **7th** VIX futures contract and the **2nd** VIX futures contract. Distinct from (and considered more informative than) the widely-cited front-month VIX inversion or VIX vs. spot.

## Why this particular spread
- The front-month / spot inversion is noisy and gets referenced by everyone.
- The 7 vs. 2 spread captures something further out the curve and is slower / stickier — it's a cleaner signal that the broader term structure has finally given up.
- Muir: the spread *held positive* through the entire four-week run into early March 2026 and only flipped inverted on Thursday 5 March.

## Muir's trading rule (per [[vix-inversion-buying-bonds-japan-jpm-option-whale]])
He watches for the spread to blow out to **−3 or −4** before covering any of his index short. As of early March 2026 it had only just ticked negative, so he was *not* yet covering despite many others calling the low from front-month VIX behavior.
