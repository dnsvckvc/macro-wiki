# Copper

Industrial base metal; a central [[PauloMacro]] theme via the copper junior mining sector, and per [[OleHansen]] potentially the **less-volatile alternative to gold for the [[CommoditySupercycle]] thesis**. Hansen: *"If copper price suddenly doubled, then you would also have some problems with some of the big projects that requires copper. But the direction is pretty clear."*

## Paulo's M&A / takeout wave thesis
Copper is in an active M&A / takeout wave, with supply-side scarcity making proven resources on listed juniors attractive acquisition targets for majors. Paulo's largest copper junior position: [[AldebaranResources]] (ALDE.CN), which on 9 April 2026 reported ~1 km of 0.62% Cu (0.66% CuEq) on infill drilling — reinforcing its attractiveness as a takeout target. ([[economic-data-copper-rollovers-and-more]])

## Hansen's supply-side wedge — the sulfuric-acid dependency ([[macrovoices-529-ole-hansen]])
- **Miners in Chile, Peru, and Congo require sulfuric acid** to break down ore and release copper.
- **~50% of global seaborne sulfuric acid exports come out of the Middle East.** The Iran/Hormuz crisis introduces a real chemistry-supply constraint on top of the existing capex/permitting story.
- *"What I don't think we knew or realized was that the miners in Chile, Peru, and Congo and other places, they need sulfuric acid in order to break down the copper and to release it from the underground."*

## Hansen's demand wedge — China inventory drawdown
- After a massive surge in exchange-monitored stocks across London, New York, and Shanghai earlier in the year, **Shanghai inventories are now drawing hard.**
- China is **paying a premium to import** (slide 12 chart). Premium-over-LME = pent-up demand emerging post-correction.
- *"Maybe a little bit too expensive last year, at least producers or users of copper had to get used to it. And once we had the correction, they came back in."*
- Implication: demand-side responsive to price (so a $/$ vol-controlled trade), but the supply side is stacking new constraints (sulfuric acid + 2025-vintage mine disruptions).

## Technicals (Hansen)
- March 2026 low = exactly the 200-day moving average. Recovery has been strong since.
- Hansen frames copper as a structural long with less downside vol than gold for the same thesis.
