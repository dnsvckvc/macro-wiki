# Strait of Hormuz

The narrow waterway between Iran and Oman through which a significant fraction of global seaborne crude and LNG moves. A single choke point whose closure or insecurity has outsized effects on global energy supply.

## April 2026 status
Per [[KevinMuir]] in [[we-need-to-talk-about-interest-rates-and-oil]]:
- The Strait has been under **Iranian control for 30 days** as of 30 March 2026 — i.e., closed since approximately 1 March 2026, following the 28 Feb US/Israel offensive.
- No near-term prospect of reopening.
- Even if the warring parties came to a miraculous agreement, the physical damage to the energy shipping complex would take **months** to clear. ([[SalMercogliano]] on Muir's "Watch Your Bids" podcast.)

## Physical-security detail (per [[oil-jobs-bond-failure-and-other-fun]])
- At its narrowest (~30km), the Strait has a navigable channel of only **a few kilometers**.
- LNG carriers cannot risk a containment-tank breach: if boiling-off LNG escapes, it could explode massively and destroy vessels in the immediate vicinity. US escorts are "unfeasible in providing absolute security."
- Qatar's energy minister [[SaadAlKaabi]] warned that "even if the war ended today, it would take Qatar weeks to months to get its delivery schedules back to normal" and that the disruption could *"bring down the economies of the world."*
- Compounding factors ([[making-macro-great-again-quick-updates]]): ports and tugs near Hormuz are gradually running out of **bunker fuel** even for tankers merely waiting to transit.

## Scale — per [[jumping-the-shark-when-a-taco-is-not-a-taco]]
The closure takes **20mmbpd / 100mmbpd = 20% of global crude supply** off the market, dwarfing every prior oil crisis:
- Iranian Revolution 1978: 5.5M/60M = 9.2%
- Yom Kippur 1973: 4.5M/56M = 8%
- Iraq-Kuwait 1990: 4.3M/61M = 7%
- Iran-Iraq 1980: 4M/60M = 6.7%
- Russia-Ukraine 2022: 2M/95M = 2.1%

## Market relevance
- The closure is the proximate cause of the [[WTI]] $67.50 → $115 rally (partially tamped to $93 by [[SPRRelease]]) and the global front-end yield explosion Muir documents.
- [[LeShrub]]'s **NACHO** meme — *"Not Actually Changing Hormuz Opening"* — captures the gap between Trump administration rhetoric and the on-the-ground reality at the Strait. See [[NachoTaco]].
- Under [[PauloMacro]]'s [[NashEquilibrium]] framing, Iranian control of Hormuz is structurally locked in: it is the physical instrument of Iran's "Never Again" deterrent and does not depend on Trump's posture.
