# Debt/Liquidity Ratio

[[MichaelHowell]]'s core quantitative metric for systemic financial-crisis risk: the ratio of **total debt stock** to **available Global Liquidity**. Not debt/GDP — because what matters is whether there is enough liquidity to *refinance* the debt, not whether the economy can *service* it from income.

## Why this ratio, not debt/GDP
- ~70–80% of all primary transactions in global markets now refinance existing debts.
- 77% of global lending is collateral-based (World Bank), mostly via repo markets.
- *"New credits rely on the integrity of old debts."* — The paradox at the heart of modern finance.
- The ratio can be understood as aggregate portfolio duration vs. a target: **high ratio → forced liquidation** (duration overshoot); **low ratio → duration extension** (asset booms).

## Historical readings
| Period | Ratio | Regime |
|---|---|---|
| Long-term average (since 1980) | **2.0x (200%)** | Normal |
| 2008 GFC | Rising through 2.0x | Crisis |
| 2010–12 Eurozone | **2.3x (230%)** peak | Severe crisis |
| 2021 post-COVID | **1.58x (158%)** trough | "Everything Bubble" (>2σ below average) |
| 2026 projected | **Breaching 2.0x** | Danger threshold |
| 2030 projected | **Retesting 2.3x** | Severe-crisis territory |

## Why the ratio is rising now
1. **Liquidity momentum is slowing** — the [[GlobalLiquidityCycle]] peaked Q3 2025.
2. **The [[WallOfDebt]] is arriving** — ~$45T in advanced economies ($65T globally) of COVID-era termed-out debt is bunching for refinancing.
3. **The real economy is competing** — *"all money that is anywhere must be somewhere; when it funds real spending it cannot also drive asset prices."*
4. **Central bank divergence** — ECB/BoE/BoJ still shrinking balance sheets even as the Fed eases.

- **Paulo's micro-observations are the symptoms; this ratio is the underlying cause.** The [[BasisTrade]] unwinding (hedge-fund net futures short down >25%), [[SwapSpreads]] going deeply negative, repo rate spikes (4Q25 quant quakes), and private credit cockroaches are all manifestations of a rising debt/liquidity ratio — collateral getting scarcer relative to the debt it supports.
