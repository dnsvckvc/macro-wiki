# Turducken of Market Risks

[[PauloMacro]]'s label for a market in which multiple historically distinct crash-ingredient regimes are *stacked on top of each other*. Introduced on [[KevinMuir]]'s Market Huddle year-end special (December 2025), and fully defined in [[the-anatomy-of-a-crash]] (14 March 2026).

## The four nested regimes
1. **1973 Nifty Fifty concentration, taken further.** 40% of S&P in 7 names ([[MAG7]]). With a new oil crisis on top of it — "how 1973 of us." ([[make-1973-great-again]] walks through the 1973 analog in detail: crude quadrupled and most of the rally was *after* the ceasefire; equities clung to highs for weeks then collapsed −17% by year-end; gold initially sold off then doubled; the curve heavily inverted.)
2. **2000 US Exceptionalism.** Growth + Tech/Dotcom/Retail mania.
3. **2007 opaque credit structures.** Circular shadow vendor financing for datacenters in the hundreds of billions, on top of a decade-long mismarked/unmarked private-credit bubble. (Paulo: *"it can't be a true Turducken without hidden financial leverage."*)
4. **1987 portfolio insurance**, re-expressed as **0DTE intraday leveraged instability** and dealer short-gamma setups like the [[JPMCollarTrade]].

> "Just one of these initial conditions would be sufficient for a pretty memorable bear market when equity valuations are trading at the highest levels in US equity market history. To have a superfecta like this is — historic?"

## How it expresses in March 2026 (per [[jumping-the-shark-again]])
A concrete instantiation of the 1987 layer:
- Short-gamma dealers against the [[JPMCollarTrade]] 6475 long-put strike into 31 March expiration, with little time and rising vol.
- Delta-hedging cascade → VIX call chasing ("wingy vanna protection") → 0DTE retail put-buying.
- Decaying [[TrumpPumps]] unable to offset.
