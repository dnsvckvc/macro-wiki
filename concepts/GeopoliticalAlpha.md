# Geopolitical Alpha

Title of [[MarkoPapic]]'s 2020 book and the formal name for his **material-constraints-on-policymakers** framework. Implemented at [[BCAResearch]] under the label *"geo-macro"* since 2011.

## The framework (per [[my-buddy-marko-broke-the-market]])
Quantitative and empirical work shows that geopolitical events **rarely have a lasting impact on markets.** Two underlying reasons:
1. **The market always overstates risk.** Investors start trading *what's possible* instead of *what's probable*. No one is there to talk about material constraints through the potential apocalypse.
2. **Reflexive policy pivot.** The event itself gives policymakers an excuse to pivot — cut rates, ease fiscal, walk back — which often turns the episode into a catalyst for stimulus.

## The "it's a book, not a pamphlet" distinction
Papic: *"If you read my book, it's not a pamphlet that says '[expletive] geopolitics, quantitative data shows it, mic drop, goodbye.' It's a book."*

Most of the time investors *over-* or *under-*state the material reality constraining policymakers. The framework's answer is not "fade everything" — it's **do the analysis and identify which constraints are real.**

- When material constraints allow a pivot: fade.
- When material constraints lock in a trajectory (e.g. Iran's regime survival being existentially threatened): don't fade.

Papic: *"There is nothing automatic or supernatural about fading geopolitical risk. It's based on a framework that focuses on material constraints, but you still got to understand those constraints and do the work."*

## The "geo-macro" extension
Papic: *"If you're not taking geopolitics to be endogenous to your process, I don't think you're a macro investor."* Treats the macro-geopolitics distinction as obsolete in a post-2022 world.

## Relationship to [[FadeGeopoliticalAngst]]
[[FadeGeopoliticalAngst]] is the *rule of thumb* a reader might extract from the book. **Geopolitical Alpha** is the underlying analytical framework. The rule of thumb works most of the time because most events don't change material constraints; the framework exists to identify the exceptions.

## April 2026 vindication on equities
Through 20 April 2026 — seven weeks into the Iran/US/Israel war — the framework's core prediction has been vindicated on risk assets despite the acute physical crisis:
- S&P 500 closed pre-war at 6879; closed 17 April at **7126 (above pre-war)**.
- Nasdaq ran 13 consecutive up days — longest since 15 July 2013.
- Both mechanisms Papic names were observable: (1) **the market overstated risk early** (the first two weeks of [[Degrossing]] in gold, L/S books); (2) **the reflexive policy pivot** (TGA drawdown, 7 April ceasefire, [[OneBigBeautifulBill]] fiscal) gave risk-asset participants permission to re-engage.
- [[KevinMuir]] himself concedes this in bull-pillar #1 of [[outlining-the-bull-and-bear-arguments]]: *"the buy the geopolitical angst trade worked in spades… just a little late in kicking in."*

This is the load-bearing validation of the framework. Papic's subsidiary tactical calls on [[StraitOfHormuz]] duration (*"mere days"*) and take-profits timing missed, but those are downstream of the meta-call and don't undermine it.

## The complacency risk — the Norman Angell parallel
Per [[KevinMuir]] in [[my-buddy-marko-broke-the-market]]: a framework that is correct most of the time becomes **dangerous** once the market adopts it reflexively, because the one event that does change material constraints is now *unhedged*. Papic accepts the parallel to [[NormanAngell]]'s *[[TheGreatIllusion]]* (1909) — a correct economic argument against war that contributed to pre-WWI complacency.

Papic's January 6, 2026 long-Brent pivot is the on-record case where Papic himself applied the framework's *exception* clause in real time:
1. US used the "R-word" (regime change) — Iran's regime now existentially threatened.
2. Gulf Arabs have plausible deniability.
3. Internal Iranian discord in December 2025.
4. Technological asymmetry: Iran can't defend its skies but can close shipping with drones (Houthi Red Sea precedent).

Each is a *material constraint* that has flipped from permissive to binding.

## The Muir companion piece — the transformative-exception test case
[[KevinMuir]]'s [[geopolitics-doesnt-change-prices-in-the-long-run]] (12 March 2026) is the wiki's most explicit *applied* articulation of the exception clause. Muir's 2022–2025 gold regression break (gold vs. TIPS + DXY) is the empirical footprint of the [[LedgerToRealWorldThesis]] transformation working through the market over multiple years. The piece doubles as the **pre-registered hypothesis** for what an Iran-war transformation would look like for equities — a slow-motion regression break relative to earnings / ERP / P/FCF fundamentals, not a Feb-28-style crash event. The clock runs in years, not days.
