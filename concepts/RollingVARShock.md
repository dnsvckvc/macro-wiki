# Rolling VAR Shock

[[PauloMacro]]'s label for a regime in which different hedge-fund strategies blow up at different times in sequence, each forcing localized de-risking that spreads the shock to the next strategy. The "rolling" dimension is temporal — the blowups cluster and accelerate toward a convergence point where *everything* goes at once.

## March 2026 sequence (per [[the-anatomy-of-a-crash]])
1. **Gold** — the initial trigger in late January 2026. (See [[Gold]] and [[buying-gold-again]] for [[KevinMuir]]'s positioning-level explanation: crowded specs + Gulf sovereigns facing cash calls forced a ~27% correction despite the supportive geopolitical backdrop.)
2. **Quant** blowups.
3. **L/S** equity strategies.
4. **Rate PM** blowups ~10 days before the 14 March essay.
5. **"Everyone got hit this week"** — multiple strategies converging in the week of 10 March.
6. **Next in line:** [[DispersionTrade]] (most critical) and [[BasisTrade]] (quietly unwinding).

## Why it matters
- The time *between* blowups is accelerating — a tell that the system is approaching forced [[Degrossing]].
- Each strategy's unwind removes a source of volatility suppression; dispersion is the last major seller of index vol.
- Confirmed to Paulo at drinks by a friend allocator on Thursday 13 March 2026 before he wrote the essay.
