# Reserve Management Purchases (RMP)

The Federal Reserve's **discretionary asset-purchase program** that replaced the formal QT (Quantitative Tightening) regime in late 2025. Operationally similar to QE but framed as *technical reserve management* rather than monetary policy easing — preserving the Fed's flexibility and political optics.

## Origin and timeline

- **Late 2025:** Fed QT (Quantitative Tightening) + [[ScottBessent]]'s TGA rebuild drained bank reserves by ~$600bn. Quant quakes in July and September. Short-term rates breached the Fed Funds corridor in 4Q25 (echoing September 2019 repo problems).
- **December 2025:** Fed abandoned QT and initiated the RMP — purchases of Treasury securities to replenish bank reserves.
- **1 January 2026:** **eSLR (enhanced Supplementary Leverage Ratio) regulatory change** phased in — reduced bank required-reserve levels by ~$251bn (per Howell).
- **Late October 2025 to early April 2026:** Bank reserves swung from a **−$400bn shortfall** to a **+$200bn surplus** — a US$606bn liquidity injection ([[crypto-gold-pboc-vs-fed]]):
  - $251bn — eSLR-driven reduction in *required* reserves
  - $192bn — RMP cash directly
  - $163bn — various other sources (Treasury operations, RRP residuals)
- **Late April 2026:** excess reserves trimmed back to around par. The RMP was *front-loaded ahead of April 15 Tax Day* — confirmed by Howell as Fed-acknowledged.

## Howell's elevation: RMP is QE in everything but name

Howell ([[crypto-gold-pboc-vs-fed]]): *"The sharp 'V'-shaped rise in the chart [of SOMA] should dispel any doubts that the Fed has revived (albeit temporarily) its QE-program."*

This makes the RMP load-bearing for the wiki's liquidity narrative — it's not technical noise. It's the active counter-cycle policy that propped up risk assets through April.

## The "front-loaded" confirmation — independent triangulation

The single most important wiki claim about RMP came from [[PauloMacro]] in [[the-north-star-vs-liquidity]] (12 April 2026): the support is calendar-driven and ends after April 15 tax receipts.

Howell ([[crypto-gold-pboc-vs-fed]]) **confirms** Paulo's hypothesis from the Fed's own communication: *"the Fed has previously warned that the RMP would be 'front-loaded' ahead of April 15th 'Tax Day'."*

This is the wiki's clearest case of **Paulo and Howell triangulating from opposite sides** of the Treasury/Fed coordination — Paulo via Bessent's TGA management, Howell via the Fed's RMP guidance — to the same answer: the support window closed at mid-April.

## Continuity uncertainty under Warsh

Howell flags: *"the Fed has previously warned that the RMP would be 'front-loaded' ahead of April 15th 'Tax Day'… the answer to the first question is uncertain ahead of Chair [[KevinWarsh]] commencing his tenure."*

If Warsh slows or stops the RMP — consistent with [[StephenMiran]]'s thesis (in [[could-fed-liquidity-plunge-by-half]]) of cutting Fed assets by $1.7T — bank reserves slip back into deficit, the SOFR-FedFunds spread positives again, and the [[BasisTrade]]/[[MOVEIndex]] coordination breaks. This is one of the explicit channels through which the [[GlobalLiquidityCycle]] turns from level-supportive to definitively negative.
