# STIR Specialist Hedge Funds

**STIR = Short-Term Interest Rates.** A category of hedge fund that specializes in trading the front end of the rates curve — Fed Funds futures, SOFR futures, BoC CORRA futures, ECB ESTR-equivalent, etc. — typically with substantial leverage and very short-duration positioning.

## Role in the wiki

[[KevinMuir]] in [[why-did-the-front-end-get-hammered]] identifies **March 2026 STIR-specialist losses** as a key positioning explanation for *why* the front-end move during the first leg of the war was so violent:

> *"Although I would have gotten this forecast wrong, I wouldn't have been alone. In fact, part of the reason the front-end performed so poorly was that too many hedge funds were aggressively speculating on lower front-end rates. You can see this in the multitude of STIR specialist hedge funds that had large losses in March. Positioning unwind made the response to the war all the worse."*

The argument: the fundamental crude→SOFR mechanism would have moved Dec 2026 SOFR from "just under 3%" to ~3.5% on its own. The full move to **3.875%** required a layer of *forced* selling from specialists positioned for cuts.

## Connection to [[Degrossing]]

This is the **front-end equivalent** of the degrossing dynamics Paulo and Muir document elsewhere:
- Equities: hedge-fund L/S degrossing cut both longs and shorts, mechanically lowering exposure ([[Degrossing]]).
- Gold: weak longs + Gulf cash calls + Turkey wartime selling drove the $1,400 collapse.
- **Front-end rates: STIR specialists who had been speculating on lower SOFR were forced out, exaggerating the move higher in implied policy rates.**

The same forced-positioning-unwind dynamic, applied to a different asset class.

## Why it matters for the next leg

The STIR-positioning argument supports [[KevinMuir]]'s thesis that **the next crude spike will not produce a proportional front-end response**:
1. The first-leg move was partly fundamental ([[OilPriceRecessionDebate]] / Hamilton transmission), partly STIR positioning.
2. The STIR positioning has now unwound (specialists took losses, books are flat or short).
3. So the second leg has no positioning kicker.
4. Plus the [[RemovalOfEasingVsHikes]] asymmetry: there's no easing left to remove, so the next 75bps would be actual hikes — much harder.
5. Combined → the crude→SOFR regression should weaken; yellow dots head below the line.
